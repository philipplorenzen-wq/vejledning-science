If you need to take an exam in a cancelled course that you have previously followed, you will get a last chance to take the exam.


Timing of exam and registration

Last chance for taking the exam in a cancelled course is placed the year after the course was last offered. When the exam is placed depends on, in which block it was supposed to be offered.

You can see the exact exam dates no later than 6 weeks before the exam.

Find time and place for your exam

Same exam form as re-exams

An exam in a cancelled course will take the same form as a re-exam in the course.

Find the re-exam form in your course description 


If you cannot meet the examination prerequisites before the re-exam

If you do not meet the examination prerequisites applying to your course, and if you are unable to meet them before the re-exam week, you are not allowed to take the exam.


If it's been a while since you fulfilled the examination prerequisites for your course, you should contact the course coordinator to find out if the period of validity can be extended.

See the rules on examination prerequisites and re-exams in your course description
