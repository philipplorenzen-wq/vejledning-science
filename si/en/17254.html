You will do the compulsory thesis at the end of your programme during the second year. Information about the second year, including the thesis, will be shared by faculty members from Milan and Warsaw during information meetings (typically in January).

Note that you can also do Projects in Practice (akin to an internship) and Projects Outside the Course Scope (essentially working more in-depth with a specific topic). A Project in Practice emphasises combining work experience with academic learning. You will need a supervisor at the University of Copenhagen with whom you must sign a standard contract. Some do it in collaboration with private companies, some with NGOs. There are many possibilities and the choice is yours. You can this in Denmark or elsewhere. Do a Project in Practice if it makes sense in your study progression. Building more practical experience is a good thing. But of course, you may prefer academic specialisation in coursework, which is also fine.

Project in practice

A project in practice is a kind of internship, and the emphasis is more on the work experience and less on the “academic” learning. You will need a supervisor at the University of Copenhagen with whom you must sign a standard contract. Some do it in connection to their thesis, e.g. work some months as an intern with an NGO and then carry on to do thesis work.Do a Project in practice if it makes sense in your study progression. Building more practical experience is a good thing. But of course, you may prefer academic specialisation in coursework, which is also fine.

Thesis

Your thesis is done at your second-year institution, and you must have a supervisor from your second-year institution. Most thesis projects are done in collaboration with external partners, such as local universities, private companies, NGOs, governmental offices, or development organisations. But it is not a requirement. Most theses would also contain an element of empirical data collection, e.g. primary data gathered in villages or forests, all depending on your topic. You may want to start considering your thesis topic during your first year. But try to keep an open mind – you will be getting a lot of new knowledge during the first year, which may lead you to a topic you haven’t thought about before.

There is no standard recipe for how you develop your thesis project. Suggestions are:

  • Start becoming familiar with the projects and potential supervisors at your second-year institution. Check their websites. Talk to visiting faculty members from those universities.
  • Find an organisation that would like to host you for your thesis.

It is always an iterative process, and it will take time. So start thinking early. Test options. Change your mind. Be inspired. Everyone will be okay if you do not make promises you cannot keep. You are an asset, and supervisors will gladly help you develop your ideas and options. Just be clear about where you are in the process, and do not hesitate to ask. Nobody has ever left the programme because the thesis did not work out, although it appears to be the most daunting part.
