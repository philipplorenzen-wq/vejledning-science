To lead a good and balanced student life, it is essential that you learn to: 

  • prioritise your time and energy 
  • accept that you cannot perform well in all areas at all times. 

As a student, you will invariably feel that there are so many things you have to do or feel you should do – and too little time to do everything. 

Today, many students feel stressed and do not thrive. This is evident, for example, from the study survey conducted at UCPH 

The Centre for Youth Research also has a strong focus on what they call a new susceptibility among young people, and the pressure that many experience. 

Read about the centre’s research (Danish website)

You decide what is most important in your student life 

What is both good and difficult about being a student is that you are solely responsible for your studies, and it is up to you to decide what to prioritise at different times during your studies. 

Even though it is important, you cannot always prioritise: 

  • being active and well-prepared for your classes
  • socialising with your fellow students 
  • being actively engaged in your study environment 
  • having a relevant student job 
  • taking care of your mental and physical health 
  • investigating your career opportunities during your studies. 

You may cope fine with being extremely busy for certain periods, but there will be times when this is not possible. And it is not healthy for you to be too busy all the time.
