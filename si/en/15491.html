You are already familiar with being a university bachelor student, but mark that the duration of the Master's programme is shorter – only two years, with the Master's thesis taking up one-half. It is thus necessary that you are active and ready from the start. It is also recommended that you begin to think about what you would like to do after your studies and gain the relevant competencies for this. Relevant student jobs and projects in practice (PiP) may be a way to get a relevant job after your studies. I strongly advise you already to apply for your first job even before you have finished your Thesis. Research has shown that the earlier you start the shorter time it takes to secure your first job.

  • Consider your studies as your work and allocate the necessary time (approx. 45 hours per week)
  • Structure your time – both weekdays and weekends
  • Make study groups with students that share the same interests as you
  • Participate in project proposal events, SPOT events, career days, and extracurricular meetings. They are announced in study messages, on message boards at the department etc.
  • Be observant considering plagiarism and cheating - ask your teachers and supervisors for guidelines
  • Use your influence to improve the study programme and the study environment e.g. through course evaluations, student union etc. 
  • Don't forget the social life at Biology (consult former BSc biology students from UCPH if you come from outside UCPH)
  • Note that Biology students have a studying/meeting/socialising room in the AKB building central on campus
  • During your last year, start putting your CV together and the last six months try to apply for your first jobs. For your CV, also remember all your general competencies that you have gained over the five years e.g. good English writing and spoken skills, super-user of this and that software, team player, used to manage deadlines etc.
