How is the job interview conducted?

Job interviews can be very different, depending on the workplace, the type of vacancy they are looking to fill, etc.

For example, the following elements may vary:

  • Who and how many participate in the interview: Sometimes only the manager is present, other times one or more colleagues, a union representative, an HR employee also participate.
  • How the interview is structured: The interview may consist solely of a conversation, or you may be asked to solve a task/case. Maybe you need to do a personality test.

You will typically be told how the job interview will be conducted before the actual interview. Ask if you are in doubt. 

The typical job interview

  1. Small talk

    The interview starts the moment you step through the door, which is your chance to make a good initial impression.

  2. Introduction to the company and the job

    The employer will typically start the interview by giving a presentation of the company and the job. 

  3. Presentation of yourself

    You will almost always be asked to tell the employer a little bit about yourself and why you have applied for the job. 

  4. Questions and dialogue

    You will be asked to elaborate on some of the things you have mentioned in your application, CV or presentation. Example: “You have said that you are analytical – can you give us an example of this, or how you think that you can use this quality with us?"

  5. Your questions for them

    You will have an opportunity to ask questions, so it is a good idea to prepare a couple of questions in advance. 

  6. Salary

    Sometimes the question of your salary will be brought up at the actual interview, sometimes not. Sometimes you may be asked about your salary expectations, whereas others will merely inform you about what you can expect.
    Contact your trade union if you need salary statistics, or if you would like to talk about salary expectations before the interview. 

  7. Rounding-off

    This is a chance to talk about any questions that have not yet been addressed, as well as the next steps in the process. If you have any questions for the employer, this is where you should make sure to ask them. You will also be informed whether there will be more interviews and when you will hear from them.

    It is a good idea to thank the employer for the interview and to express your interest in the job – that is if you are still interested.
