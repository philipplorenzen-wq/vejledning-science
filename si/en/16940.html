Guide on how to apply for dispensation

Your application will always be processed based on your specific situation, your reasons and any enclosed study plan and documentation.

Your application for a dispensation must or may contain four elements.


1) Written explanation

You must describe your exceptional situation and your need for a dispensation.

You must include the following in your written explanation:

  • State the rule from which you are applying for a dispensation (if you use the form 'Other'). You can, for example, refer to the curriculum for your study programme or the site here on Study Information describing the rule.
  • State what you are applying for a dispensation from briefly and concisely, i.e. what you would like to have permission to do.
  • State reasons for applying for an dispensation . You should describe as clearly as possible the reason(s) why you need to apply for a dispensation.

Make your description as detailed as possible and be aware that it supports the documentation that you upload when you apply for dispensation.


2) Study plan

It is usually relevant to fill in and attach a study plan with your application for an dispensation. The study plan must set out your plan for the rest of your study programme with the changes for which you are applying. Specify when and how you are going to complete your study programme.
Use the study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure.
If the dispensation will not affect your course of study and does not affect your ability to meet the study activity requirements, you do not need to complete a study plan. This applies, for example, if you want to apply for a different exam form.


3) Documentation

You must – to the best of your abilities – document the reason why you are applying for a dispensation. Relevant documentation may, for example, be maternity records/birth certificate/adoption documents, a medical certificate, an opinion from a psychologist or psychiatrist, a statement from your supervisor/head of studies, etc.

Enclosed medical certificates or opinions from other therapists must include an assessment of

  • how much/little you are able to study
  • how long the exceptional circumstances on which your application is based are expected to last (as precisely as possible). 

If necessary, you can forward such documentation later. However, the processing of your application may be delayed if you do not submit all the documentation together at the same time. Write in the comments field if the documentation will be submitted later.

See a guide to what your documentation should include – and maybe bring it to your doctor.

If you cannot document the reason why you are applying for a dispensation, you must explain why – either in your enclosed written explanation or in the comments field on the form.


4) Comments

All forms contain a comments field. You can use it to add important information which is not provided elsewhere – or to highlight a specific point to which special attention should be paid.

For example, you can use the comments field if

  • you have applied for other dispensations based on the same circumstances
  • you want to change your course/project registrations if your application for a dispensation is approved.
