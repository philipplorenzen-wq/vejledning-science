To get through your studies successfully, you must take responsibility.

  • You have enrolled in the Geology-geoscience programme which is one of the smaller programmes in UCPH. This gives you a good opportunity to get to know your peers and teachers well. You also have a good chance to talk to teachers and researchers, as we have an open door politic at the department.
  • Your programme is your job, and you should expect to spend up to 40 hours on it weekly. You must structure your everyday life so you are well-read and well-prepared.
  • It is incredibly important that you participate in a study group or form one yourself, so you have fellow students to discuss and meet up with. If there is something you don't understand, ask your teachers and fellow students for help. Make sure that you give a helping hand, when fellow students ask you. Find tips for a good study group.
  • Participate in the activities of STENO or DGF to build your network.
  • Participate actively in teaching, field work, excursions to build your network.
  • Find out what gives you energy and how you relax best so you have the necessary strength for your sometimes busy life as a student. Find more info on student well-being.
  • Participate in GeoCouncil and other types student politics.
  • Participate in SPOT events and other events.
  • Read your study messages and study events in order not to miss important messages and events.
  • If something seems unmanageable talk to your peers, UCPH Study Guidance or your Head of Studies. There is always help to get. 

I encourage you to evaluate your courses when you finish them. Your input is important to improving the programme.
