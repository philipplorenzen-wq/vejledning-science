What you should do will depend on the time of illness. 

If a member of your group has fallen ill before submission If a group member falls ill during the project, 

  • the rest of the group must continue the project and consult your supervisor about how to complete the project with one group member missing. 
  • the member who has fallen ill applies for a dispensation to be withdrawn from the exam due to illness. It is up to the person who has fallen ill whether he or she will continue the project with a revised project contract or start a brand-new project and enter into a new contract. 


How to apply for a dispensation: 

  1. Obtain documentation for your situation, such as a medical certificate, a note from your psychologist, or a statement from your supervisor, head of studies, contact in your company, etc. 
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Write a statement describing the extraordinary circumstances that prevent you from submitting the report/taking the exam. 
  3. Prepare a study plan for your entire course of study showing how you intend to complete your degree programme. Use the study planning tool under Programme structure. 
  4. Complete an application via ‘Apply for a dispensation ’ and attach your documentation, reasons and study plan.

Apply for a dispensation 

As a general rule, you will receive a reply within 6 weeks of the Study Bboard having received all relevant information about your application. How the study board will process your application.


A group member has fallen ill after submission

The other members of the group must still take the exam in the joint project if a group member falls ill before or during the oral exam. 

The group member who is ill must

  • give notice of absence from the exam due to illness
    Read more on this page under “I am ill for the oral exam”
  • agreeing with your supervisor on when you will restart take the project
  • register for the same project 
  • (re)submitting your report – possibly in a revised version that remains within your problem formulation 
  • taking the oral exam.
