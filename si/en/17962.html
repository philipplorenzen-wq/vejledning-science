If you like having a detailed plan, you can enter what preparation you will do and when in your weekly schedule (see Plan your week). 

3 important factors to consider when planning: 

  1. The specific preparation: Which articles, books or proofs do you need to read? Are some things more important than others? 
  2. Length and difficulty: What form are the texts in? How long are they? How difficult are they? Are they primary texts or supplementary texts? Are they in a foreign language? 
  3. The type of work the text requires from you: Does it need your full concentration? Do you need to take thorough notes? Do you need to memorise something? Or just skim the texts? 


Once you have taken a closer look at the preparation required, you can decide: 

  • how much time it will realistically take 
  • at what time of day and where you can do the specific preparation. Sometimes of day and places of study might be better suited to specific types of preparation. 

Then enter your preparation in the schedule.
