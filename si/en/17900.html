When you have completed your studies, there are many career possibilities. Many look for jobs in the private and public job market, typically in R&D or production in biotech companies or the medical industry. Others go into private consultancies, governmental research institutions, and universities or become product specialists in lab equipment. Some wish to continue doing basic research and they look for Ph.D. programmes. Use your network actively and join career-oriented social platforms. Keep an eye on events where employers inform about career paths and requested competencies. 

I suggest that you are open to a wide field of possibilities and trust your competencies. I also recommend that you do not limit yourself geographically and consider that you also can pursue a job or follow a Ph.D. programme abroad.
