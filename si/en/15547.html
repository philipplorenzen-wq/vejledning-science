It can be difficult to concentrate on your studies in case of death or illness in your family. Here, you can see what you need to think about and when.

In the other sections on this page, you can read more about the most important procedures in the step-by-step guide.


1) As soon as you can: Withdraw from any exams and courses taking place in the near future

Find out if you are registered for any study activities (exams, courses or projects) in the near future which you are unable to do.

Withdraw from courses and exams that you will not be able to do.

How to withdraw from exams
How to withdraw from courses


2) While you are away: Check your KUmail

Check your KUmail once a week while you are away. This is where you will be notified if there is anything important you need to deal with.

Otherwise, try to take it easy with regard to your studies. You will not be disenrolled from your study programme from one day to the next.


3) When you are able to do so: Get relevant documentation

When you are away for a period of time, you may have to be able to document the reason for your absence when applying for a dispensation, leave of absence etc.

Read more under 'How do I document illness or death in the family?' 


4) When you have a little more time and energy: Prepare a new study plan

Once you have a bit more time and energy, you can try to prepare an alternative study plan that takes your situation into account.

See how to organise a new study plan with the possibility to study at reduced hours in a special situation


5) If necessary: Apply for a dispensation or leave of absence

Your new study plan can give you an overview of whether you will be able to meet the study activity requirements.

If you are not able to comply with the requirements and rules applying to your study programme, you can either

  • apply for a dispensation from the requirements and rules which you are unable to comply with (e.g. activity requirements, additional exam attempts etc.)
  • apply for leave of absence due to extraordinary circumstances for a period (without SU).
