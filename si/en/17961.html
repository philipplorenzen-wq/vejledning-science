When you open a book and start reading or doing an assignment, a lot of thoughts will probably pop into your head that aren’t relevant to what you are doing, such as: ‘I must remember to buy salt’, ‘I must remember to swap my shift at work’ etc. 

This kind of mental noise is unavoidable, but you can learn to put such disruptive thoughts aside on a to-do list, so you can stay focused on what you are doing. 

How to set disturbances aside:

  1. Find a pencil and paper and have both ready beside your computer or book. 
  2. While working, note down any thoughts that pop into your head that you need to remember, but which are not relevant to what you are doing.
  3. Only turn your attention to the items on your list once you have finished working. For example, you can enter the tasks on the list into your calendar, or wherever they belong.

You can also use to-do lists to plan your preparation days.
