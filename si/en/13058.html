What is a phishing e-mail?

Placering: IT and facilities on campus › IT and support › IT-security

Sprog: English · Elementtype: Accordion (narrow format)

Gælder for målgrupperne: Københavns Universitet - Alle uddannelsesniveauer

Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/phishing.aspx

Phishing is fake emails that are sent to you in the hope that you will click on a link and give away your login or account information. If you provide your information, the sender can access your, your fellow students' and UCPH's data and steal or exploit it.

The sender will often pretend to be a person or organization you know. You could even get a fake email from someone claiming to be from, for example, UCPH.

  • Do NOT reply and DO NOT click on links in emails you are not absolutely sure are secure
  • You should never enter passwords, credit or account numbers
  • Always rate emails based on the whole and use the checklist below

Checklist: Is it a phihsing email?

You should always judge an email based on the whole. Here is a checklist to review when you receive an email.

If you can answer no to just one of the following questions, it's most likely a phishing email you received - and you should delete it immediately:

  • Do you know the sender?
  •  Is it likely that the sender would send you such an email?
  • Does the email look like it usually does?
  • Does the sender address look real?
  • Is the email written correctly without grammatical or linguistic errors?
  • Is the information you are asked for someone you should normally provide?

If you receive a phishing email

If the email you received is phishing, avoid clicking on any links in the email and just delete the email.

If you accidentally end up clicking on a phishing e-mail and/or submit your information, it is important that you contact UCPH IT and tell them as soon as possible.
