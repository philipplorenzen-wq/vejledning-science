Can I get support or special conditions while gaining job experience?

Your options for support depend on whether your job experience is integrated in your degree programme or takes place outside of the programme:

If your job experience is built into your degree programme, for example in the form of collaboration with companies, internships or clinical training, you will often be able to obtain Special Educational Support (SPS). This could be assistive devices, a mentor or a support person.

Contact the SPS Counselling to hear about your options

If your job experience takes place outside of your programme, for example student jobs or voluntary work, you must contact your municipality about support opportunities, typically via the job centre.

For example, see the municipality of Copenhagen

 If you require special conditions such as reduced hours or more frequent breaks, this is often something that you agree on directly with the employer or with the company. If it is in connection with a course or an exam, you can contact UCPH Study Guidance for advice and guidance.
