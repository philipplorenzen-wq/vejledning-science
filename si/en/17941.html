As a student, it is a good idea to maintain a responsible research practice. This also applies to the textbooks that you access via various courses during your studies. It is important to emphasise that, as a student, you are not allowed to share your digital textbooks.

This applies to:

  • Sharing e-books or scanned books with people other than members of your household
  • Procuring, sharing or selling e-books via Facebook, Messenger, DBA/eBay, Dropbox, etc., unless the publisher/author has allowed the e-book to be shared
  • Copying an e-book borrowed from the library

However, you are allowed to:

  • Make 1-2 printed photocopies of a physical textbook for yourself/family/friends
  • Print an e‑book that you have purchased for your own use
  • Upload definitions and your own notes to chatbots

Sharing digital textbooks between students is illegal and can have major academic consequences for both you and your fellow students. Illegal sharing of textbooks may result in fines and, in the worst case, could give you a criminal record.

So please remember to buy your own textbooks and maintain a responsible research practice – then you will be safe.
