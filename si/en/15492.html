There are several ways successfully, to become an FNM graduate. First, and foremost, it is important that you stay motivated, and secondly, that you actually take responsibility for your own education and learning. You will have to make choices during the programme, and you are the only one who knows what is right for you. If you are in doubt, you are always welcome to contact me, or UCPH Study Guidance, for advice and feedback. It is also an option to have a zoom meeting at the beginning of your studies, e.g. during your first block/semester. 

Making it successfully through the FNM programme requires motivation, independence, self-discipline, study skills, the ability to communicate and cooperate... and most of all: hard work. In return, you will learn and grow - as a person and a professional. Make sure, throughout your studies, to stimulate, nurture and develop your curiosity, as the main driver for your work and effort. The success of this strategy will most clearly show while working through your thesis :)

In order to progress as planned through the programme structure without unnecessary stress or delays, my advice to you is that you:

  • Consider studying as a full time job. Studying full time means that you on average are expected to invest at least 45 hours per week in learning and improving your skills and competencies.
  • Plan your study/work time, and stick to it. Get more advice here on study techniques.
  • Keep yourself informed on the requirements for you and how to plan your studies.
  • Attend the available SPOT events that can help you to better manage your time and adopt more efficient study techniques and practices.
  • Engage in social activities, with your fellow FNM students as well as others.
  • Stay motivated and stimulate your curiosity, e.g. by engaging in relevant (scientific) activities, such as department seminars and other events.
  • Attend exams, and avoid cheating or plagiarizing (knowingly or unknowingly).
  • Use your influence to improve the programme and your study environment, i.e. participate actively in course evaluations and annual study environment assessments.
  • Use your alumni e-mail.
  • Read your study messages and posted study events not to miss important messages and events.
  • Join the Forest and Nature Management group on Facebook to connect with other FNM students as well as graduates.
