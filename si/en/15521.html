In connection with an exam, you can, e.g., complain about:

  1. Legal issues (whether the rules for the program, the rules in the Public Administration Act or administrative law principles have been followed)
  2. Academic issues here under the assessment and the examination basis (questions, assignments and the like)
  3. The examination process (completion and circumstances of the examination)

Questions can be asked in different degrees of difficulty within the course or exam goal description

You cannot complain about the questions asked at your exam as long as the questions are within the course description. Examiner and co-examiner have the opportunity to ask questions of varying difficulty and in related topics to assess your level.

The exam is assessed based on an overall evaluation

According to the Grading Scale Order, the assessment of your examination must be given based on an overall evaluation of the degree to which the examination's performance meets the objectives of the subject. When you write your exam complaint, it is therefore important that you consider your whole exam and not only specific questions. Consequently, it is impossible to compare your performance with other students' responses and assessments 1: 1.
