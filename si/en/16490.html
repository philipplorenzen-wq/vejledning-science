You can do a thesis preparation project when it fits into your programme. Talk to your supervisor about what makes most sense in relation to your project and the upcoming thesis.  

Depending on your study programme, you can do the project as either:

  • an elective project
  • a restricted elective project.

See what your options are under Planning your studies.

If you want to do the project as a restricted elective project, and it is not on the list of your restricted elective courses, you must apply for and receive a dispensation for it to be included as a restricted elective.

Apply for a dispensation to use restricted elective ECTS credits

You can apply for a dispensation from your study board to do your thesis preparation project with restricted elective ECTS credits.

How to apply for a dispensation for restricted elective

Prepare a document with a draft project contract. The draft must include:

  • the type of project it concerns
  • how many ECTS credits it gives
  • the title of the project
  • a project description.

Complete the form ‘Approval of a SCIENCE course as restricted elective’. Remember to attach the draft project contract.

Complete the dispensation form


Send your dispensation application well in advance

Usually, you will receive a response within 6 weeks from the date the study board has received all relevant information about your case.

You must have received a response to your dispensation application before you can start completing your project contract.

How the study board will process your application

When you can do your project

A project totalling 7.5 ECTS creditscan only be done over one block – in blocks 1, 2, 3, 4 or 5.

You must start your project at the start of the block and finish it in the exam week in the last block of the project.
