It can be quite demotivating if you never get the feeling of achieving what you set out to do because of an overly optimistic to-do list. You can counteract this by prioritising and allowing yourself to postpone certain tasks as part of your planning. 

What to do:

  1. Write down everything you hope to achieve on the given day. This could be anything from buying books to preparing a presentation for class. 
  2. For each item, consider: What do you have to find time for, and what you would you like to find time for. Sort the items into four categories:
    a. Has to be done today.
    b. Can wait until later in the week.
    c. Can wait until next week/month.
    d. Can be done when you have time to spare. 

If you fail to do a category b, c or d activity, you can simply put it on your to-do list for another day. 

As time goes by, you will no doubt get better at making realistic to-do lists for each day.

You can also use your to-do list to put distractions out of your mind while studying.
