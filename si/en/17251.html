Sustainability implies that we conduct ourselves in a way where environmental, economic and social needs today does not restrict the possibilities to meet the societal needs of the future. 

To obtain a sustainable future is the driving force behind all the specializations in Environmental Science. No matter if your study focus is on risk assessment and regulation, circular systems, soil - water - atmosphere chemistry, microbiology, ecotoxicology, analytical chemistry, impact assessment, pollution research and remediation, human health and toxicology, data science and modelling, water and soil resources, land use or environmental solutions, you will acquire the knowledge, skills and competencies to promote sustainability in your future work-life (see our recommended study tracks for suggestions on how you can become a sustainability promotor within these topics). 

In addition to the specific recommendations on the Environmental Science program, you can find an overview of 300+ courses, which are relevant in a sustainability-context on the UCPH One Planet website, where you can also see the content of the courses and the requirements for signing up: UCPH One Planet.

Also on the UCPH One Planet website, you can see a list of green student organisations at UCPH, that you can join if you want to engage in sustainability changes.
