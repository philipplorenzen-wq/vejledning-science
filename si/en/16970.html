Before you appeal against a grade or another academic issue, it's important that you:

  1. are given reasons for your grade by the examiner so you can assess the basis for a possible appeal
  2. are given a copy of your submitted exam paper by your examiner if you do not have access to it yourself
  3. understand what your grade means and are familiar with the learning outcomes that have been set out for the course in question.

Find the learning outcomes in the course description
If you do not have sufficient time to do this before the deadline for appeals, you can apply for permission to appeal after the deadline. Read how under 'What are the deadlines for appeals?'


How to appeal an exam result at SCIENCE

Write a reasoned appeal and submit it along with the online form 'Submit your exam appeal'.


As a minimum, your written appeal must include:

  • a description of what you are appealing against and the exam concerned
  • the reasons for your appeal
  • an explanation of what you want to achieve with the appeal. 

Include relevant documentation, if available.

Submit your exam appeal


Appeals concerning exams at other faculties

If you are on an inter-faculty study programme, you must follow the appeals procedure at the faculty that is in charge of the course/exam.
