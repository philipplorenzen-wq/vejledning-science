Good scientific practice is when your oral and written work is the result of your own efforts and that you make it clear when you are using sources to support your work.

You must observe the principles of good scientific practice in your everyday life as a student at UCPH.

This means that you must:

  • Follow the rules that apply for your exams, for example not use exam aids or work together if it is not allowed at the exam in question. Find the rules for the individual exams in the course description in the course database. your curriculum.
  • Make clear citations of all the sources you are using. Use the 8 tips for source citation shown below as a checklist when beginning an assignment.
