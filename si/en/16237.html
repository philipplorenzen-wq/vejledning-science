Most of the courses in the first year are compulsory for you. I think of these courses as the cornerstones of the program that lay the foundation for your later specialization. It is a good idea to follow these courses in the sequence that is laid out in the recommended course of the study. This way you will experience the intended scientific progression where elements of one course extend on elements from a previous course.

Specialization

At the end of year 1 and in the first half of year 2, you have freedom to specialize by choosing the restricted elective and elective courses that you find most interesting. For the restricted electives however, you must choose courses from List of restriced elective courses, where we have identified courses that are particularly relevant for you as an ENRE student.

Courses at other universities

You can also apply to take restricted elective courses at other faculties of UCPH, other universities in Denmark or other countries. Applications are assessed on a case-by-case basis, but the criteria are fairly straightforward: A restricted elective course must have a certain minimum amount of economics and it has to be at MSc level, not overlapping with any of the compulsory courses. You are always welcome to contact me if you would like my advice as to what courses I find most relevant.

Elective subjects

The elective subjects are almost completely unrestricted, so you can principally choose any course that you find interesting. I do advise, though, that beyond personal interests you also think about the professional profile and specialization that the chosen courses will give you. For instance, in relation to what you would like to work with in the future and/or where you would like to get a job – does your profile match what your future employer might be looking for?

Design your own study plan from scratch or work from the pre-filled study plans under Program structure.
