Some of the pressure we put on ourselves is due to the mindset we approach a given task or challenge with. 

Many of us tend to focus too much on our performance and too little on the process – what we are doing and what we are gaining from it, regardless of the result.

4 things you can remind yourself of when feeling pressured: 

  • Studying is about learning new things, gaining knowledge and developing – not about being good at everything from the outset, or being among the best on all your courses. 
  • You always learn something – even if something goes badly or you do worse than you or others had expected. 
  • Your mistakes and critical feedback from teachers or fellow students can be key to developing and getting better at something. 
  • When you dare to make mistakes, you dare to challenge yourself, take a leap into the unknown and learn more. And the more mistakes you make – the less frightening it will be to do so.

It is, of course, easier to say all this than to believe it and live it out. But like everything else, you can practise and gradually get better at it.
