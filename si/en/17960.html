It is important to prioritise your mental energy, so that you do not waste it. There are many factors that affect your life as a student, and some of them are beyond your control. 

You should therefore practise: 

  • accepting what you cannot change 
  • saving your energy for areas where you can influence your situation.

Examples of things you cannot change

Things you can decide

The compulsory elements of your study programme
How you prioritise the elective elements of your study programme, and how much effort you put into each course
Illness or functional impairment that impacts your studies
How you use the resources available to you
Who is in your class or year
 Which social communities you seek out and prioritise


Think of your area of influence as a fried egg 

It may help to picture your area of influence as a fried egg: 

  • The yolk is everything you know you can influence. You can make choices and decide on your own priorities in this area.
  • The egg white represents the aspects of your life you might be able to change. You may like to consider whether the effort needed to effect change in this area is worth it.
     
  • The frying pan represents all those things that are beyond your control. These are the things you need to lay aside and be at peace with, so you can use your energy on the things you actually can influence.
