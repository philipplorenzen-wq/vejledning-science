Since your timetable changes, it is a good idea to create a new overview for each semester/block. So you know, for example, when you can fit in work shifts, go on holiday or take part in your regular leisure activities. 

You can use your phone or email calendar. Or you can create your own customised calendar, which you can print and hang up. For example, you can create a customised calendar at kalendersiden.dk.

Items to enter in your plan for the semester/block: 

  • Submission dates, exam periods, teaching days and any known deadlines or assignments. 
  • Days when you know you will not be able to study due to work, major events or other commitments. 
  • Holiday periods and some whole days off at weekends, where you plan to not study but rather relax and re-energise. 


Before the semester/block starts, you can find information about your courses and exam periods in: 

  • the course descriptions at courses.ku.dk 
  • here on Student Information under Exams > Find time and place Exam cheating

Is your plan realistic? 

Finally, assess whether your plan looks realistic in relation to your daily routine, including preparation and leisure time.

If the plan looks overfull already, ask yourself: 

  1. what can I cut out or spend less time doing? 
  2. which of the non-compulsory elements do I want to prioritise? 


Only you can decide, in relation to your situation, whether it makes the most sense to devote less time to your student job or put a leisure activity on hold etc. 

You may also have to cut down on your preparation time or lower your academic aspirations for a while.
