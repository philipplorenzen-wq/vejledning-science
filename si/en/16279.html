What is the extent to which I can study at reduced hours for no special reason?

Placering: Planning your studies › Changes to your course of study › Study at reduced hours

Sprog: English · Elementtype: Accordion (narrow format)

Gælder for målgrupperne: Det Natur- og Biovidenskabelige Fakultet - Kandidat

Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/What-is-the-extent-to-which-I-can-study-at-reduced-hours-for-no-special-reason-SCI.aspx

You can slow down your studying pace as much as the framework of your study programme allows if you have no special reason for doing so, but, for example, just want more time for

  • academic immersion
  • work
  • family
  • other things.

The extent to which you can slow down your studying pace depends on how quick you have been in your studies so far.


1) Prepare a study plan covering the whole of your study programme

Prepare a study plan which allows you to study at the desired pace. You can keep a block free from scheduled teaching activities if your plan permits.

Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure.
Important when preparing your study plan:

  • Include all programme elements (including the ones you have passed).
  • Check that your courses are offered in the block in which you want to take them.
  • If you change the order of your courses, you must make sure that this is possible – does one course require you to take another course first, or does switching the courses around affect your academic progression? Find answers in the course description or ask the course coordinator.
    Find the course description 
  • Comply with the maximum duration of study You can add another year of study in the digital study plan in the bottom right corner.
    See your maximum duration of study
  • Make sure to add time in the plan for preparing for any re-exams.


2) Find out whether you meet the study activity requirements

Under each year of study in the digital study plan, it will be shown how many ECTS credits you have entered.
For each year of study, make sure you still meet

  • UCPH study activity requirements
  • SU study acticvity requirements
Modify your plan so that you are sure to meet the study activity requirements.


3) You may consult a study and career guidance counsellor about your study plan

If you need guidance on your new study plan, you can contact a study and career guidance counsellor from UCPH Study Guidance.
