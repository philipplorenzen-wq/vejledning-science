I encourage you to try studying abroad or by other means to get some international experience – particularly if you have not tried it already. However, this is a programme with many international students, so many of you went abroad to Denmark in order to study in this programme.

There are several possibilities for going abroad for a full semester. The mobility window can be broadened by switching the Innovation and Entrepreneurship course from block 4, year 1 to block 1, year, 2 while the course Food and Meal Consumer Research could be replaced with a similar course taken at the visiting university.

Also be aware that you can get experiences from "being abroad" by other means than taking a full semester at another institution. For instance, you can take a summer course abroad in block 5, year 1. Or you can make a project outside of scope or a project in practice in block 3, year 1. It is also possible to do your thesis abroad in full or partly.

The following universities are some of the universities that University of Copenhagen collaborates with that offer courses particularly relevant to your programme. The list is meant for inspiration and may not be complete, and collaborative partners and courses offered are subject to chance. Also note that you cannot be guaranteed a seat - there is a fixed application process you must follow, and you need to plan some time ahead.

  • Wageningen University in Holland (very strong in this field, and with a block structure that facilitates exchange)
  • Eidgenössische Technische Hochschule Zürich (ETH Zurich) in Switzerland
  • Sveriges Lantbruksuniversitet (SLU) in Uppsala, Sweden (limited number of relevant courses)
  • University of Umeå, Department of Food Nutrition and Culinary Science
  • Lund University in Sweden (very nearby, you do not need to move, if you do not want to)
  • Ghent University in Belgium
  • Örebro University in Sweden (strong within Culinary Arts and Hospitality Science, but most courses are offered in Swedish)
  • Universität für Bodenkultur Wien (BOKU) in Austria (many relevant courses)
  • University College Cork in Ireland
  • Cornell University in USA
  • Deakin University in Australia (sensory and consumer science, flavour analyses, specifically sensory marketing, sensory science, flavour analyses, food choice)
