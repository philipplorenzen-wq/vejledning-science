Your brain needs to be warmed up before you start on a task. This improves your focus and your ability to remember what you are reading or working with. 

Try approaching the task as you would a jigsaw puzzle:

  1. Get a grasp of the ‘picture’ you are about to build
    Skim through the assignment or the text and get a general idea of what it is about. 
  2. Lay the ‘frame’ for the puzzle
    Read the preface, look quickly at the headings or acquaint yourself with the theoretical framework.
  3. Sort the ‘pieces’
    Skim quickly through the various parts of the assignment or sections of the text, to get an impression of the different elements. 
Once you have completed this exercise, your brain is ready to start working on the assignment or text.
