Highlighting the texts you read can help you to get an overview of, understand and remember them – especially if you are consistent in how you do this and do not highlight too much.

Highlight systematically by deciding in advance: 

  • what you want to highlight in the text and why 
  • which colour to use for what. 

3 ideas for things you can highlight in a text: 

  1. Things you do not understand. So you can quickly find the things you need to ask about at the lecture or practical class. 
  2. Key points. To get an overview of the most important content, and to be able to quickly refresh your memory. 
  3. Things you find particularly surprising/interesting. So you can later identify the things you want to study in more detail, for example in an assignment or a project. 

You can use different colours for the various highlights if you want to highlight different elements in the same text.
