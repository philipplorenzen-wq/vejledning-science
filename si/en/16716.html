You can either apply for

  • special conditions that will make it possible for you to participate in an exam
  • change of exam form, e.g. from written to oral exam. 

Special conditions can take many forms

UCPH tries to meet you and your fellow students' needs. Therefore, there is not a complete list of what the special conditions are.


Examples of special conditions at the exam:

  • Permission to use special aids for a written or oral exam, e.g. aids you have received through the SPS Counselling
  • Prolong duration of examination and preparation time
  • Permission to bring your own computer at written on-site exams
  • Electronic handing in and out at written on-site exams
  • Sit a written on-site exam with only a few or no other students in the exam room
  • Permission to bring an interpreter

You are not guaranteed to get exactly the same special conditions as at your home university, unfortunately.

Changed exam type may be an option

You can apply to change the exam type, e.g. from a written to an oral exam.

This is only possible if you both

  1. have a very good reason why this is necessary for you
  2. are able to find an alternative exam type that lives up the learning outcome of the course. Ask your course coordinator for hers/his suggestion for such an exam type.

It's the study board who will assess from your application whether or not you are allowed to take the exam as another exam type.
