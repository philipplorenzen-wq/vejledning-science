From the outset, you must be aware of whether your absence has consequences if there are ongoing tests on your course – i.e. where you do not pass the course at a final exam, but via, for example, tests, assignments or the like during the course.

See if your course has ongoing tests in the course description

What to do in case of absence

Talk to your course coordinator continuously to assess whether you can still pass the course. 

By the last course day, you must have agreed with the course coordinator on whether your illness prevents you from passing the course. If that is the case you need to submit a formal notification of illness.

You can also try to withdraw from the course if it's not compulsory for you (find info below).


If you need to submit a notification of illness

  1. Contact your doctor or psychologist during your illness to obtain documentation of your illness. Read more on this page under 'What are the requirements for documentation from my doctor?'
  2. Submit a notification of illness via the online form below and upload your documentation no later than 8 calendar days from and including the last course date.
    If your documentation isn’t ready by the deadline, you’ll need to upload a document instead, stating when you expect to be able to submit it. 

Notify of illness
If your notification of illness is approved, you will avoid using an exam attempt. You will usually receive a reply within 6 weeks. 

You must assess whether you have to

  • register for the course again
  • register for the exam without following the course.

Read the course description and talk to your course coordinator about what is possible for you.

If you don't want to take the course again 

If you do not want to take the course again and it is either an elective or a restricted elective course, you must apply for a dispensation to withdraw from the course after the deadline instead of submitting a notification of illness.

If you use or have used an exam attempt for the course, it is very difficult to withdraw.

Read more about how to apply to be withdrawn from a course after the deadline
