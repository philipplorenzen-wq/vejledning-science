As a graduate student, you are expected to be independent and responsible for your own education.

It is your responsibility to be aware of deadlines for registering for courses and exams and where the classes and exams take place. All important messages about your study programme and deadlines are announced as study messages, on the  KUnet front page, as well as on Absalon. It is very important that you read these messages on a regular basis in order not to miss important information. 

I encourage you to get involved, both academically and socially in the daily life as an MSc student at MATH. I hope you will find studying at MATH inspiring and rewarding. 

Here is some advice for your studies: 

  • Participate in the online welcome programme. See here. 
  • The MSc programme is a full-time job. You should expect to spend 40-45 hours per week on your studies. It is important that you structure your everyday life to accommodate this. 
  • You should try to understand the rules  of your programme. Read about it here 
  • UCPH Study Guidance can help with many things, like  guidance on your study plan, rules and regulations, or on career issues.
  • The teaching and curriculum improve, when you give feedback. Please evaluate the courses after each block.
  • UCPH takes cheating very seriously. Make sure you are familiar with the rules.
  • There are many interesting research seminars at MATH, and MSc students are always welcome. Check the department calendar regularly. 
  • The department colloquium and the prestigious Harald Bohr lectures are a real treat for Masters students. This is the chance to meet some of the giants of mathematics.
  • There are many other activities at MATH and UCPH  that you could  get involved in: Student hangout in S01, Haralds følge, Fagrådet, MatRevy (satire show by students), MathGalla, Didaktikklubben, Student Cafés, student politics,  Math students Facebook-page (and humor), and many more. 

Last but certainly not least: you need to find a healthy study-life balance.
