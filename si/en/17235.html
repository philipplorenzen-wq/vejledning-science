The decisions we make do not always lead to the outcomes we expected or hoped for. And it is easy to blame yourself when you were the one who made the decision. But this will not make you wiser or happier.

5 tips for looking ahead: 

  1. Remember that you have learned something, no matter what. This may seem like an annoying tip at the moment, but think about all the ‘mistakes’ researchers have to make before they arrive at a theory, formula or new discovery.
  2. Allow yourself to feel frustrated and disappointed. This is part of the process that you must go through.
  3. Talk about it, for example with your study group, other fellow students, or your supervisor. There are sure to be others who have experienced something not going as well as they had hoped, or who have had to change course during their studies or working life.
     
  4. Reach out, for example to your study and career guidance counsellors or your lecturer, if you need help to find out what your options are in the situation or to discuss the actual consequences of your decision.
  5. Think of it as one unfortunate choice among many good ones. You cannot avoid making choices that miss the mark – no matter how carefully you consider them. But you are sure to also choose well some of the time. Perhaps you had to make this choice in order to work out what was right for you?
