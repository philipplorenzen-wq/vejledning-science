How and when you apply differ a little bit depending on whether you apply for specicial conditions or a different type of exam.

Special conditions for an exam – how  and when to apply

The application deadline for special conditions for an exam depends on the type of your exam. In all cases, you should apply as soon as possible.

  • If your course has ongoing tests or part of your exam takes place during the block, you must apply no later than 6 weeks before the first part of your exam. Are you a new student at your study programme, you must apply as soon as possible.
  • For all other types of exams, the deadline is Friday in the 3rd week of the course's block. If your course runs over 2 blocks, the deadline is Friday in the 3rd week of the course's last block.

Read about the possibilities of applying after the deadline under 'Can I apply for special conditions for my exam after the deadline?'


3 steps when you apply for special conditions:

  1. Get documentation for illness or decreased functionality, e.g. doctor's certificate, test results.
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Write a statement describing why you apply for special conditions at the exam. If you apply for additional aids, you must specifically state what aids you are applying for and why.
  3. Fill out an application by clicking the button below and upload documentation and your written statement.

Change of exam type – how to apply

You need to apply no later than 6 weeks before your exam in order to get an answer on your application in time.

4 steps when you apply for a change of exam type:

  1. Get documentation for illness or decreased functionality, e.g. doctor's certificate, test results.
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Write a statement describing why you apply for another exam type. 
  3. Get a written statement from your course coordinator with a suggestion for an alternative exam type.
  4. Fill out an application by clicking the button below and upload documentation and the written statements.
