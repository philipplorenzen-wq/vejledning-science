If you are sitting an exam where notes are allowed, you can upload them to Digital Exam no later than two hours before the exam, unless something else has otherwise been specified in your programme curriculum or course description for your current exam.

It is not allowed to bring notes on a USB memory stick.

How to upload your notes to Digital Exam:

  1. Log on to Digital Exam
  2. Click on your exam
  3. At the bottom of the page ‘Exam information’, click on ‘Upload notes'.

Your uploaded notes will be available on your exam in the Exam House  

Please note that:

  • You can upload your notes one week before the exam at the earliest and two hours before the exam starts at the latest.
  • You can upload a max. of 1 GB in total
  • You can upload entire folders. Be aware that uploading folders with many sub-layers of folders can be problematic.
  • Check that you have uploaded all notes/folders you need for the exam
  • You need to be able to open the files on the Exams House computers. You can check which programs will be available for your exam at Digital Exam. The most common text file types are always a good choice – for example txt, pdf and docx.
  • Your notes in OneNote will generally not be accesible in the Exam House. That is because the setup in the Exam House does not allow cloud access. You may consider exporting and uploading your OneNote files as notes to your exam.

Avoid plagiarism

Digital notes are not subject to plagiarism control. However, everything you submit at the exam will be checked for plagiarism. Therefore, you must make sure to cite correctly, for example if you cite from shared notes.
Read how to cite correctly when using shared notes.
