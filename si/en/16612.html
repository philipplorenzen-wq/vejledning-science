The International Alliance of Research Universities (IARU) is a network of 11 leading international universities, of which the University of Copenhagen (UCPH) is a member.
Several of the universities offer summer courses (IARU Courses) specifically aimed at students from the institutions in the Alliance.

You can find the list of this year's IARU Courses on the IARU website.

Application

You apply for admission to an IARU Course by submitting your application directly to the individual university. The application procedure and deadline are stated in the course description.

You should also contact the student guidance service for your degree programme, which can advise you on how to apply for pre-approval.

Finances

You must pay a tuition fee to participate in an IARU Course.
Information about the tuition fee and any additional expenses can be found in the individual course descriptions.

Study Abroad Grant:
If you receive SU and you can transfer the course credits to your degree at UCPH, you can apply for apply to the Danish State Education Grant (Udlandsstipendium) and Loan Scheme for a study-abroad scholarship.

See how to apply for a study-abroad scholarship on the Danish State Education Grant and Loan Scheme's website.

In addition, you may apply for a UCPH IARU scholarship (for short IARU Courses lasting up to approximately four weeks and held during the summer months):

IARU Travel Scholarship: If you will be attending a regular in-person IARU Course abroad, you may apply for a UCPH IARU Travel Scholarship. You can apply for DKK 5,000 for courses in Europe and DKK 8,000 for courses outside Europe.

IARU Tuition Scholarship: You can apply for a UCPH IARU Tuition Scholarship of up to DKK 4,000 to help cover the tuition fee.
This scholarship is only awarded if you are not already awarded a Study Abroad Grant (Udlandsstipendium), or if the Study Abroad Grant only partially covers the tuition costs.

To apply for a UCPH IARU travel and/or tuition scholarship, you must send your application by e-mail to iaru.gsp@adm.ku.dk, including the following documents:

  • Admission letter for the relevant IARU Course
  • Pre-approval of courses from your faculty
  • Invoice for tuition invoice (when applying for a tuition scholarship)
  • “Award notification" from the State Education Grant Office (SU-kontoret) documenting the amount of the Study Abroad Grant or a rejection thereof

Application deadline is 1 May 2026.

Note: If UCPH receives more applications than the number of scholarships available, scholarships will be awarded based on grade point average.
