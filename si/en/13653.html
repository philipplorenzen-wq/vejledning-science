Below you will find a number of tips for source referencing, which can function as a checklist when you write an assignment.

8 tips for source referencing

  1. Be clear when you cite your sources – clearly indicate which sources you are using where. If relevant, ask your teachers if they have any recommendations. As a student at UCPH, you can freely use the EndNote and Zotero citation tools. You should consider citation tools as help tools. It is still your responsibility to make correct and adequate citations in your paper.
  2. Always provide exact source citations when you cite sentences, tables, figures, phrases, structure or ideas from other people's scientific work.
     
  3. Remember to cite the source when you use special terms and expressions from someone else's work.
  4. You can also cite yourself and a former assignment or scientific article if you want to incorporate your own previous points or results.
  5. Remember also to cite shared notes. Notes that are written together with others are not your own, but the group’s. You must state who the notes have been made with, and specify precisely which part of the exam paper has been taken from the shared notes.
  6. Make quotations where relevant. When a piece of text is reproduced verbatim, remember to use quotation marks and to cite the source – even if it is your own formulation that you have used in a previous assignment.
     
  7. Remember to mark it clearly if you temporarily copy a piece of text from a source to your draft assignment – for example to remind yourself to write a paragraph about it. In that way you can remind yourself to either delete it again or clearly cite the source before submitting the assignment.
     
  8. Better to make too many citations than too few. Ask your teacher or head of studies if you are in doubt as to whether you should cite or not.
