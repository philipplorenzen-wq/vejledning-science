There is more than one way to become a successful Food Innovation and Health master's student. It is important that you take responsibility for how you study and your choices during the programme. I recommend that you make your choices based on your own academic interests. If you need sparring in order to qualify your choices, you can contact me or UCPH Study Guidance.

Here are some points that may help you along your way:

  • Being a full time student in this programme requires a considerable amount of work. Expect 35-45 hours per week. It may be helpful for you to structure your "study time" and plan ahead when you follow classes, participate in lab work or group work, prepare yourself and make room for sports, social activities etc. Read about study techniques.
  • During the programme you will encounter several different teaching styles. It may also be beneficial for you to be more aware of your own personal preferred learning styles and how to best accommodate these. Many students find it helpful to form study groups. Get inspiration for your study group.
  • As well as different teaching styles, we also have different exam types, which may be different from what you are used. Read about good exam techniques.  The university take cheating and plagerisms seriously, so read here about how to avoid plagiarism and cheating at the examination. 
  • We take great interest in your opinion about the courses, which are continuously adapted based on the feedback students give. I therefore highly encourage you to evaluate courses. You can also talk to the course coordinator while the courses are running if you have some input. The hierarchical structure in Denmark is generally flat, and most lecturers are open to input.
  • I encourage you to check out the study events at the university. They cover a wide range of interesting topics.
  • Read your study messages and check your study events on a regular basis not to miss important messages and events.  
  • The first year, I will invite you to block meetings with your fellow students, please attend to hear information from me as well as good advice about for instance course registrations and thesis writing.
  • Being a student means having a busy schedule. It is, however, also important not to get too stressed. Learn more about how to get refilled with energy or to gear down and relax to avoid harmful stress.
  • Our organization is called FEF. They arrange many interesting things. dor students. 
  • Furthermore, you can be active in the social group Gastronomic Playground
  • You may join the Facebook group for your year of studies if you wish. You will be informed about the group's name during the welcome days.
