As a part of your studies, you will also have to do projects, some of these will be coordinated by course coordinators, but others such as your thesis and projects outside course scope will be (partly) coordinated and at least initiated by you.

There are a few general considerations regarding these projects. First of all, start your preparations well in advance, because sometimes these projects cannot be initiated from week to week, but rather from month to month or even year to year.

Project in Practice, which is a collaboration with a company requires that the student will have to find a contact in the company, and also to find a potential supervisor from the University. It is not impossible, but this type of project requires a fair amount of dedication from the student. This should not dissuade you from exploring the opportunity, but a lot of the exploration comes from YOU, not the university.

Project outside the course scope, is a project within the university, which means that you only have to find a supervisor from the university. This is a very good solution if you are interested in a particular topic that is not offered as a course. Some students have also made a project outside course scope within a topic that is already offered as a course, but because the course did not fit into their study plan, they did a project. The only thing to remember is that the teacher is not obliged to supervise you, so you have to ask nicely.

The Master's thesis can be performed either entirely at the University or in collaboration with a company. The same suggestion about being in good time applies to finding a supervisor. Remember that you can also find a “new" topic for your thesis, and use the thesis to obtain novel competences, but most of you will normally find a supervisor from courses that you have already followed throughout the years.
