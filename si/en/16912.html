During the Forest and Nature Management programme you have to carry out at least two projects. The first is part of the compulsory Forest and Nature Management Planning course, the second is your Master's thesis. Further, you can do a Project in Practice and/or a Project outside the course scope.

Forest and Nature Management Planning

The purpose of the compulsory block 4 course Forest and Nature Management Planning is to familiarize with the planning process and give you experience with writing a scientifically based, yet practically oriented management plan. It is a group project, so you learn to work as a team on a larger project – to produce a management plan and write a project report about it. The planning project synthesizes across the whole curriculum and represent an opportunity to consider possible topics and ideas for your master's thesis.

Master's Thesis

The master's thesis is the final part of the programme, where you experience/show that you have become a Forest and Nature Management Expert. The thesis represents the last chance to specialize further in the direction you want. The standard Master's thesis equals 30 ECTS, but it is possible to carry out a 45 ECTS thesis, typically to allow for collection of empirical data. The 45 ECTS option requires planning as it cuts the number of elective courses.

You have to choose your topic for the thesis and find a suitable supervisor. I recommend that you develop your idea for a thesis topic. However, if you don't know what you want to write about, or who you would like to supervise you, you can find inspiration in KU Project and Jobbank . In relation to this, IFRO, annually, hosts a master's thesis startup seminar. The purpose is to run over the practicalities, give hints and advice regarding good study practices, support your efforts to find a topic and a supervisor, as well as a Q&A session.

Remember that it is your responsibility to find a thesis topic and supervisor. It might take some time and require some effort, so it is a good idea to start doing this well in advance, at least a couple of months before the thesis starts. If you want to do an empirical or experimental study, you need to consider the 45 ECTS option and you need to start planning, ahead at an early stage.

I deeply recommend that you from the very beginning of your studies make it into practice, to consider, note, and collect possible ideas for thesis work – themes you find interesting, relevant “un-answered" questions of importance for society, good research questions, subjects that stimulate your curiosity, and/or even could open doors for an interesting future? You may even along the way develop some of those ideas into rough sketches of possible thesis problem statements. Then you have something to work from as you finally approach your thesis.

Project in Practice

I encourage you to consider the option of doing a Project in Practice. Experience and statistics show that this may be an excellent springboard to getting the first job after your education – maybe even with the study host. At any rate, a Project in Practice is an excellent chance to get some valuable experience and insight into how a workplace works, and an opportunity to reflect on how you can contribute and apply your academic knowledge in a practical setting – in a private company, a public organization, or maybe in a NGO. A project in practice will substitute some of your elective courses.

Read more about the Project in Practice.

Project outside the course scope

You also have the option of spending some of your elective credits on doing a Project outside the course scope. This is an option to work with some special topic of your own particular interest. The option might also be used to fill in a few ECTS points needed to finish off, while meeting the requirements of a full programme (120 ECTS).
