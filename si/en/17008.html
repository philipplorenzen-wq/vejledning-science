You are basically free to shape your project in practice according to your academic interests, but within a fixed framework.

Your project must include:

  1. Spending time in and working with a company, acquiring both practical experience at being in a workplace as well as academic knowledge. It can also be spending time working with our own idea/company.
     
  2. A project report in which you explain, analyse and process the material you have collected in the company from an academic perspective.
     Read more about your report
      
  3. An oral exam which is based on the project report which you have handed in.
     Read more about your exam


Project purpose and requirements

The purpose of the project in practice is basically to provide you with concrete experience and a better understanding of your academic profile in a professional context.


With your project in practice, you must demonstrate, among other things, that you can

  • apply theory and methods from your study programme in a company or organisation
  • reflect on your academic skills in relation to a professional context
  • gather new academic knowledge and communicate it.

Read more about the purpose and requirements your project in practice must live up to in the shared section of your curriculum.
