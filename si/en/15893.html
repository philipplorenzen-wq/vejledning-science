What is a relevant student job?

You can acquire relevant experience in many different types of student jobs.
So don't be afraid of taking a job that is not directly relevant to your studies. You will most likely learn something new – and often it can become a good stepping stone towards more relevant student jobs later on.
A relevant student job may be
  • directly related to the contents of your study programme and offer you a specific opportunity to test your expertise in practice and to work with tasks and problems related to your study programme
  • outside the academic contents of your study programme, but may nevertheless provide you with experiences and general competences that you will need in your working life.

Different ways your student job can be relevant

A job not directly relevant to your study programme can typically still provide you with experience with e.g.
  • administrative work or project management in some form or other
  • cooperation with colleagues and partners.
An administrative student job within your area of interest but with less relevant assignments can give you
  • relevant insights into a specific sector or type of organisation
  • contacts within a relevant sector.
