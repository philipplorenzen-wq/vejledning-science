There are several ways to follow the curriculum and to complete your studies successfully. It is important that you take responsibility of how you study and your choices throughout the programme. These two years are a unique period in your life. You have now the opportunity to learn about new and advanced topics in Computer Science and to lay the foundation for your future career.

My advice to master your studies and take advantage of your study opportunities:

  • Your study is your work. Expect to use 40-45 hours a week for your studies, and sometimes even more before the exams and hand-ins. Internships and jobs are best placed in the summer period.
  • You are in charge. A large part of your studies is independent or in groups outside the scheduled lecture hours, so it is largely up to yourself to manage your time and to organize yourself. It is a good idea to structure and plan your weeks, blocks, and academic year. Try new study techniques, take advantage of the rich study facilities including libraries and thesis workstations.
  • Widen your network. You are part of an international study programme. A great chance to expand your network of friends and colleagues. Denmark is casual and hands-on. Say “hello” and talk to your fellow students, don’t be shy. Take part in classroom discussions. Networking opens many new opportunities. Why not participate in Social events, join a sports activity, or Study abroad?
  • Expand your knowledge. Many scientists and PhD students from all over the world visit our department. Participate in guest lectures, summer schools, and other events organized by our research groups. Hear first-hand about the latest research and trends. Read about the DIKU research groups and follow DIKU on Facebook.
  • New in town? Explore the town and its environs – even your campus is home to exciting museums and gardens. Attend the study introduction program, meet your Buddy and, especially if you come from abroad, read about adjusting to life in Denmark to avoid culture shock.
  • Questions? Explore the wealth of online resources available on the KU intranet including more information about this master's programme.
  • Finally, a warning. The University takes plagiarism and cheating very serious. You are subject to the same general principles of scientific integrity as the academic staff at the University. The regulations may be stricter than what you are used to.
