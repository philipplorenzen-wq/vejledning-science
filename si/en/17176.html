Travel Grant:

All students receiving an Erasmus Traineeship-grant in the autumn 2024 (traineeship must being 1 August 2024 or later) and onwards will receive a Travel Grant.

The Travel Grant is a fixed amount given as a constribution for travel expences when travelling to and from your host institution (round-trip).

If you choose to travel sustaianble to and from your host institution, you will recieve a higher Travel Grant.

Application

When applying for the Erasmus Traineeship grant you must tick off in the application whether you want to receive the Travel Grant for sustainable or non-sustainable travel.

Requriements for the grant:

  • You must travel sustainable to and from your host institution at the start and end of your exchange
  • You must upload documentation for the sustainable travel (e.g., train ticket with your full name and dates for the travel) in the application module (Mobility-Online), when evaluating your Erasmus grant at the end of your exchange

If you end up travelling non-sustainble, we will substract the extra Travel Grant from the ordinary Erasmus Travel grant, when we approve the evaluation of your Erasmus grant, when you have completed your Erasmus Traineeship.

Definition for sustainable travel: Sustainable travel is defined as follows by the EU Commission: “Sustainable means of transport (green travel) is defined as the travel that uses low-emissions means of transport for the main part of the travel, such as bus, train or car-pooling". This means that travel by plane and ferry* is not considered sustainable. 

* If travelling by boat or ferry is necceary on a short leg of the journey, you can still receive the grant, e.g. if you travel by bus from Copenhagen to Amsterdam, and the bus goes by ferry between Denmark and Germany.
