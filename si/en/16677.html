As a Master's student you typically have to form one or more study groups with some of your fellow students. 

Depending on what is important to you, you can either form a study group with

  • fellow students from your courses 
  • other MSc students from your programme.

The size of your study group 

The ideal size of a study group depends on 

  • what works best with the course's or your programme's methods and ways to solve assignments (work in a laboratory, at a screen, etc.) 
  • your own preferences of how many you want to work with.

The 3 key ingredients and how to incorporate them 

The 3 most important ingredients in a good and stable collaboration is that you all 

  • have more or less the same expectations to the group work 
  • share your preferences for how you work best 
  • that you prioritize the course/studying more or less the same. 

You do not necessarily get the best study group by choosing your best friends. Your study group is actually a unique chance to practice working with people you don't know that well – an important and sought after skill that you will also need and practice in your work life.
