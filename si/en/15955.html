Feeling motivated about your studies is key to not giving up and to basically thriving as a student – even when you: 

  • encounter challenges along the way 
  • run into parts of your study programme that you find less interesting. 

Always bearing in mind the importance of keeping up and strengthening your motivation can therefore be helpful.

Characteristics of motivation: 

  • It can rise and fall in intensity but is more lasting and stable than desire. 
  • You can work with it and nourish it in various ways. 
  • It is particularly influenced by and dependent on your feeling of being on top of your studies and finding your studies meaningful as well as your sense of community with other people on or associated with your study programme. 

If you are interested in a more theoretical perspective on motivation in relation to student life, you can read sociologist Vincent Tinto’s article, Through the eyes of students.
