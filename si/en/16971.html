What if I don’t meet the requirements?

As a general rule, if you have not been approved for an exam, you will not be allowed to take the exam.

You will use an exam attempt regardless of whether you have been approved or not. If you do not meet the prerequisites for taking the exam, you will be given the grade LoQ (Lack of Qualifications).


You may still be able to meet the examination prerequisites before the exam

Always ask your course coordinator about your options in your specific situation. In some cases, you may still be able to meet the prerequsites before the exam, even if you were not initially approved for it.


You may be able to meet the examination prerequisites before the re-exam

On some courses, you may be able to take the re-exam if you manage to meet the examination prerequisites before then. See your course description under 'Exams'.

To see whether you will be able to meet the examination prerequisites before the re-exam, check your course description 


If there is still time to meet the examination prerequisites before the re-exam: Ask the course coordinator what you need to do to meet the prerequisites for taking the exam before the re-exam. 


If there is no time to meet the examination prerequisites before the re-exam:
You will need to register and take the course again the next time it is offered. You will be guaranteed a place on the course if you retake it.
