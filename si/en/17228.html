A mindset is the perspective or approach you take to a task or challenge.

According to mindset theory, there are essentially 2 types of mindset that we all alternate between in various situations: A growth mindset and a fixed mindset.

Most of us can benefit from developing more of a growth mindset.
A growth mindset helps you to: 

  • handle adversity and things that are difficult for you in a more constructive way 
  • get the most out of your studies and continue to develop along the way. Characteristics of the 2 mindsets

Characteristics of the 2 mindsets

The growth mindsetThe fixed mindset
Sees challenges and mistakes as opportunities to learn something new and develop
Avoids new challenges for fear of failing
Persists despite obstaclesGives up easily if something is difficult
Is responsive to and learns from feedbackHas difficulty receiving feedback
Sees hard work as essential in order to become good at somethingSees hard work as a waste if you do not have the talent
Focuses on learning and the process rather than the resultFocuses extensively on results, e.g. grades
The woman behind the mindset theory is Carol Dweck, who is a professor of psychology at Stanford University.

5 things you can do to train your growth mindset

How to challenge yourself, for example at times when you feel stuck in a rut:

  1. Throw yourself into a task, even though it is difficult and your subconscious is shouting “No, no, no!”.
  2. Keep going for another 5 minutes when you get frustrated – often the frustration disappears, and it will be easier to handle the difficult situation. 
  3. Talk to others about what you find difficult – this will make it less overwhelming. 
  4. Ask for feedback from people you trust, e.g. your study group. Try to accept the feedback you receive, without commenting or explaining yourself in the first instance. 
  5. Ask your fellow students what they have done in a particular situation or challenge that you would find difficult to handle. You can learn a lot from hearing how other people tackle the things you are struggling with.
