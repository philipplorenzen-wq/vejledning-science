Many institutions, organisations and associations also provide support or counselling. Below is a list of the places that focus on students. 

Some are at UCPH, others are outside. Common to them is, however, that they are all there to help:

  • Student Counselling: Find your faculty or department – they can help with questions about special conditions, such as extra time for exams.
  • SU guidelines: Find out about the SU rules, disability supplements, activity requirements and additional grant portions.
  • Student Counselling Service: Social and psychological counselling, among other things
  • The Psychiatry Foundation: Exercises that can help you deal with anxiety, stress, depression, etc. (in Danish)
  • Student chaplain: Students' own chaplains. They listen and can help to clarify your options.
  • Student Ambassador Provides help if you are unsure about your rights or need help to apply for a dispensation. 
  • SUMH: Organisation for young people with mental, physical and neurological disabilities. 

See a list of people who can help you at UCPH.

What are special conditions?

 

In the film above, you can hear more about what we call special conditions (note that the video is in Danish).
If you have a functional impairment, you can apply for special conditions in class or for exams. This could be extra time for your exams or a different examination form. 

The student counselling at your faculty or department can help you to apply for special conditions. This is not something that the SPS counsellor can do for you.
