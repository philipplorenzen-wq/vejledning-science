How to spend your elective credits

In all specialisations you have elective credits to spend. You can for example spend your elective credits on:

  • MSc Physics courses from the list of restricted elective courses of your own specialization or of one of the other MSc Physics specializations. Check the recommended qualifications before you register.
  • BSc courses from Physics or other programs, at maximum two courses (15 ECTS). Use this opportunity if you need additional qualifications.
  • Project outside the course scope (individual project). Maximum allowed is 15 ECTS.
  • A  thesis preparation project (PREP) . This gives you a chance to start early on e.g. learning to use methods or lab equipment or doing literature study for your thesis. Note that the contents of your PREP cannot be re-used in the thesis itself.
  • A project in practice (PIP) is a project carried out within a company.
  • Other relevant MSc courses from UCPH, e.g. from mathematics, computer science, chemistry…
  • A language course in Danish.
  • And more…

Example

  • The MSc course Condensed Matter Physics 2 (CMP2) requires qualifications equivalent to the BSc course Condensed Matter Physics 1 (CMP1). If you plan to take CMP2 in block 2 and do not have sufficient qualifications, you can choose CMP1 in block 1 of your 1st year as an elective course.
