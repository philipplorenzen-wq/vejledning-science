How do I write a good solicited application?

There is not one single correct way of writing your application. But there are some standards and steps that employers will often expect and that it will therefore be advantageous for you to consider.
Your application must include 5 elements:
  1. A catchy and relevant heading
  2. Your motivation for applying for precisely the job in question at precisely this workplace
  3. How you can perform the various tasks and which of your competences you can use. 
  4. A little bit about yourself as a person: How are you as a colleague?
  5. A brief conclusion.
Get tips for each element in this application guide
See an example of  a good application

Show how you would be right for the position

In your application, you need to imagine your future at the workplace and write yourself into the position in question. Imagine how you would perform the specific tasks and which competences you would use. 

Read more about how to prepare a good CV


Make your application well-structured and clear

Preferably use sections with sub-headings and bullets in your application. In this way, you make it easy to read for your recipient and you highlight your most important points. The length of your application must be max. 1 page.

Other rules if you are applying for a PhD

If you are applying for a PhD position, you should be aware that other specific rules and frameworks apply to applications.
