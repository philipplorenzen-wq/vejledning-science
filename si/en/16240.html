Specialisations and compulsory courses

You have already selected one of the specializations (Plant Science or Production & Environment) of the MSc Agriculture programme. If you are unsure whether you have selected “the wrong specialisation" and consider changing it, contact me or UCPH Study Guidance as early as possible for guidance. The two specializations have their specific profile but also have some overlap. It is usually possible to change between specializations early in your programme with no delay in your studies. Be sure to spend some time to get to know the programme structure of your specialization.

Both specializations start in Block 1 with a compulsory course.

Plant Science students follow the Experimental Plant Science course which runs during the first two blocks.

Production & Environment students follow the Advanced Crop Production course.

In both courses you are introduced to the core areas of your specialization. You also meet key teachers and get acquainted with the research groups within your academic area.

In Production & Environment there is a second full time compulsory course in Block 4 in which you go off campus and work with real life problems with external partners, Farm and Food Systems.

Depending on your specialization you will do a thesis of 9 (60 ECTS), 6.5 (45 ECTS) or 4 (30 ECTS) months. All thesis sizes fulfill the goals of the programme but are suited for different profiles. When choosing the thesis length, think about two things: do I prefer to use my time on course work instead of thesis work, and does the thesis topic or the methods I want to learn require a certain amount of time due to e.g. seasonal field work or other experimental work. Take these wishes to your supervisor and make the decision about the size together.

Find more information about the Master's thesis.


Course types and selection

We have structured the MSc programme to ensure that you get certain key competencies (compulsory courses), but that you also have the freedom to pursue your own interests through a range of restricted elective and elective courses.

Think about the restricted elective courses as “core courses" of which you have to select a certain amount depending on your thesis size. You can use all your credits on restricted elective courses, but you can also use up to 22.5 ECTS credits on elective courses.

See the lists of restricted elective courses in your programme structure.

The elective courses give you the opportunity to get into fields or learn methods, in which you are specifically interested, or which you see as useful for your thesis work. You can also use your elective credits to follow relevant bachelor level courses in case you missed something more basic. The points for elective courses give you the freedom to go outside the typical topics or to follow courses abroad or offered by other faculties or universities.

See how you can use your elective credits.

Other activities than courses

Use the many learning opportunities beyond regular courses. Investigate the opportunities you have for shaping your profile and become competent in the areas which interest you the most or which you see as professionally promising. You can do individual research projects and projects in collaboration with an external organization or company, or you can do a project in relation to an internship (see below).
