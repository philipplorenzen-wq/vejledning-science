How do I resume my studies after taking leave of absence?

Your leave of absence ends after the period of leave applied for. If you do not extend your leave, you will be regarded as active on your study programme from when the period of leave has ended.

What to remember when you return:

  1. Register for courses and exams you must take after your leave of absence.
    See how and when to register for courses
    See how and when to register for exams
     
  2. Please notify the SU Office that you have resumed your studies and want to start receiving SU again.


 See how your maximum duration of study is affected under 'How does taking leave of absence affect my maxium duration of study?'


3 tips for getting back on track after period illness etc.

  1. Find out how much you can study. It is important that you resume your studies at a pace that suits you. You may like to consult your doctor, psychologist, etc., about how active you can be on your study programme. If you feel that you need to take things a bit more slowly, you can apply for a dispensation.
  2. Update your study plan. This can help you get an overview of any courses you still need to take, when to take them, and any changes to your study programme.  
    Read more about how to prepare a study plan tailored to your situation  
  3. Make appointments for the first days you are back. It may be good to arrange a lifeline or two when resuming your studies. Lifelines may be fellow students, a study and career guidance counsellor or perhaps a lecturer.
