If you need data about other persons for a project or in your master's thesis you must ensure that the data are not disclosed to someone without a lawfull access to the data. If data are disclosed they may be abused for identity theft. It is your responsibility to ensure that data are kept in a secure way.

It is also your responsibility to inform the persons being interviewed or the persons responding to a questionnaire about:

  • the purpose of your data processing
  • to whom they can address themselves if they regret their participation.

If you use biological material from other persons such as blood samples you equally are obliged to comply with the rules.

The participants have various rights when their data are processed. You are obliged to ensure these rights. To be on the safe side and in order not to violate the rules, it is important to study the rules you are obliged to follow as responsible for data about other persons.

FACTS:

The rules on collecting and processing personal data are laid down in the Regulation on Personal Data. It is also called GDPR, which is short for General Data Protection Regulation.
