How do I make a strong, secure password?

As a student at the University of Copenhagen, it is important that you are the only one that have access to your personal accounts, e.g. KUmail and Absalon. Among other things, you can ensure that by creating a secure password.

A secure password is:

  1. Long: A password should consist of at least 12 characters – but preferably more. You do not need to use special characters in your password. The most important thing is to create a long password that is easy to remember.
  2. Strong: Do not create passwords using words that are related to your everyday life, for example, passwords that are easy to guess when someone knows you well. Such information is very easy to find by using a search engine. Weak passwords are, for example, your child's name, your pet, your address, your workplace or other things from your everyday life.
  3. Unique: Avoid passwords that consist of a single word or a number combination. Hackers systematically try to access your account and use long lists of words in different languages that they find by searching in dictionaries, on Wikipedia, in the Bible or other sources on the internet. For example, if your password is 'Summer', 'London' or another word that can be found in a dictionary, hackers can more easily get access to your data.

A good tip: Use a passphrase ✔

Strong passwords can be difficult to remember, but you can make it easier by using a so-called passphrase instead of using a single word.

Compound sentences make good passwords because they are long and complicated. There are many methods for creating passphrases.
Here are two examples:

Example 1
Every word starts with a letter in the name of a person you know well. For example:
  1. coldanimallockselevatorbuilding (Cold Animal Locks Elevator Building = Caleb)
  2. girlinvitednineangelfish (Girl Invited Nine Angelfish = Gina)
  3. eachmanmadearmchairs (Each Man Made Armchairs = Emma)
You can replace some of the letters with symbols and numbers if you want to make your password even stronger. For example:
  • C0ldanim@ll()ckselevat0rbuilding
  • Eachm@nm@ade@rmchairs

Example 2

Choose five things from a room in your home, your latest holiday, a shopping basket etc. 

Examples:

  1. CasseroleRecipeKnifeCabinetFood
  2. CafeMuseumPoolSunHoliday
  3. FruitYoghurtBreadCakeCoffee
