There may be several reasons to appeal an exam result.


Four typical areas of appeal:

  • Legal issues – if rules and regulations concerning exams have not been complied with.
  • The basis for examination – for example if the exam question was formulated vaguely or went beyond the curriculum.
  • The examination process – for example if the exam question was sent out too late, if you were not allowed to use exam aids that are permissible according to the exam rules, or there was noise or disturbances in the exam room.
  • The assessment – if you feel the reasons given for your grade are arbitrary and unfair.
