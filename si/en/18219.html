If your application for a credit transfer for a course from a foreign educational institution is rejected on an unfair basis, you can lodge an appeal concerning the academic aspects of the decision to the Qualifications Board.


The decision may be based on the academic grounds

  • that the academic level of the desired course is not high enough
  • that the academic content does not fall within the scope of your study programme.


How to appeal the academic grounds for a decision

Submit your appeal within 2 weeks from when you have been notified of the Study Board's decision. You must formulate the appeal to the Qualifications Board and send it to studyguidance-science@adm.ku.dk. In exceptional circumstances, you may, in some cases, be allowed to submit your appeal after the expiry of this deadline.

Your appeal must be made in writing and must contain

  • a description of what you are complaining about
  • an explanation of the academic grounds on which you find that the decision is wrong.
  • Documentation of why you think the decision made by the Study Board is wrong (as far as this is possible).


Processing of your appeal

UCPH Study Guidance will forward your appeal to the Qualifications Board together with an opinion. The Qualifications Board makes the final administrative decision.


Possible outcomes of your case

The Qualifications Board may:

  • grant a credit transfer for more programme elements than approved by the Study Board
  • reject your appeal.

There is no fixed rule for when you will receive a reply to your appeal in such cases, but you can expect a reply within a reasonable period of time.
