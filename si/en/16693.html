Where can I find inspiration for my topic?

There are several places where you can find inspiration for your topic:

  • UCPH Projects & Jobs is a database with specific project proposals. Here you can either:
      • Find relevant project proposals targeted to your study programme
      • Search the database more widely for relevant project proposal from companies and other research units
          
  • Your (potential) supervisor can act as a sounding board with insight into your field of work.
  • Courses and lecturers who have introduced you to areas you would like to work with again.
  • Courses you would like to have done about a topic that sparked your interest, but which did not tie in with your curriculum.
    Look for inspiration at kurser.ku.dk
  • The departmental websites can provide information about a variety of research areas and units. See the pages about research.  

Two important questions to consider when choosing your topic


1) What do you want out of your project?

  • Are you looking for a specific academic outcome? For example, certain methods that you want to learn, a particular subject area you want to immerse yourself in, or a particular academic profile you want to strengthen.
  • Are you keen to develop certain general competencies? For example, your communication skills, your ability to work with other people in a group project.
    Read about the possibility of doing a group project


2) Do you have a specific idea, or are you open to input?

  • Is there a specific idea, or a specific field of interest that you are passionate about?
  • Are you open to a particular topic, modelled on a particular company that you would like to cooperate with?
