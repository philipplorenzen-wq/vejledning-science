Graduates from Environment and Development pursue employment in various industries. A growing number of private companies hire graduates with expertise in sourcing, certification, corporate social responsibility or ESG (environmental, social and governance), or product development. Traditionally, many get employment in environmental, agricultural, and nature management, whether in ministries and other public offices, or NGOs and private consultancy companies. Some decide to pursue a PhD, and eventually work in research organizations, universities, or research based extension and consultancy firms. Others opt for roles in development organizations and NGOs serving, e.g., as technical experts or managers.

The key qualifications graduates bring from their Environment and Development education include:

  • Interdisciplinary experience: The ability to situate knowledge in a broad (development) context.
  • Intercultural collaboration: Valued by companies, since this is crucial for those working in diverse cultural settings.
  • International fieldwork experience: Experience gained not only in but also away from the classroom
  • Project management and problem-solving skills: Gained through hands-on fieldwork, group projects, and thesis work.
  • Academic excellence achieved by mastering a chosen topics, or by becoming an expert generalist.
