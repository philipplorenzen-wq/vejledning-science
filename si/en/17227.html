Body and mind are closely linked. You can therefore also improve your mental health by doing something about your physical health.


3 key areas

3 areas are particularly important when it comes to ensuring you have the necessary resources for your life as a student: 

  1. That you are physically active
    Exercise improves your mood and concentration, and gives you more energy and greater resilience in stressful situations. Exercise can take the form of a brisk walk or run, cycling to the university, or a visit to the swimming pool. Do something you find fun and include it in your schedule. If possible, do it with a friend – so you can hold each other to it.
  2. That you sleep well and enough
    Sleep helps your body recover and regenerate. It is important for both your health and mood, and crucial to being able to concentrate and remember complex material as a student. Improve your sleep by going to bed and waking up at the same time every day, and by making your bed a screen-free zone before bedtime.
    Get more practical sleep tips from UCPH’s SmartSleep research project 
  3. That you eat well
    A good and varied diet is also crucial to having the necessary energy for your studies. Remember to eat breakfast and to eat during the day, so your blood sugar doesn’t crash in the middle of a lecture or in the reading room.
    Get inspiration from the Danish Health Authority’s dietary recommendations


It is just common sense – and you may have heard it all before. But given the busy life you live as a student, it’s easy to forget.
Reflect on whether and to what extent you need to focus on one or more of these areas.
