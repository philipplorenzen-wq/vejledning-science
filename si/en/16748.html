What am I allowed to use generative AI for?

Limited extent means you are allowed to use generative AI for dialogue and discussions before and during the development of your project or take-home written assignment, e.g.:

  • As part of the initial idea generation and conceptualisation (e.g. suggesting an overall structure or elaborating on concepts and ideas)
  • For source- and literature search, in the same way you would use a search engine like Google
  • For some of the clarifications you would typically discuss with your supervisor, fellow students, or others


As long as you follow UCPH's rules for the use of generative AI, you may use whichever generative AI tool or programme you prefer. Ask your supervisor or course coordinator if you are in doubt.

If you use generative AI, you must follow the principles of good scientific practice.

Read about good scientific practice


Be cautious about what you share with generative AI

Before you start using generative AI, remember the rules on copyright and GDPR.

When using generative AI, you are not allowed to share

  • copyrighted data – e.g., electronic books, articles, and images
  • confidential data or information, e.g. laboratory- and experimental results
  • personally identifiable information, such as personal ID numbers, phone numbers, voice recordings, etc.
