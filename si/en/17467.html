What should I do about my courses, projects and exams?

If you are absent from your studies due to illness without taking leave of absence, the first thing you need to do is

  • withdraw from the courses, projects and/or exams that you are unable to take
  • do not register for new courses in the blocks where you are ill.

Depending on when you find out that you have to withdraw from courses, projects and/or exams, it may be necessary to apply for a dispensation.


Withdraw from courses, projects and exams before the deadline

You can withdraw from courses and exams via Self Service.
NOTE! In the case of your Bachelor's project, you must apply to be withdrawn if you want to start a new project.
See the deadlines for withdrawing from courses and projects

See the deadlines for withdrawing from exams


Apply for withdrawal from courses and projects after the deadline

If the deadline for withdrawing has passed, you must apply for a dispensation to be withdrawn from courses and projects.

If you have previously used an exam attempt for a course/project, it is very difficult to be withdrawn from the course/project after the deadline. You can still apply to be withdrawn from your exam.

Read about the rules on withdrawing from courses


Withdraw from or submit notification of illness for an exam after the deadline

If the deadline has passed, you must either apply for a dispensation to be withdrawn from the exam or submit a notification of illness. In this way, you may be able to avoid using an exam attempt.

You must submit a notification of illness if you fall ill for an exam

  • immediately before the day of the exam (about a week before)
  • on the actual day of the exam
  • during a written take-home assignment
  • during a course with ongoing tests, preventing you from passing the course
  • during a course with specific examination prerequisites, preventing you from being allowed to take the exam.


Apply for a dispensation to be withdrawn from the exam if there is still some time before your exam (about a couple of weeks) and your course concludes with a 'regular' exam (without specific examination prerequisites or ongoing tests).
