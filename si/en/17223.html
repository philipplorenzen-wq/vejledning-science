Meaning and motivation are inextricably linked. If you do not feel your studies are meaningful and cannot see the relevance of what you are learning, it is very difficult to drag yourself to the reading room.
You experience meaning when: 

  • your studies are meaningful and rewarding for you, e.g. because you are extremely interested in your field of study or because you see your studies or a specific course as key to the career you want to pursue
  • you can see why something is important and relevant – even if it may not be what you are most interested in
  • you can see a common thread in what you are doing – the link between and the progression of your courses and your study programme as a whole, or how you can use it in your future working life.

Ways to cultivate and rediscover meaning

4 tips on how to work with meaning in your studies:

  1. Remind yourself why you started your programme.
  2. Look into what others have used the programme for. For example, you can look for graduates with your background on LinkedIn. Go to the University of Copenhagen’s LinkedIn page, select ‘alumni’ and search for your programme
  3. Look ahead at the coming semesters/blocks. Are there specific courses you are looking forward to doing, or projects or electives where you can delve more into your specific interests? Get an overview of your course of study under Planning your studies > Programme structure
  4. Ask your lecturer where you can use the methods you are learning on the course.
