An employer spends between 30-40 seconds on your CV, so you need to make it as easy as possible for your reader to quickly get an impression of who you are and what you can do.

7 tips for sharpening your CV

  1. Always format it in reverse chronological order, starting with the most recent education, work experience, etc.
  2. Choose a clear layout and use headings, bullet points, and short paragraphs to make it easier for the reader to get a quick overview of your CV.
  3. Structure the years in your CV by placing them under each other in the same column. Optionally, write how many months each activity lasted – for example, 2020, 6 months.
  4. Stick to a maximum of 2 pages, so it is quick to skim through – this should be enough for students and recent graduates.
  5. Use the same graphic expression (e.g., with font type and colour palette) in both your cover letter and your CV. It provides a coherent and professional expression.
  6. Always write your CV in the same language as the job advertisement if you are applying for a posted position.
  7. Convert your CV to a PDF file before sending it.


Many ways to create a CV

The layout and format of a CV can vary a lot. You can find many good, free CV templates on the internet – for example, on your union and/or unemployment fund's website.
