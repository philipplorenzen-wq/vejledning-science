If you collect personal data yourself from test persons or in questionnaires for use in your assignment, project or master's thesis, you are responsible for the collected data, which means that you are responsible for:

  • obtaining consent from the participating persons
  • to ensure that the participating persons can enjoy their rights as data subjects,
  • storing their data in a secure manner,
  • to describe the purpose of the collection and processing in a record.

You must also describe the purpose of your processing of personal data and what data you collect, in what's called a "record of processing activities".

Open the record of processing activities

If you need to collect personal data from municipalities, hospitals or companies etc. you must follow the rules on data protection.

You are entitled to have access to the personal data if the data subjects have given their consent to your use of the data.

You are also entitled to access to data without the consent from the data subjects if your supervisor confirms that you need the data for scientific research activities. You find a form for confirmation from your supervisor below.
