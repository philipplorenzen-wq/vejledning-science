A weekly schedule gives you a more detailed overview of the weeks that make up the block/semester. 

The aim of the plan is to have a better overview and more energy – not a guilty conscience. Therefore, be as realistic as possible and leave room for both study and leisure time.

Download a weekly schedule template


5 steps towards a good and varied weekly schedule:

  1. Things you have to do:
    Start by entering all the things you have to do during the week, for example: classes, student job, regular leisure activities, medical appointments, picking up children, study group meetings etc. .
  2. OVERVIEW I:
    See how much time you have left during the week – on weekdays, at the weekend and in the evenings. 
  3. Allocate time for preparation:
    Enter the times during the week when you want to prepare for your courses. Decide how much time to allocate based on your daily routine. For a full-time study programme, the prescribed study time is 40-45 hours per week (including preparation and classes), but this may not be realistic for you every week. 
  4. OVERVIEW II:
    How much time do you have left? Are all your weekday hours and many of your evenings already used up, or is there still room to relax and take some time off from your studies? 

  5. Things you want to do/need:
    Now consider which activities that are not compulsory or regular you want to allocate time to, for example, hanging out in the Friday bar, catching up with family, going for a run or relaxing at home. 

    If there is no room for leisure time, you should consider whether you can cut down on something else. Because it is important – also for your academic progress – that you recharge sometimes. 

Check if you planned your week well by asking yourself: 

  • Is there sufficient variation on a daily basis, for example between activities requiring a lot of concentration and those that need less? 
  • How many hours have you allocated to your studies in total? Is this realistic in relation to your daily routine? 
  • Are there enough gaps between the various elements for you to get mentally ready for the next activity? 
  • Do you have time to relax and do things that give you energy during the week? 
  • Is there some flexibility to handle unforeseen events? 
  • Is there anything you can and have to cut down on? 


If you decide that your week should be different, you can go back and adjust the schedule. 

It is a good idea to regularly adjust your weekly schedule. Over time, you will get a better sense of how much time the various activities need.
