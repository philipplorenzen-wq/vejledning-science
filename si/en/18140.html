On some courses, your teachers will divide you into groups using My Study Group – a digital tool for forming groups in Absalon. 

My Study Group divides you and your fellow students into groups based on a few simple principles derived from research into constructive group work. 


How My Study Group works 

My Study Group assigns you to groups based on your answers to 3 primarily practical questions. 

Research shows that: 

  • a key factor for successful group work is that practical and logistical elements fall into place, e.g. that group members are able to meet at the same times 
  • it is good for the group to have different types of people in it. 

When everyone has answered the questions, the algorithm divides you into groups based on your responses. You will be notified directly in Absalon about who is in your group. 

You can ask for more group members via My Study Group, for example if a member has left the group. 

In My Study Group you can also find the group work tools described on this page if you need them.
