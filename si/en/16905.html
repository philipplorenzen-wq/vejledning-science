Project in Practice

A Project in Practice (PIP) counts as restricted elective or elective and is a chance to get insight in a company or an organization and to build a professional network. You are connected to the company like a regular employee, but you are not paid. 

You cannot do a PiP with a researcher at the university. In a PiP the learning goals are partly academic, partly about using your academic and personal competencies in a work-like situation. Because hosting a student for 9 weeks full time is a commitment for the companies it is not always easy to get it arranged, so start early. 

It is your own responsibility to find a host company or organization, but consult teachers in the relevant area or me to get ideas and contacts.

Get inspiration for your Project in Practice at the SPOT ON events.

Project outside course scope

A Project outside the course scope (POCS) is an elective project and is different from PiP. Here you work independently with a specific topic, either alone or with other students. 

You can do a POCS at the university, working with a researcher or a lecturer, or you can do it with a company. If the project topic is within the core academic area of your programme, you can apply to get it approved as a restricted elective activity.


How to get started with the Master's Thesis

The Master's thesis is a compulsory part of your programme. Start early, but no stress. Keep your mind open, when finding a topic and a supervisor. 

When you hear teachers talk about their research projects in class, think about the option of working with them in your thesis. 

When you visit companies or organizations on excursions, consider whether some of the topics raised could be a thesis topic, and test it on the spot with your hosts. 

Choosing a topic and choosing a supervisor are both important, and it is OK to “shop around" among potential topics and supervisors as long as you are clear about it. Researchers at SCIENCE are used to students asking for a brief discussion about a possible thesis, and they are generally helpful.
