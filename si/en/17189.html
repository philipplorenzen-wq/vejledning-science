Remind yourself that it is quite normal and natural to feel nervous about a job interview. It is a physical sign that you are ready to perform in an important situation.

4 tips on how to deal with your nervousness

#1 Preparation is your friend!

Make sure that you are well prepared. Find specific tips under 'How can I prepare?'.

You can also prepare mentally: What do you normally do if you feel nervous about a particular situation? And may you be able to use some of the same tricks?

#2 Practise, practise, practise

Practise your presentation in front of a friend or your family and tell them to ask you some standard interview questions.

#3 Accept your nervousness and do exercises to cope with it 

It's completely okay and completely normal and a sign that you have something at stake when you get nervous about a job interview. The more you accept your nervousness, the less it will occupy you.

Find simple exercises and techniques to reduce your nervousness under Improve your exam techniques

#4 Say it out loud

Saying it out loud may help alleviate your nervousness. You could e.g. express it like this:
“I'm very nervous because I really want this."
People generally understand and respect that.
