If you are going on a study abroad stay at a university that does not use ECTS points, you can see the amounts of the ECTS points of the courses you have to take on the charts below.

The conversion of the points apply to students who go on a study abroad in the academic year 2023-2024 or later.

If your host university does not appear on the list, it's your own responsibility to find out how to convert the foreign credits to ECTS points. 
Read more Pre-approval of courses

If you are going on exchange in one of KU's collaboration agreements, you must take 30 ECTS during your stay. Read more about it on the page 'How many ECTS will I take during my stay' under Applying for an exchange place.

Please be aware that:

The course level determines the credit conversion: 

The table shows how many local credits you need to take at the undergraduate and graduate levels. It is the level of the course that determines the conversion – not your status as an undergraduate or graduate student at the University of Copenhagen.

For example, graduate students may sometimes take undergraduate courses abroad. In such cases, the conversion must be based on the course level, not your own study level.

When converting, always ensure that the workload is assessed according to the specific course level.

If you have any questions or uncertainties, it is recommended to contact the International Education Office for further guidance.

The conversion table does not apply to mandatory stays (HUM) or clinical stays (SUND).
