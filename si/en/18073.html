Exam nervousness is completely natural. Some degree of nervousness can actually boost your performance. If your nervousness takes too much of your energy up to and during the exam, you can practise how to handle and accommodate it.

Here are 3 exercises that can help you relax and reduce your nervousness.


Exercise #1: Listen to your surroundings

It can be difficult to focus on anything other than your nervousness before the exam. This distraction exercise can help you think of something else.


What to do:

  1. Close your eyes and sit comfortably.
  2. Focus on all the sounds around you.
  3. Concentrate on distinguishing between the sounds.
  4. Say, inside your head, where each sound comes from.


Exercise #2: Breathe

Your breathing plays a big role in your well-being. By doing breathing exercises, your body becomes calmer, which has a positive effect on your nervousness.

What to do:

  1. Breathe in deeply. You can put a hand on your stomach to feel that you're breathing deeply.
  2. Inhale and hold your breath as you count to 10.
  3. When you have counted to 10, exhale while saying the word 'calm' inside your head.
  4. Do it for a few minutes.


Exercise #3: Say stop

When you are nervous, your thoughts can start spiralling, so focusing and performing at the exam can be difficult. You can practise saying STOP to your thoughts.

What to do:

  1. Say stop – either inside your head or aloud if you can.
  2. Breathe in deeply.
  3. Observe your surroundings; where are you, what is the weather like, etc.
