Master's thesis

There are some general rules about when you can start the Master's thesis and how it is placed. The thesis is placed in the 2nd year of the programme, and you normally start your thesis when you have completed the course work.

The MSc thesis can be 45 ECTS or 60 ECTS. Some students find it useful to start early on the thesis work by doing a  thesis preparation project (PREP) before starting on the thesis itself. Other students plan to start the thesis in their 2nd year after completing the course work and building up their knowledge.

The preparation projects can consist of one or two projects, totaling 15 ECTS, and are usually scheduled from Block 3 of the first year onwards. The options depend on the type of project and the research groups. Ask UCPH Study Guidance and your supervisor what is possible, if you are in doubt.

Supervisor and contract

You are responsible for finding your own Master's thesis supervisor. I recommend that you contact the research groups or potential supervisors directly and ask for possible thesis projects within their research area. In the autumn, the Niels Bohr Institute arrange the event “find your supervisor", where you can meet potential supervisors from the research groups at NBI.

You must make a thesis contract before you start the thesis. Questions can be directed to eksamen-noerre@adm.ku.dk.

You can also do your thesis in collaboration with a company or an external institution. Please note, that you must have an internal supervisor at SCIENCE as well. Read more below.

Read more about the time to start the thesis, who can be your supervisor, the thesis contract, and other relevant information about the Master's thesis.

Project outside the course scope, thesis preparation project or project in practice

If you wish to do an independent project instead of taking a course, you can do one or two projects up to a total of 15 ECTS. This is called a Project outside the course scope.

Remember to make a contract with your supervisor and include a proper description of the learning goals of the project. Find more info on how to draw up the contract.

Also read the specific guidelines for project descriptions from the Niels Bohr Institute .

You can make a project outside the course scope before you start your thesis. This provides an opportunity to start working with your supervisor or learn about a specific research field.

Read more about Project outside the course scope.

A project in practice is a project you do in collaboration with a company, and is 15 ECTS. Please note that you must have an internal supervisor at SCIENCE as well. You can visit the NBI webpage  to learn about business collaborations. Read more about Project in Practice.

A Thesis Preparation Project (PREP) is usually supervised by the same person as your thesis. It gives you the opportunity to start working on experiments, learning to use equipment or software, and so on, before you start working on your thesis. Read more about Thesis Preparation Project (PREP).

Projects with a company or an external partner

It is possible to do projects with a company or with an external institution, both for the MSc thesis and for projects outside the course scope.

The opportunities depend on the research field and the specialization. In some research areas there are many business collaborations with companies, e.g. quantum technology, seismic surveying, AI and Machine Learning. There are well-established collaborations with public institutions in other research areas such as e.g. Rigshospitalet, The Danish Meteorological Institute, The Geological Survey or the Health Authorities.

You can learn more about these opportunities from various sources:

  • You can visit the NBI webpage  to learn about business collaborations, and you are welcome to contact the business coordinator Albert Schliesser  directly.
  • You can participate in career events and talk to external partners about possible projects with them.
  • You can ask the academic coordinator of your specialisati on or potential supervisors for opportunities.

It is also possible to pursue projects yourself but notice that you need to have an internal supervisor at SCIENCE.
