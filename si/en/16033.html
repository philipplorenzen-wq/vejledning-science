What can I apply for a dispensation for?

You can apply for a dispensation for

  1. special conditions that will make it possible for you to participate in an exam
  2. change of exam type, e.g. from written to oral exam. 
  3. extended time for your Master's thesis.


1. Special exam conditions can take many forms

UCPH tries to meet you and your fellow students' needs. Therefore, there is no complete list of what special conditions could be.


Examples of special conditions at the exam:

  • Permission to use special aids for a written or oral exam, e.g. aids you have received through the SPS Counselling.
    Read more about support and aids through SPS
  • Prolonged duration of examination time or preparation time.
  • Permission to bring your own computer to written on-site exams.
  • Electronic assignment delivery and submission for written on-site exams.
  • Sitting in a room with few students or in a private room for written on-site exams.
  • Permission to bring an interpreter or similar.
  • Extended time for your Master’s thesis.


2. Changing the exam type may be an option for your course

You can apply to change the type of your exam, e.g., from written to oral.

It is only possible to change the exam type if

  • you have a good reason why it is necessary for you
  • it is necessary to equalize you with other students
  • it does not change the academic level of the exam
  • it is practically possible.


You must apply to change the exam type for your specific exam – not for, e.g., all written exams at once.

The study board will assess from your application whether you are allowed to take the exam as another exam type.


3. Extended time for your Master's thesis

If you know in advance that you need extended time for your Master's thesis, you can apply for it. 

If you already have a general dispensation for special exam conditions, you may also have received extended time for your Master's thesis. Check your decision, which you received on your KUmail.
