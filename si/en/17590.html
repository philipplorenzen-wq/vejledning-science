If you have a clear idea of a career path, your time as a student is a good opportunity to try it out and learn more about it. What you can do:

  • Contact people who work in areas you would like to work in and ask them how they got there. Use LinkedIn to find interesting people
  • Collaborate on a project with a company. This could be a project in practice or a thesis collaboration. You might be able to gain good, relevant contacts in your network that you can draw on when you look for jobs.
    Learn about the possible project types
  • Find out what competencies your chosen career path requires and plan how to acquire them during your programme.
    Learn how to identify your existing competences

Remember to regularly take stock of what you learn from the things you try out. You may well discover that the path you thought was yours is not right for you after all. But that can be a very important experience in itself.
