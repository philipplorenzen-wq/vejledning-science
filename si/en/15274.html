If, as a student, you are attached to a research project in order to perform tasks for a researcher, you must enter into a guest agreement with the Department at which the researcher is employed.

If your work on the project leads to results that it is important for the project to be able to use – including inventions - your rights will belong to the university according the agreement. You will have the same rights as an employee in relation to copyright and patents if you make an invention.

If you agree with the researcher on amendments to the template for guest agreement in respect to intellectual property rights, the researcher must ensure that the agreement is signed by the University of Copenhagen's Tech Transfer office.

If, as a student, you are attached to a research project in order to perform tasks for a researcher, and if the tasks involve the handling of personal data, you must enter into a guest agreement including a data processing agreement. 

It is not necessary to enter into a data processing agreement if you only get to view personal data and you do not have to work with them. If you receive personal data that you are responsible for storing yourself, you must store data in your account on OneDrive.
