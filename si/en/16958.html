How do I apply for a dispensation from the study activity requirement?

You can apply for a dispensation if you can document that you have been unable to meet the study activity requirement due to exceptional circumstances.


Exceptional circumstances include, for example:

  • being or having been seriously ill
  • having a functional impairment
  • serious illness or death in your immediate family.

Circumstances to do with your student job, housing situation or finances are not regarded as exceptional circumstances, but may be worth mentioning in your application.


Apply for what you can document a need for now

You should initially apply for what is necessary in your current situation and what you can document a need for here and now.

You can apply again later if you need further dispensations and can document this.
The Study Board will not be able to process your application before you are able to document that you need all of the dispensation you're applying for. You may reapply for a dispensation once you meet the criteria.


How to apply for a dispensation

4 steps when you apply for a dispensation:

  1. Get documentation of your situation, e.g. maternity records/birth certificate/adoption documents, a medical certificate from your doctor or an opinion from a psychologist. In some cases, documentation can also be statements from your lecturer, bachelor supervisor, head of studies, etc.
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Write a statement, which contains a description of the exceptional circumstances due to which you need a dispensation.
  3. Create a study plan for your entire course of study, which shows how you intend to complete your study programme. Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure.       
  4. Fill out an application by using 'Apply for a dispensation' and enclose your documentation, written statement, and study plan.

Apply for a dispensation

As a general rule, you will receive a reply within 6 weeks of the Study Board having received all relevant information about your application.
How the Study Board will process your application


Ask your study and career guidance counsellor

If you need advice on your application for a dispensation, contact a study and career guidance counsellor from UCPH Study Guidance.
Unfortunately, your study and career guidance counsellor cannot read through your application for you.
