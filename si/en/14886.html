What happens if I do not meet the SU activity requirements?

The SU activity controls are conducted twice a year in March and September. If you do not meet the SU activity requirement at the time of the controls, you will receive a letter in your e-Boks at the end of March/September or the beginning of April/October stating that you will be declared inactive as to receiving SU and that your SU will be terminated. 

If you are declared inactive in the spring, your SU will be terminated from May onwards, and if you are declared inactive in autumn, your SU will be terminated from November onwards.

In those months where you are declared inactive, you are not entitled to receive SU, state education loans, completion loans, disability supplements or provider supplements. 

You can reapply for SU from the month in which you are no longer delayed in your studies. Simply put, when you are delayed by a maximum of 30 ECTS.

Remember that you cannot apply for grant portions for ceased months.

If your delay is due to illness, other special conditions or council work, you can apply for additional grant portions. Read more about additional grant portions here.
