Gælder for målgrupperne: Environmental Science - Kandidat

The MSc programme in Environmental Science is cross disciplinary and attract students from a range of different disciplines from chemistry to biology and a variety of Environmental Science BSc-programs. This is a strength, as you all join with different competences.

Getting up to speed on knowledge gaps

Coming from different disciplines is also a challenge, as you will not all be at the same level, when it comes to hard core knowledge within math, chemistry, physics, soil-, water- and environmental chemistry, microbiology, organism physiology and ecosystem functions. We therefore expect all of you to read up on the knowledge gaps you might have, and to share your knowledge and help fellow students that might find particular disciplines difficult.

Work together and help each other

If you work together and help each other, our experience is that you all reach a high common level of knowledge within the first year - and at the same time create strong bonds to your fellow students (and future colleges!) from all over the world.

Working in groups is a large part of the programme. Improving your teamwork skills during your studies is essential for success and for your later career. Almost all of you will be working as part of teams when you get employed in a public or private enterprise. 

Talk to your teachers

Student-teacher relationships are informal in Denmark, so don't be afraid to call your professors by their first names or to participate in class discussions. In fact, we expect you to!

Preparation and relationships are important for successful studies

Being prepared and showing up in class is the best thing you can do to be part of the group and develop close relationships with your co-students and devoted teachers. Sometimes those relationships are what keeps you hanging on through difficult times with lots of work. Your teachers will always be ready to help, if you need advice on topics you find difficult.

Your evaluations helps us improve

If there are things, you like or things you think could be done better in the courses, please let us know by filling in the course evaluations. We discuss them every year in the Departments where each course belongs and adjust the courses accordingly. If you have more general issues concerning the course content, you are always welcome to contact me. Teachers appreciate feedback on course contents, teaching and we stimulate interactive teaching forms. 

Go on excursions and participate in extra-curriculum activities

Going on excursions (i.e. visiting companies, public institutions or places relevant to the programme) is a nice, informal way to learn and to get to know your fellow students. I encourage you to arrange trips together with fellow students and faculty. There are a larger number of institutions and companies in the Øresund Region that deal with a diversity of questions and challenges within Environmental Science. Also the European Environment Agency is located in Copenhagen. 

You also have the possibility to join career events for students and companies. 

Meeting me, older students and yearly events

You will meet me during the welcome week. In addition, I arrange informal meetings in blocks 1, 3 and 4 where you can meet across specializations and across years. At these meetings, you will also meet alums, who will tell you about their study, career choices and professional life. Usually, the meeting in block 1 will include a session with MSc-thesis project pitches from supervisors presenting different topics, and you will get a chance to discuss elective and restrictive elective courses. At the meeting in block 4, we will evaluate how things have been going and we will prepare to meet the new group of students in August/September.

I hope you will enjoy studying Environmental Science at UCPH!
