For the specialisation in Chemistry, Toxicology and Health, all of the first year has compulsory courses, and thus traveling abroad during the first year is difficult. For the specialisation in Soil and Water, it is possible. If you are in the Chemistry, Toxicology and Health specialisation - you can go abroad during the first half of the second year. If you choose to do so, your Master's thesis will be 30 ECTS credits.

If you wish to take courses at another university in Denmark or outside Denmark, I therefore advise you to plan well in advance. Summer courses are also an option. 

Working on your thesis abroad

Alternatively, you can do all or parts of your thesis at another university, research institution or company. We have had students doing fieldwork in the USA, Canada, Costa Rica, Greenland, the UK, Australia, Switzerland, Svalbard and other locations. You have to also include a co-supervisor from the University of Copenhagen to ensure alignment with the study programme and to keep contact with the network. 

The key to arranging a thesis abroad is to be out in good time and find supervisors with good collaborations. Alternatively, you might have a contact in another research institution and make that connection yourself. The November meeting concerning elective courses and thesis opportunities is a good forum to discuss potential ideas for studying abroad.
