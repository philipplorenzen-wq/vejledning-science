There are two stays abroad on the Global Environment and Development programme:

There is a field course in the first year, with a 2-3 week field stay in a rural area, typically in either Africa, Asia, or Europe. It is fantastic. You get to work in the field, problem-based, and with local university partners. The learning curve is amazing; just look forward to that experience.

The second field stay is in connection to your thesis. You will spend quite some time collecting your own empirical data. It does not have to be in a village somewhere, you can spend your time in an air-conditioned FAO or Embassy office in Jakarta if you wish, or you can go horseback riding to your experimental fields in the mountains of Lesotho. Or something in between.

Do try to pursue fieldwork as part of your thesis. There are so many things you cannot learn in the classroom, and your future employer will look at your CV and ask: Are you field-wise? This is the way to demonstrate that you have the experience the employer is looking for.
