By preparing carefully for the interview, you show your potential employer that you are thorough and serious. 

7 ways you can prepare for a job interview

#1 Practise giving a presentation of yourself

What are the most important points about yourself that you would like to communicate to the employer? Write down keywords about yourself and your experience and competences based on what the workplace is looking for. This gives you a sense of security and an overview if you feel a bit nervous about the interview. Practise giving the presentation.

#2 Practise answering typical questions

Certain questions are often asked during a job interview. It is a good idea to prepare for answering these questions. Try saying your answers out loud to yourself or do a trial interview with a friend. Having to say your answers out loud is completely different to thinking about them.
Typical questions you can prepare:

  • Would you like to tell us a bit about yourself?
  • Why have you applied for the job?
  • How do you work with others?
  • How would you solve X task? 
  • Describe your strengths and weaknesses?
  • In your application, you talk about having such and such a competence. Can you give some specific examples of this?
  • What are your long-term career aspirations?

#3 Prepare questions you can ask the employer

Take time to think about what you would like to know about the workplace and tasks. This is an opportunity for you to learn about the job. Asking questions also signals that you are interested and curious. Examples of relevant questions:

  • Which of the tasks mentioned in the job advertisement will I spend most of my time on?
  • What will it take for me to do well in this job? 
  • How is the work organised: Will I be working in a team or more independently?
  • What are the challenges in this position or workplace?
  • Who will be my manager and how will he/she manage me?  
  • What is a good employee and colleague to them?

#4 Check up on the workplace before the interview

Is there any news on its website? Is it possible to find information about the workplace on other news websites or in specialised magazines? Make sure that you are up to date so that you can demonstrate at the interview that you have prepared for the interview and that you take a real interest in the workplace.

#5 Check route and transport time to the workplace

Find out in advance how to get to the workplace and how long it takes. Make sure to leave plenty of time to get there to avoid surprises. It is good manners to arrive approx. 10 minutes early.

Remember that the job interview starts the moment you walk through the door. If you show up too early, they will need to decide what to do with you, and if you arrive at the last minute, it may give a bad impression.

#6 Consider what to wear

You should dress comfortably and in a style that more or less matches the workplace culture. If you are in any doubt, it is better to dress up slightly and conservatively than to wear something a bit too casual.

#7 What you need to bring to your job interview

You need to bring  

  • the job advertisement
  • your CV
  • your questions and possibly keywords and points
  • a notepad and a pen
  • References and/or a list of previous employers that they may contact. This is relevant if you have had previous jobs, worked on projects and/or done volunteer work. You can hand these things over after an interview.
