There are many different ways of approaching your studies and defining your study programme. It is important to realize that you are responsible for how, and to a large extent, what you are studying.

I recommend that you choose courses based on especially your interests, perhaps partly considering your strengths, but less so based on what you imagine a prospective employer may care about (note that there is no actual unemployment amongst graduates from the Agricultural Economics programme at KU/UCPH.

Some specific suggestions for how to become a good student on the MSc programme in Agricultural Economics:

  • Your studies are your (main) job, so you should expect to spend on average of 35-45 hours per week on your studies. With that said, having an interesting and relevant study job is not necessarily a bad idea, as it strengthens your CV and prepares you for subsequent employment. Make sure you organize your time so you can fit everything in, including preparation for lectures, extracurricular activities etc. You can read more about study techniques and about planning your time here. 
  • It is generally a good idea to work with others, so consider forming study groups. Don't be afraid to ask your fellow students for help and also try to help others when possible and relevant. You often learn the material better when you have a chance to discuss it with others, and you learn from trying to explain things to others.
  • Avoid plagiarism and other forms of cheating on assignments! Being caught in any form of academic misconduct can have serious consequences for you, including a ban from the university, with no study grants during that period.
    If you come from a different academic system than the Danish, be aware that our interpretation of the rules may be very different from what you are used to. Note that ignorance of the rules is not a valid defense. So make sure you familiarize yourself with the rules regarding exam cheating and our recommendations on  good academic practice.
    If you have any doubts about what the correct approach is to, for example, referencing, talk to your lecturers or me.
  • Please remember to evaluate all your courses after each block. This is the way students can give feedback to the lecturers and the department, and the only way we know how things work at your end and thereby improve our teaching.
  • Attend and be actively involved in different activities at UCPH (often referred to as KU), SCIENCE or IFRO, e.g. study events, various communities or seminars, for example at IFRO.
  • Read your study messages and information about study events to update yourself on important messages and events.  
  • Expect to be busy. Learn how to recharge and relax in order to avoid stress. Read more about positive and negative stress
