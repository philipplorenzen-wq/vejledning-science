When can I start my parental leave and how much leave am I entitled to?

 If you are the parent giving birth:

  • you are entitled to 12 months of maternity leave on the degree programme you are enrolled on when you become a parent. If you start your maternity leave or if you give birth during a semester or a block, you can take maternity leave for the rest of that semester/block + 12 months.
  • you must have given notice of or started your maternity leave before the end of your maximum study completion time. You can start your maternity leave at the earliest when you have been given a maternity record (vandrejournal).

If you are the parent not giving birth:

  • you are entitled to 12 months of leave on the degree programme you are enrolled on when you become a parent. If you become a parent during a semester or a block, you can take leave for the rest of that semester/block + 12 months.
  • you can give notice of taking leave or start your period of leave only when the child is born or adopted.
  • you must have given notice of or started your leave before the end of your maximum study completion time.

If both parents are students at UCPH, you can each take 12 months of maternity/paternity leave.

Do you need help to plan your maternity/paternity leave? 

  • If you are unsure about how to plan your maternity/paternity leave, you can always talk UCPH Study Guidance about making a study plan.
  • If you are unsure about how to plan your SU grant portions and SU birth support, you can contact the SU office.
