All students going on Erasmus exchange in the autumn 2024 and onwards will receive a Travel Grant.

The Travel Grant is a fixed amount given as a constribution for travel expences when travelling to and from your host university (round-trip).

If you choose to travel sustaianble to and from your host university, you will recieve a higher Travel Grant.

Please note: If your Erasmus exchange is outside EU/Europe, you might not recieve the Travel Grant due to budget rules in the Erasmus programme.

Application
In the semester before going on Erasmus exchange, you must inform UCPH, if you want to receive the Travel Grant for sustainable travel or non-sustainable travel.

UCPH will send you a KUmail where we ask you to tick off in the application module (Mobility-Online), which Travel Grant you want to recieve.

Requriements for the grant:

  • You must travel sustainable to and from your host university at the start and end of your exchange
  • You must upload documentation for the sustainable travel (e.g., train ticket with your full name and dates for the travel) in the application module (Mobility-Online), when evaluating your Erasmus grant at the end of your exchange

If you end up travelling non-sustainble, we will substract the extra Travel Grant from the ordinary Erasmus Travel grant, when we approve the evaluation of your Erasmus grant, when you have completed your exchange.

Definition for sustainable travel: Sustainable travel is defined as follows by the EU Commission: “Sustainable means of transport (green travel) is defined as the travel that uses low-emissions means of transport for the main part of the travel, such as bus, train or car-pooling". This means that travel by plane and ferry* is not considered sustainable. 

* If travelling by boat or ferry is necceary on a short leg of the journey, you can still receive the grant, e.g. if you travel by bus from Copenhagen to Amsterdam, and the bus goes by ferry between Denmark and Germany.
