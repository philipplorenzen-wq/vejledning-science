The application you send to SU at the University of Copenhagen must contain the following:

1. Receipt from MinSU

  • The receipt page confirming your application for additional clips
  • You must apply for the period in which you were delayed

2.  Personal statement

Describe in your own words how your illness or special circumstances have affected your academic progress

  • Be specific and include:
    • Which courses you have not passed
    • Course names and ECTS credits
    • The semesters involved

3. Documentation

  • For example, a medical certificate or other relevant documentation
  • It is a good idea to show your doctor what information the certificate must include to meet the requirements

Read what medical documentation must contain

Please note! Usually, you cannot use the so-called 'check form' because it does not contain the information needed.

If you have a statement from a psychologist, you must supplement it with a referral from your doctor, as evidence of your illness.

If you have the option to take the re-examination, but are unable to go to the exam, the documentation must also cover the re-examination period.

If your medical condition continues into a new semester, you will need to re-apply for additional grant portions with updated documentation.

Apply for additional grant portions via minSU on www.su.dk.
