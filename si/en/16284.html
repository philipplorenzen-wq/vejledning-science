How can I take a break from my studies within the framework of my study programme?

You can plan a break within the framework of your study programme without this being based on exceptional circumstances (e.g. illness, childbirth or military service). This allows you formally to continue to meet the study activity requirements and maybe receive SU.

In practical terms, you can take a break from your studies within the framework of your study programme by

  1. not registering for courses during the desired period
  2. withdrawing from any courses and exams for which you are registered during the period.


Before taking a break from your studies, you need to find out if, and if so, how long a break you can take within the framework of the course of study of your programme.
If you take a break from your studies, while you still formally have to meet the study activity requirements, the same rules and requirements apply as usual.


Find out when and for how long you can take a break

1) Prepare a study plan covering the whole of your study programme

Find a study plan template in which the recommended structure of your study programme under Programme structure
Fill in the plan with the break that you want to take.

Remember this when preparing your study plan:

  • Include all programme elements (including the ones you have taken).
  • Check that your courses and exams are offered in the block in which you want to take them.
  • If you change the order of your courses, you must make sure that this is possible – does one course require you to take another course first, or does switching the courses around affect your academic progression? Find answers in the course description or ask the course coordinator.
    Find the course description 
  • Comply with the maximum duration of study. You can add another year of study in the digital study plan in the bottom right corner.
    See your maximum duration of study 
  • Make sure to add time in the plan for preparing for any re-exams.

2) Do you still comply with the study activity requirements?

Under each year of study in your study plan, it will be shown how many ECTS credits you have planned.
Check if you still comply with:

  • UCPH's study activity requirements
  • SU study activity requirements

Modify your plan so that you are sure to meet the study activity requirements.


3) You may consult a study and career guidance counsellor about your study plan

If you need advice on your new study plan, you can contact a study and career guidance counsellor from UCPH Study Guidance.
