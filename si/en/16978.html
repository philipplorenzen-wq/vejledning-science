Before your exam, it's a good idea to structure the time you have to study for it.


Get an overview of the following when structuring your time:

  • How much time you have to prepare for the exam
  • What to study before the exam.
  • How to prioritise your studying – meaning 'nice to do' versus 'need to do'.


Make a timetable

To get an overview of how much time you have and what you need to do, you can make a timetable for the days you will be studying for your exam.
Make a realistic timetable
Stick to your timetable as much as you can. That enables you to study as much as you need an to take time off with a clear conscience.
