The Global Environment and Development programme has compulsory courses that all students must complete. In addition, you must complete a number of restrictive elective courses - the list of available restricted elective courses differs between the two second-year specialisations. These available courses allow you to build a unique profile.

To ensure you get the most out of your studies, I'll invite you to a meeting where we – just you and me – discuss what courses are best for you. This meeting will take place in September when you have arrived at the University of Copenhagen. We will discuss your suggestion for courses to be taken during the first year.

And please remember:

  • Compulsory courses mean compulsory! You may get an exemption if you can prove you have exactly those skills, in which case you must apply to the Study Board.

  • Restricted electives must be chosen from the list specified by your chosen specialisation. However, if you find a relevant course not on the list, it may be accepted as a restricted elective. This option is only available to students going to the University of Warsaw in their second year. Check with me and then apply to the Study Board to get it approved. Relevant is defined as relevant for Global Environment and Development.
