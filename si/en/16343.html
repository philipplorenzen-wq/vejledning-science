You must be able to document that extraordinary circumstances are preventing you from submitting your thesis on time in order to apply for a dispensation to postpone the submission.

Apply for the additional time that you and your supervisor (and possibly a doctor) deem to be realistic in your situation. 

See what counts as extraordinary circumstances on this page under 'Can I postpone submitting my thesis report?' 

How to apply for postponement

The more precise and thorough you are in your application, the easier it will be to process your case without additional steps.

6 steps when you apply for postponement (a dispensation):

  1. Get documentation of your situation, e.g. a medical certificate from your doctor or an opinion from a psychologist.
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Get a written statement from your supervisor that describes how far along your Master's thesis is, if progress is being made right now, and how the project will be completed within the additional time that you apply for. Your supervisor must also confirm that he/she is able to continue supervising you until the oral defence.
  3. Write a statement that describes
    - the extraordinary circumstances
    - why these mean that you cannot submit by the deadline 
    - how your situation has changed.
  4. Create a study plan with a complete overview of your entire programme (not necessary, if you only have your Master's thesis left in your programme) which shows how you intend to complete your study programme. Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Planning your studies.
  5. Find out if you need a dispensation from the maximum duration of study, if you get at postponement of the submission of your thesis report, You will be asked whether you also want to apply for this, when filling out the application form.   
    Check out the rules on maximum duration of study
  6. Send an application by clicking 'Apply for a dispensation '. Complete it and enclose your documentation, statements, and study plan.

Apply for a dispensation 

Reply to your application

As a general rule, you will receive a reply within 6 weeks of the Study Board having received all relevant information about your application. If you need to send supplementary information, you will be contacted via your KUmail.
