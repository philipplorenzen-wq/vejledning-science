It is a good idea, because you will have more SU grant portions available.

You have a limited number of SU grant portions for your degree programme. If you experience delays, you risk running out of portions before completing your studies.
If you are granted additional portions, you will have more SU grant portions available. This reduces the risk of losing your SU before finishing your education.

You can see how many portions you have left on MinSU → Se din SU → Klippekort.

Additional grant portions affect the ECTS requirement

Extra grant portions can also impact whether you meet SU's study activity requirements.

Twice a year (in March and September), your study activity is checked. At that point, you may not be more than 30 ECTS behind relative to the number of SU grant portions you have used.

If you are granted additional portions, your total portion consumption is reduced, and therefore your ECTS requirement is often lowered as well. This can be crucial for whether your SU payments continue or stop.

Example:

A student has passed 75 ECTS and received SU for 25 months, resulting in a portion consumption of 25. With 25 used portions, the ECTS requirement is 90 ECTS.
The student therefore does not meet the requirement and risks being declared inactive, which would stop SU payments.

The student then applies for additional portions due to one semester (6 months) of delay and is granted 6 portions. Their portion consumption is therefore reduced from 25 to 19 (25 – 6 = 19).

With a consumption of 19 portions, the ECTS requirement is only 60 ECTS. Since the student has passed 75 ECTS, they now meet the requirement, and SU payments will continue.
