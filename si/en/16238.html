The study programme for the MSc in Agricultural Economics is very flexible.

You only have 2 compulsory courses: Applied Econometrics and Incentives and Regulation for a total of 15 ECTS credits.

Restricted electives courses

At least another 2 courses (15 ECTS credits) have to be chosen amongst the following courses: Industrial Organization, Advanced International Trade, Advanced Development Economics, Microeconomic and Econometric Productions Analysis, Natural Resource Economics and Market Design.

45 ECTS credits have to be taken from the longer list of courses available. See under Programme structure > Choose your year of admission.

For the restricted elective courses, it is possible to apply to take courses elsewhere, for example at the Department of Economics at UCPH (you can find their courses in the UCPH course catalogue, at Copenhagen Business School or other universities.

I generally consider most courses at the Department of Economics to be very relevant for students of Agricultural Economics. You can apply to get such courses approved as restricted elective courses. 

For courses to be accepted as restricted elective courses, an important criterion is the economic and/or analytical content of the course.

Elective courses

Finally you have the option of using 15 ECTS credits on elective courses or projects.

In the course catalogue, you can find all courses offered at UCPH.

Note here, for example, the course “The Food Industry: Structure and Economics" which, albeit not a restricted elective course, may be interesting and relevant for anyone interested in the Danish agri-food industry.

Note that if you take courses outside of the faculty, SCIENCE, the registration deadline, term structure and time table might be very different from the one we use here. Make sure to double check the formalities and practicalities.

Master's thesis

The programme is completed by writing a Master's Thesis.

IFRO organises a Master's Thesis seminar to kick off the thesis writing process. It typically takes place in November.

You are supposed to define your own idea for your thesis, which you can then discuss and formalize in cooperation with prospective supervisors.
