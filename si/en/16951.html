How do I take courses outside SCIENCE?

If you have compulsory or restricted elective courses outside SCIENCE, you register for the courses at self service.

Otherwise, you need to follow this guide to take a course at another faculty or university and make it part of your study programme at SCIENCE.


If you would like to do a course at a foreign university, it may be a good idea to start by applying for an exchange place.

Planning your study abroad


1) Apply for pre-approval at SCIENCE

You must apply in advance for approval of a specific course at another faculty or university. You do so by applying for pre-approval.

A pre-approval from SCIENCE does not ensure that you meet the admission requirements for the course. Check whether you meet the requirements at the faculty or university that offers the course.

How to apply for pre-approval at SCIENCE


2) Apply for a place at the other faculty at UCPH or university

You must comply with the deadlines and procedures applicable at the relevant university/faculty.

  • Courses at UCPH: You do not have to enclose your pre-approval when applying. The other faculty at UCPH will check with SCIENCE whether we have pre-approved the course.
  • Courses on other university: Enclose your pre-approval from SCIENCE when applying. At some universities/faculties, you may enclose a copy or verification of your application for pre-approval until you can send the actual pre-approval.

3) Have credits transferred or notify about failed courses outside UCPH

  • Did you take the course at UCPH? Your ECTS credits will automatically be transferred to your programme. If you fail the course your programme will be notified automatically.
  • Did you take the course on a different university? Whether or not you pass the course, you need to complete and submit a credit transfer application as soon as you have received documentation for the results from the other university.
    Your credit transfer application may be key to ensuring that you meet the study activity requirements for receiving your student grant (SU).
