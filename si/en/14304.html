How will my application be assessed?

When the department assesses the application, the following factors are weighted: 

  1. The purpose of the activity. It should be clear what the purpose of the activity is, and the purpose should be academic, or study related. Dining can support this, but dining as a purpose and social activity is usually not sufficient to obtain support on its own. 
  2. Other support options. An event can be both relevant and worthy of support, but this does not mean that the Department is the most obvious funder. Opportunities for other funding sources (e.g. the SCIENCE pool, GamMa, Haralds Følge, etc.) are included in the processing of an application. 

    Activities included in the study start programs are not subject to funding applications; they are agreed between the study start coordinator and the tutor coordinators. 

  3. Target group and inclusion. As a starting point, the event should be open, inclusive and aimed at broad groups of students at the department. Events can be aimed at students with a special interest if the opportunity for participation is open. Predefined boundaries should not be less than study program or year level. 
  4. Study or student political activities. It is generally not obvious for the Department to provide support for student political activities. However, in special cases, student social and academic activities may be difficult to distinguish clearly from academic or student political activities. In such cases, it will be part of the assessment whether there is a balanced representation of interests and viewpoints in the activity. 
  5. Easy administration. If your activity involves a lot of expenses, reimbursements, invoice payments, etc. there may be a mismatch in the proportions between the activity and the required administrative support. In these cases, we will condition support on a changed setup for purchasing and payment.
