Disagreements are a natural part of a group work, because you often work with people who think and work differently to you. 

First, try to refresh your collaboration agreement, which was based on your various preferences and aims for the group.

Remember that: 

  • you are not competitors – you are there to support and strengthen each other 
  • give each other space to make mistakes and for different approaches 
  • your differences also mean that you can supplement and challenge each other positively.

Work out what your disagreement is about 

Disagreements can be an opportunity to evaluate your collaboration and work out what the real root of the conflict is. This will give you a clear picture of: 

  • what you disagree on 
  • what is actually going well.

If you are unable to agree 

It is a good idea to get an outsider to look at your situation and collaboration if you cannot resolve the disagreement yourselves. For example, you can seek help from your: 

  • study and career guidance counsellors, who can help you to handle and work with your challenges and collaboration
    Find your study and career guidance counsellors 
  • teachers, who can help you if you need to be assigned new groups.
