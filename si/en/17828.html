How do I apply for a dispensation for late course withdrawal (with a used exam attempt)?

If you have already used 1 or more exam attempts for the course, it is very difficult to withdraw. This is only possible in highly exceptional circumstances, and you must submit a very detailed application.


You can apply for a dispensation on the grounds of e.g.

  • illness or other exceptional circumstances over an extended period that prevents you from completing the course.

The exceptional circumstance must be directly connected to the course and prevent you from completing it, also at a later time.

Extension of studies and changed academic interests will not be included in the assessment of whether there are exceptional circumstances.

If you withdraw from the course, you will no longer be bound by your course registration – unless it is a compulsory course.


Maybe you can withdraw from the exam instead

If you are not granted a dispensation to withdraw from the course, the Study Board will decide whether you can instead be allowed to withdraw from the exam to avoid using an exam attempt.

If you are withdrawn from the exam, you are still bound by your course registration, and you must therefore pass the course during your study programme.


How to apply for a dispensation when you have used exam attempts

4 steps when you apply for a dispensation:

  1. Get documentation of your situation, e.g. maternity records/birth certificate/adoption documents, a medical certificate from your doctor or an opinion from a psychologist. In some cases, documentation can also be statements from your lecturer, supervisor, head of studies, etc. 
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Write a statement, which contains a description of the exceptional circumstances due to which you need a dispensation.
  3. Create a study plan for your entire course of study if you withdraw from the course. Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure.
  4. Fill out an application by using 'Apply for a dispensation' and enclose documentation, written statement, and study plan.

Reply to your application

Unfortunately, at the moment the processing time can be a bit longer than the usual 6 weeks for this type of application.

If you need to send supplementary material, you will be contacted via your KUmail.

How the Study Board will process your application


Ask your study and career guidance counsellor

If you need advice on your application for an dispensation , contact a study and career guidance counsellor from UCPH Study Guidance.

Unfortunately, your study and career guidance counsellor cannot read through your application for you.
