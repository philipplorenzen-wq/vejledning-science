Background

This study track covers a broad range of courses related to machine learning and language technology including deep learning, advanced courses in natural language processing, and information retrieval. The language technologies study track equips students with a diverse skillset, combining fundamental and applied courses, as well as a combination of theoretical and practical skills. Graduates will have a strong background in machine-learning approaches to natural language understanding and processing, giving them an excellent platform for pursuing a career in industry or academia.

Recommended prerequisites

We recommend a BSc in Computer Science, Machine Learning or Data Science.

Career opportunities

Natural language processing is often referred to as one of the three core pillars of Artificial Intelligence. Demand currently strongly outweighs supply when it comes to trained professionals with skills in language technologies – both in Denmark and internationally.

Sectors employing graduates with language technology skills including core technology companies developing, among others, search engines, recommender systems, chatbots or content moderation platforms; but also, companies or the public sector seeking to digitalize their internal processes or looking for automated decision support methods. In addition, language technology is an active and rapidly growing area of research, giving MSc graduates the opportunity to pursue a Ph.D.
