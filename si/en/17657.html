You can draw energy, a sense of security, support and joy from the communities you become part of as a student. Whether they are small or large – and in your year group or in the broader study environment.

For many students, being part of one or more communities is essential to their sense of belonging on their study programme.

Examples of rewarding communities with other people on the programme:

  • being part of the community in your year group, study group or class, or joining an association on campus 
  • having academic and/or social relations with your fellow students and teachers. 

5 ways to cultivate communities in and around your programme

  1. Arrange to have a cup of coffee with a fellow student in the local student café.
  2. Take part in an academic or social event alongside your studies.
  3. Apply for a job at the university, e.g. as a mentor or tutor, or in a research group.
  4. Join an association or club on campus.
  5. Organise a social event with your study group, such as having a meal together, a trip to a museum etc.
