Actually, your future career starts when you start the Bioinformatics programme. I highly recommend that you visit the Study Start module "How to explore your career possibilities" in Absalon, which can help you think about your future career.

Bioinformatics alumni are in short supply, so the unemployment rate is near zero, and you are likely to find a job you enjoy. In particular the biotech and biomedicine industries need bioinformatics graduates. You may also want to continue in basic research and look for PhD programmes.

To create a professional network, I recommend that you join the UCPH Alumni.

You can participate in SPOT events on career.

If you are an international student, you have of course all the same options. And remember that English is the preferred language in bioinformatics no matter where you work, and job opportunities are very good in Denmark.
