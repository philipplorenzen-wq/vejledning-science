Start by identifying relevant foundations and scholarships and apply well in advance – preferably 6 months before you intend to go abroad. Some foundations only accept applications once or twice a year.

Consider applying to several foundations as you can often reuse the same documents.


Tips for your application


1)    Get an overview

It is a good idea to get an overview of the grants and scholarships you would like to apply for. This makes it easier to keep track of the deadlines for applying when grants are awarded, which documents to enclose with your application etc.

It is also a good idea to make a note of any special requirements – for example if a foundation expects you to submit a report at the end of your stay. 


2)    Gather relevant attachments

Make sure to gather all relevant documents so that you have them on hand and can easily attach them to your applications.

Always remember to check which specific attachments you need to submit. Different foundations may ask for different forms of documentation – only attach the attachments you are asked for.

Attachments may include:

  • Letters of recommendation
    A letter of recommendation can be from your lecturer, supervisor or employer. 1 or 2 recommendations is usually enough. Ideally, the person providing a letter of recommendation should be academically relevant, either to the stated purpose of the foundation or your stay abroad.
  • Budget
    You must provide an overview of your income and expenses in connection with your stay abroad. Expenses include travel, accommodation, living expenses, language tests, books, visas etc. Be realistic and honest when doing your budget – you may want to refer to the university's website, which often gives an idea of what it costs to live in the city.
  • Transcript of exam results/grades
    You can download a transcript of your exam results/grades on Self Service.
  • Confirmation of admission
    Often, you will have to submit documentation that you have been admitted to the host university or been allocated a place by UCPH. Alternatively, you can submit documentation that you have sent an application to the host university, and when you expect a reply.
  • CV
    It is a good idea to select the parts from your CV that are of particular relevance to your stay.


3)    Write a personal statement

A personal statement should often be approximately 1-2 pages. It is a bit like a job application, where you describe who you are, where you are going and why you are applying for the grant or scholarship.

  • A little about you
    It is a good idea to start by writing shortly about who you are, your degree programme, where you are going and your reasons for wanting to spend some time abroad.
  • Project description
    Describe the host institution. If your host university is leading in a particular field or is at the top of the rankings, you may want to mention it. Explain why it is a perfect fit for your degree programme and your academic interests or career plans.
  • Motivation
    Explain why you have chosen this particular programme or place to study, and why they should choose you. It is good to say something about what you expect to take home with you, and how your studies abroad will contribute to your professional and personal development. Express your enthusiasm for what you are embarking on.
