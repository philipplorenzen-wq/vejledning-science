Gælder for målgrupperne: Geografi Og Geoinformatik - Kandidat

Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/Your-options-after-graduating-SCI-GG.aspx

Graduates with a Master's degree in Geography and Geoinformatics are employed in many different sectors.

Many are occupied in municipalities and other governmental organisations and work with issues of pollution, waste management, ground water, urban, countryside or costal planning, both hands-on and strategical planning, as well as more GIS and remote sensing related tasks. A good number of graduates also work in NGOs on development or environmental issues.

A large number of geographers find employment in the private sector where many work in consultancy on similar topics as listed above related to physical geography, human geography and geoinformatics. There are also an increasing number of graduates finding employment in a wide range of companies in the retail or production sectors as well as in the financial sector, typically working on sustainability issues related to value chains, marketing, and investment.

Students who have the Master's degree with a minor subject often go on to become teachers in upper secondary education (i.e. gymnasieskolen), though this degree also qualifies you for all of the above.

Together with Geofagrådet, a student association, we will arrange meetings where geographers working in different sectors, come and explain their way into the job market and the skills companies and organisations are looking for in future candidates.
