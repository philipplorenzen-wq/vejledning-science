You can apply for additional grant portions if you are delayed as a result of working with students' social or educational conditions (student social work), for example if:

• The work benefits a larger group of students (i.e., students broadly across UCPH)
• You have held a leadership position in a nationwide organization (e.g., DSF)
• You have worked for exchange organizations (e.g., AIESEC)
• You are a member of the board of Studenterhuset

Note: The work must be unpaid. Delays caused by paid work, such as being a tutor or “rusvejleder," do not qualify for additional grant portions.

What documentation must you submit?

1. Documentation of your role

A letter from, for example, the council secretary or the board describing:

  • The membership period
  • The scope of the work

2. Your own description of the work

You must describe:

  • The tasks you carried out (the nature of the work)
  • How much time you spent per week on average
  • Meeting preparation and meetings with your constituency

Important: Meeting participation alone does not entitle you to additional grant portions. The overall time commitment is assessed. You must therefore document that your activities were extensive enough to delay your studies during a period in which you received SU.


You must apply for additional grant portions via minSU on www.su.dk.
