All students must complete a combination of compulsory courses, restrictive elective courses, and elective courses as part of their program.

  • Compulsory courses are listed in the Environment and Development Curriculum. You are required to complete these courses, unless you can document that you already possess the corresponding skills. If so, you must apply to the Study Board for approval of an exemption. 
  • Restricted elective courses vary by specialization (Agriculture and Ecology or Livelihoods and Governance). When selecting restricted electives, you must choose from the approved list for your specific specialization, which can be found in the Environment and Development Curriculum. If you identify a course relevant for your specialization but not included in the list, you may apply to the Study Board for approval. For example, a course on weed science with a term paper focused on tropical weeds would likely be approved as a restricted elective for the Agriculture and Ecology specialization. 
  • Elective courses offer the greatest flexibility. You are free to choose any course, but I strongly recommend selecting courses that help strengthen your academic profile.

Restricted electives and electives may be taken in any of the four teaching blocks (1-4), except where compulsory courses are scheduled.

You may also complete part of your program at other faculties or universities by applying for a transfer of credits through the Study Board. This includes opportunities to study at other universities in Denmark or abroad.
