How do I store and share data securely?

As a student at UCPH and as a private person, you must protect your own and others' data - consider where and how you store and share data.

Protect your information

This means, among other things, that it is important you do not disclose either your own or others' information to strangers.

  • You need to be extra careful when someone asks you to provide e.g. your card information, civil registration number or address. If you have the slightest doubt about whether you should hand over data, then double check if the person has a legitimate purpose.
  • When you need to share data, you should carefully think about how you do it. Always consider if the way you share your data could give strangers access to it – and if it could – find another way to share it. This means e.g. that you must not send someone's or your own personal information on an unencrypted email or share it in a facebook group. 

Personal data

It is your responsibility to take good care of personal data. It can e.g. be interviews, information about subjects or your or someone else's social security number. 

You can read more about how to store or share personal data securely under Planning your study > Rules and dispensation > How to collect and handle personal data.
