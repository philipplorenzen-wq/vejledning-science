Job searching is much more than writing job applications and doing interviews.

Get off to a good start by doing some research and thinking before you start applying for actual jobs.

This part of the process you can start as soon as you'd like – preferably no later than a year before your planned graduation.

6 ways you can prepare your job search

  1. Reflect on what is important to you in a job and write it down. This can be both about the content and terms of the job, but also the social and practical circumstances surrounding it.
  2. Assess your skills and strengths so that you'll be able to put into words what you bring to the table as an employee.
    Find out how to identify your competences
  3. Research companies and job types you might find interesting and notice what skills they require.
    Get inspiration for this research
  4. Match your wishes and skills to the different job types and companies you have found in your research.
  5. Make a LinkedIn profile and be active. Don't forget to let your network know that you will be graduating soon.
    Get tips for your LinkedIn profile
  6. Write a comprehensive CV that can serve as the starting point when you start writing tailored CVs for specific jobs.
    Get inspiration for a good CV

Start your actual job search 3 months before graduation 

Approx 3 months before you graduate you can start applying for jobs.
