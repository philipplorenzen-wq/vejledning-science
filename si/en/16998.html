Should I draw up an agreement with my host company?

You need to draw up a contract with your host company if

  • you work with confidential content in the company
  • you need to protect your rights to ideas, articles or products developed during the cooperation
  • the company wants to protect its rights to ideas, articles or products developed during the cooperation.

Use the UCPH template for external cooperation to get a transparent agreement that insures both your interests and needs as well as the company’s.

Fill out the agreement with the company

Important if you have to sign an agreement drawn up by the company

You should only sign a confidentiality agreement from the company if necessary. Be especially aware how the contract affects your right to write and talk about your results in your Master’s thesis report and in the period after you finish your thesis.

Before you sign a confidentiality agreement from a company:

  • Consider discussing the agreement with your supervisor.
  • Try to keep the period in which you are required to maintain confidentiality to a minimum.
