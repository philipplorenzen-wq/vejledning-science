What do I do with my Master’s thesis?

If your illness prevents you from submitting your Master's thesis on time, you must apply for a dispensation to postpone the submission date.


Talk to your thesis supervisor before applying for a dispensation

Your thesis supervisor can help you find out whether you need to postpone submission, and if so by how long.


Important to note when applying for a dispensation:

  • Specify exactly how much extra time you need in your application and study plan.
     
  • Apply as early as possible after realising that you will not be able to submit your thesis on time, and be realistic about how much extra time you need. If it is less than two months before you have to hand in your thesis, you must apply as soon as possible to ensure that you can get an answer to your application before your original deadline for submission.
     
  • Please attach a statement from your thesis supervisor which 1) describes how far you have progressed with your thesis, and 2) gives an assessment of whether you will be able to finish your thesis if you are granted the extension you are applying for.
     
  • If the extension you want goes beyond your maximum duration of study, you must make this clear in both your written application and in the form.

If your illness prevents you from taking the oral thesis exam, you should not apply for a dispensation, but submit a notification of illness for the exam.
