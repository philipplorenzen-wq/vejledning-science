When and how can I retake the exam?

You can choose the exam period in which you would like to retake the exam.

You can retake the exam in:

  • the first re-exam period for the block to which your course belongs.
  • a later re-exam period in which your exam is offered. This can be either an ordinary exam period or a re-exam period.

You are responsible for registering for re-exams yourself

You're always responsible for registering for re-exams yourself, regardless of when and why you need to retake exams.

See how and when to register for your re-exams

Potential problems with postponing your re-exam

If you do not retake the exam in the re-exam period in the same block as the ordinary exam, be aware that

  1. most exams are only offered in one ordinary exam period and one re-exam period each year
  2. taking a re-exam at a later time may prevent you from meeting your study activity requirements.
