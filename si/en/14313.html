How far apart can my bachelor's and master's degree programme be in order for me to receive SU in between the programmes?

To receive SU in the months between your bachelor's and master's degree programme, they can maximum be two months apart.

For example, you are not eligible for SU between your bachelor's and master's degree programme if you complete your bachelor's programme in May and start on your master's programme in September.

If you complete your bachelor's degree programme on the first day of a month

Please note that if you complete your degree programme on the first day of a month, you are not eligible for SU in the month in question. This means that if you complete your bachelor's degree programme on 1 June or earlier at the summer exams, you are not eligible for SU in the period between your bachelor's and master's programme.


Example – eligible for SU:

You complete your bachelor's degree programme after 1 June and start your master's degree programme in September.
You can receive SU on your master's degree programme from July, as there are two months between the completion of the bachelor's degree programme and the start of the master's degree programme.


Example – not eligible for SU:

You complete your bachelor's degree programme in May and start your master's degree programme in September.
You can apply for SU for your master's degree programme from September, as there are more than two months between the completion of the bachelor's degree programme and the start of the master's degree programme.
