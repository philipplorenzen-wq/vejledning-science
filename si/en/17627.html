How do I write and send an unsolicited application?

In the same way as with solicited applications and CV's, there is not one single correct way of preparing unsolicited applications. It will depend on the company in question and on what you are applying for.
Here are 2 ways that you can consider:
  1. Send a short email and your CV
  2. Send an unsolicited application and your CV.

In both methods you should always follow up on your enquiry. The employer has not initiated the contact, and the responsibility therefore rests with you.
Some companies have special procedures for how you can submit an unsolicited application, for example by creating a profile on their website.
Short email and CV – How to proceed
Find an employee in the company who works in your area or in the team in which you are interested. Send the person a short email with your CV attached.
The email must contain a short description of
  • what you are specifically applying for, e.g. a project collaboration, a full-time job or a student job  
  • who you are and what you can contribute, your motivation for applying for a job with precisely the company in question as well as your relevant competences and experience.
  • how and when you will follow up on your application, e.g. that you will call the following week.
The email must be short and precise and have as its primary purposes to arouse interest in your recipient so that they want to talk further with you.

See example of unsolicited email about project collaboration 


Unsolicited application and CV – How to proceed
Write an actual application based on your knowledge about and wishes for a job with the company and send it together with your CV – either directly to a relevant employee, e.g. a local manager, or to the company's HR department.
Also here, it is a good idea to write, for example in the email, how and when you will follow up on your application, for example that you will call the following week.
Otherwise, the same rules and advice apply to structure etc. as to a solicited application based on a job advertisement.
