My most important advice for you as a student at the University of Copenhagen is to demonstrate responsibility for your own education. You are all adult human beings and even though I and all my colleagues are willing to help you with many practical challenges…..it is still YOUR responsibility to finish the education in a manner that satisfies YOUR ambitions.

There is not a universally applicable framework that will suit all students but here are some of my thoughts on this subject

  • Familiarize yourself with the curriculum and the rules you will have to follow. This includes e.g. the content of the compulsory courses, deadlines for registration of courses and exams, and very importantly, the rules to avoid plagiarism and cheating. Read more about rules and exemptions.
  • Involve yourself in your education. Remember to evaluate the courses that you follow, so that the following students can benefit from your experiences. Participate in student organized activities, e.g. via FEF, which is the name of the association of food science and nutrition students at the Faculty. You can also benefit by participating in special seminars/arrangements. For example, they can be about study techniques, but it can also be presentation of possible projects that you can participate in, and various arrangements that are targeting your career after the University.
  • Find your OWN way through the education. Some of you prefer a lot of compulsory courses because you feel this will guarantee the optimal learning outcome, whereas others want a larger number of courses that they choose themselves. Both options are equally fine. It only demonstrates your personal preferences and that is a very important parameter in your studies. Many students find it beneficial to study in groups with other students, whereas other students prefer to study alone, and you should choose the strategy that works for you. (Although I have to emphasize that several of our courses will include a certain level of group work, simply because this reflects your working environment after graduation).
