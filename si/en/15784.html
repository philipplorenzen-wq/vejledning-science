You decide yourself whether you want to join a trade union or not.

If you are a member of a trade union, you can receive help in all stages of your working life if situations arise in your workplace - or in the process of applying for a job - that you cannot handle yourself.


Services your trade unions will typically offer you:

  • Job search help and career guidance
  • Salary benchmarking and insights into salary negotiating techniques
  • Professional and social events hosted by your union
  • Contract reviews and legal assistance

The political role of your trade union

Many trade unions also handle their members' interests politically by

  • representing their members' interests in political bodies
  • influencing the public agenda and contributing to political solutions at Christiansborg, the seat of the Danish Parliament.

Many trade unions are involved in the bargaining of collective agreements between the employer and one or more trade unions. Collective agreements lay down the overall terms and conditions for your employment, e.g. on salary, pension and working hours.
