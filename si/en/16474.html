How do I network?

Networking is about exchanging knowledge and information. Therefore, you network by asking questions with curiosity and interest when you meet people with interesting jobs and/or workplaces.


Practice your networking questions

Questions are an important tool when networking with someone. Therefore, it is a good idea to prepare some good questions and practice by asking them to family, friends, and classmates.

Do not ask directly for job opportunities but focus on exchanging knowledge.

8 questions you can ask when networking:
  • How did you end up where you are today – was it planned or accidental?
  • How do you use your expertise in your day-to-day work?
  • What do you find exciting about your job?
  • What particular competences do you need for your job?
  • What do you enjoy doing?
  • What inspires and motivates you?
  • What advice can you give me if I want to move in the same direction?
  • Do you know anyone who might be relevant for me to talk to?

 
Consider what you can offer to your network

When networking, it's about both

  • giving and taking
  • being interesting and interested.

Therefore, you need to consider how you can give something and be interesting to others.

3 examples: How you can create value for others:
  1. Share what you know, for example, through your studies, on platforms like LinkedIn, at events, and in conversations with professional contacts.
  2. Offer to mentor high school students or younger students considering going in the same direction as you.
  3. Publicly praise someone on platforms like LinkedIn or share their posts.
