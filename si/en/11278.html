My name is Nicolas Thibault and I'm your Head of Studies. As your Head of Studies I know the academic contents of your programme in detail, and have contributed in determining your curriculum in coorporation with the four research groups at Geology-Geoscience. 

My research revolves around paleoclimate and evolution of calcareous plankton, especially in periods of greenhouse when CO2 atmospheric levels were much higher than today. I focus particularly on integrated stratigraphy and climate cycles that aim at building precise age models for marine sediments. 

I teach in the courses Palaeontology and History of Life as well as Past Climate. And in addition I supervise project courses and BSc and MSc projects. 

As a student you have many options and a flexible framework for influencing the contents of your course of studies. The choices within your curriculum are yours to make. 

Here are my advice and my recommendations for completing your master's programme in Geology-Geoscience successfully. Read my advice as inspiration for your choices - not as rules to follow. 

My recommendations are written on the basis of the latest programme structure, unless I have specifically written otherwise.
