How does the inclusion survey differ from the educational environment assessment and the study environment survey?

The study environment survey, known as Studiemiljøundersøgelsen or SMU in Danish, is conducted by UCPH every two years. SMU is part of the efforts to optimise the framework for teaching and learning and it involves students in the work to identify and solve any study environment challenges.
The SMU also contributes to UCPH's educational environment assessment, known as Undervisningsmiljøvurdering or UMV. An UMV is an educational institution's official status on its study and educational environment. It presents a status regarding its degree programmes and through action plans for the study environments it describes how it will address challenges or issues.


The inclusion survey is a different kind of survey. Both students and employees participated in the inclusion survey, and several identical questions were asked to both target groups.


As the inclusion survey is conducted at a more general level, it has the potential to uncover other questions, including questions about minority group affiliation based on e.g. ethnicity, sexuality or political beliefs, that the SMU and UMV cannot. The inclusion survey thus complements these surveys with important new perspectives on possible study environment challenges. The results will also inspire initiatives connected with UCPH's new strategic framework for diversity, equity and inclusion (2025 - 2030), where initiatives will be launched locally and across the university, targeting both employees and students.
