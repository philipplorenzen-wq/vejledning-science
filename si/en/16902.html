During your studies, you may undertake a Project in Practice (internship) and/or a Project outside the course scope, and you are required to complete a thesis.

Project in practice

The Project in Practice (PIP) is an internship combined with an academic report. Its primary aim is to give you hands-on work experience, while also incorporating a strong academic learning element.

To complete a PIP, you must find a host organization or company, where you can carry out the internship. Further, you must secure a university supervisor and sign a standard contract. Toward the end of the internship, you are required to submit a written academic assignment to the university. Some students choose to combine their PIP with their thesis. For example, they may first work as an intern at an NGO for a few months and then continue with their thesis on a related topic. 

Whether or not a PIP fits well with your studies is entirely up to you. Gaining practical experience can be very valuable, but if you prefer to focus on academic specialization through coursework, that is equally valid.

You can read more here: Project in Practice.

Project outside course scope

The Project Outside the Course Scope (POC) is an independent, individual assignment. It may take various forms, such as laboratory experiments, a literature review, or another type of academic project. This option offers a high degree of flexibility, allowing you to explore and investigate in greater depth a topic of your own choosing. You are required to have a supervisor for the project.

Read more about  Project Outside the Course Scope.

Thesis

There is no single standard approach to developing your thesis. Many students choose to carry out their thesis in collaboration with external partners, such as universities, private companies, NGOs, governmental bodies, or development organizations, but this is not a requirement. What is mandatory is having a main supervisor from the Faculty of Science, regardless of how the project is organized. 

You may develop your thesis in several ways, for example by:

  • Defining a specific topic together with your supervisor.
  • Integrating your thesis into an existing research project.  
  • Conducting your thesis work at a host university or organization

As an ED student, you may apply for funding to support fieldwork conducted outside Denmark. 

Read more about the conditions and procedures for applying for fieldwork funding

Thesis development is an iterative process that takes time, so it is important to start early. Explore different ideas, remain open to adjustments, and seek inspiration. Your supervisor acts as a facilitator and will support you in refining your ideas once a supervision agreement is in place. Do not hesitate to share early drafts even if they are rough. It is only the final thesis that is assessed.
