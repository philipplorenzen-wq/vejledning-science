Computational Biology

The Computational Biology track is intended for students with a strong background in biology, biochemistry, molecular biology or similar who want to apply bioinformatics and data analysis in biological problems.

The compulsory courses focus on giving you a strong foundation in structural bioinformatics, sequence and high throughput data analysis and population genetics during the first year.

In the very first block you can take an elective or restricted elective course. If you are not familiar with Python programming, I strongly advise you to take the course Python Programming for data science as an elective course since Python programming is extensively used in bioinformatics. Note that this is a BSc-level course, you will need to use as a free elective 7.5 ECTS slot (not a restricted elective). If your Python skills are solid, you may consider Machine Learning A.which many of your classmates will also take. There are also a number of courses to deepen your specific biology or computer science knowledge, such as The Human Microbiome or Natural Language Processing. Please check the Recommended Academic Qualifications for each course to make sure your BSc provides you with a suitable background, or plan additional time for additional studies. There are many other courses available, just make sure they do not clash with Biological Sequence Analysis (taught in Schedule A).

You have more room for restricted elective courses in blocks 3+4 of the first year.

The second year starts with two blocks that consist only of elective and restricted elective courses, where you can form your own academic profile and prepare for your thesis. You can take elective and restricted courses or bioinformatics projects (at the department or in the industry) or you can go abroad. You should also consider whether courses in Innovation and Entrepreneurship are relevant for you. If you have a good idea you can do a project in practice in the industry or the UCPH Innovation Hubs. It may also be relevant to find courses about sustainability. Remember that you can swap the location of elective and restricted elective course (e.g. you can take an elective course block 1 first year and 2 restricted electives in block 1 second year).

The final two blocks consist of your thesis which can be carried out at the department, at a hospital or in the industry. Note that you will need an internal supervisor if your thesis is carried out outside of the faculty of SCIENCE and the GLOBE institute.

Computer Science

The Computer Science track is intended for students with a strong background in programming, statistics, computer science and similar who wants to develop algorithms and software for bioinformatical purposes. Importantly, one of the mandatory courses on this track, Advanced Algorithms and Data Structures, assumes that you have had an introductory course to formal algorithm analysis, proof of time complexity and correctness, etc.

The compulsory courses in the first two blocks focus on giving you a strong foundation in Biological sequence analysis, structural bioinformatics, Machine Learning and an introduction to Molecular Biology. If biological terms and concepts are mentioned in a course that you are not familiar with, do not hesitate to ask your instructor, TA or classmates for explanations and resources for further study. Molecular biology is broad and there can be a lot to catch up on when you start a bioinformatics education. Don't worry, you will get there – but it does take time and effort.

Blocks 3 and 4 of the first year and blocks 1 and 2 the second year only have one compulsory course in Advanced Algorithms and Data Structures, while the remaining courses are elective or restrictive elective. You can use this period to form your own academic profile and prepare yourself for your thesis. You can take elective and restricted courses or bioinformatics projects (at the department or in the industry) or you can go abroad. You should also consider whether courses in Innovation and Entrepreneurship are relevant for you. If you have a good idea you can take a project in practice in the industry or the UCPH Innovation Hubs. It may also be relevant to find courses about sustainability. Remember that you can swap the location of elective and restricted elective courses (e.g. you can take an elective course block 3 first year and 2 restricted electives in bloc 1 second year).

The final two blocks of the second year consist of your thesis which can be carried out at the department or in the industry. Note that you will need an internal supervisor if your thesis is carried out outside of the faculty of SCIENCE and the GLOBE institute
