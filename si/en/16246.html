Most of the courses in the first year are compulsory for you. These courses are the cornerstones in the programme. I recommend to follow these courses in the sequence laid out in the programme structure, i.e. starting in block 1. This way you will experience the intended scientific progression where elements of one course extend on elements from the previous course.

Specialisation

This study programme is fundamentally about sustainability – sustainable management of forests and nature. No matter how you compose your education you will cover aspects of ecology, economy, and society combined with knowledge, tools, and skills regarding planning and management. Within this broad sustainability framework, you can specialize and make choices to create a certain profile – for example with a stronger emphasis on either economy, ecology, or society. The choice of elective courses, restricted elective courses, as well as your choices regarding your Master's thesis all represent important opportunities to specialize and create your own profile. Do you want to go abroad? Do you want to go deep? How can you design your master's degree to complement your bachelor's degree in a powerful way, pointing towards an interesting career?

At the end of year 1 (block 3) and in the first half of year 2 (blocks 1 and 2), you can specialize by choosing elective courses that you find most interesting and relevant. For the restricted electives however, you must choose courses from the lists in the programme curriculum, featuring courses that are particularly relevant for FNM students.

Courses at other universities

You can also apply to take restricted elective courses at other universities in Denmark or in other countries. Applications for pre-approval are assessed on a case-by-case basis.

You are always welcome to contact me, if you would like my advice, as to what courses may be most relevant.

Elective subjects

The elective subject elements are unrestricted, so you can in principle, choose any course you may find interesting. It is a good idea to pursue your personal interests, curiosity and gut feeling. I do, however advise, that you look ahead and consider the professional profile and specialization that the chosen courses will provide. Think about your hopes and expectations for the future, and envision possible jobs and desirable career paths. Does your profile match what your future "dream employer" might be looking for?

Design your own profile

You can design your program as you wish by use of the elective and restricted elective courses. As inspiration, I propose seven different profiles, below, that represent different specializations and job opportunity directions.

Many different compositions can be developed within each of the seven suggested profiles – and you can certainly develop your own profile reflecting your personal interests, hopes and beliefs about the future job-market. At any rate, your profile should be, composed in accordance with the programme structure including compulsory, elective, and restricted elective courses. I encourage you to play around with the options and spend time carefully considering your desires and possible futures - before you settle on your plan and profile. It may also be a good idea to think out of the box and check possible options at other universities in Denmark or abroad that may shape or strengthen your desired profile.

Whatever profile you choose – consider the list of restricted elective courses under the programme structure specifically with regards to your personal interests, curiosity as well as your needs, dreams and desires for the future. For example if you want to be a researcher you should consider “Planning Interdisciplinary Research” no matter which of the above profiles you pursue.

Before you make your final decision, you should perhaps also look into the full KU-list of possible courses to check if there are courses that exactly match your personal profile or strategy.

You can design your own study plan from the pre-filled study plans inspired by the seven profiles.
