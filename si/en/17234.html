How do we ensure good collaboration in the group?

Good group work is not about how similar you are, personally and academically, but about how you work together. The foundation for good collaboration is primarily clear communication and a clear framework. 


Three key steps to create a solid foundation for good collaboration: 

  1. Align what you expect from the group 
  2. Make clear agreements for your collaboration 
  3. Talk about how you communicate in the group 

Specific tools for helping you with all three steps are described below.

Talk about your expectations of the study group 

Start your first meeting in the study group by discussing and aligning your expectations and wishes for your collaboration. This can prevent many misunderstandings and potential conflicts from arising. 

Your study and career guidance counsellors have developed a question guide to help you align your expectations within the group.

TALK ABOUT EXPECTATIONS

Make clear agreements for your collaboration 

When you have talked about your expectations of the study group, make clear agreements for your collaboration. 


For example, it is important that you agree: 

  • what you will be doing in the group 
  • how often, when and where you should meet 
  • how you will handle disagreements in the group. 

Your study and career guidance counsellors have developed a template for a written agreement that you can use as a basis.

Focus on good communication in the group 

When communication in the group is good, everyone feels seen, heard and respected. This creates a comfortable space, where you dare to launch into new things and make mistakes in the group. It also creates the best conditions for learning and developing through your group work – professionally and personally. 


4 things you can do to improve communication in the group: 

  • Make sure that everyone has a chance to speak. For example, you can take turns to chair the meeting. 
  • Give everyone equal speaking time at the meetings. Use a stopwatch if necessary. 
  • Discuss how you talk in the group (tone, humour etc.), so that everyone feels comfortable speaking. 
  • Make sure to listen to each other, and always let people finish speaking – even when you have an answer or question.
