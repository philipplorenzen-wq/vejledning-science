The inclusion survey has identified experiences with inclusion and with being part of a minority at the university. It thus identifies tendencies among employees and students across the entire UCPH.
To ensure respondents' confidentiality, the analysis is kept at a general level. Some minority groups are quite small, and as a minority person it can be challenging to respond honestly to surveys because of the worry that one's answers will be recognisable. Because of this, the analysis has employed a discretionary consideration of 20. This means that there had to be at least 20 people in a group of respondents for their answers to be included in the result reports.


Although the data analyses are not designed with a programme-specific focus, they provide insights into everyday situations that all students and employees are familiar with: interactions at meetings, with supervisors, etc.
