Make a study plan – and adjust it

  • Two years is not a long time. You will do thesis work for 4, 6.5 or 9 months. So, you want to make the best use of the remaining time. Make a study plan early in the first year to make sure that you will be able to follow your favorite courses. Since you will not have time for all of them and you will get new ideas, you will most likely have to adjust the plan along the way.
  • Discuss your plans with other students, with me or with the Study and Career guidance counsellors at UCPH Study Guidance. I invite you to a student meeting every block and I am also available to discuss your ideas individually with you. Contact me by e-mail or in person for a talk. 
  • Make sure you comply with the requirements in your curriculum, but do not let the rules restrict you too much. If you find exciting and relevant courses offered elsewhere it is possible to apply to get them into your study plan – but only if you find out well in advance. Doing something outside the normal takes time. Read about how to use your electives and maybe change your course of studies.
  • You are responsible for complying with the existing rules and regulations. Use your programme structure website. Here, you will find the answer to many specific questions. Also know where to find your curriculum. It is a dry piece of text that sets the frame for your program.

Your daily student life

  • Consider your studies as a full time engagement. Many of you need to have jobs and the logistics are not always easy. If you need additional information on field work and excursions for your planning ask the course responsible.
  • Attend classes, prepare well and participate actively. This is the most efficient way to learn and thus to get good results. See a few examples of different study techniques.
  • Keep your eye open for meetings, seminars, PhD courses and conferences in plant science, agriculture and environment. If the events look like they are restricted to academic staff and researchers or to professionals, contact the person responsible directly and ask whether it is possible to participate as an MSc student. Often it is possible to participate and to get a discount fee of participation.
  • Contribute to the social coherence of your class: speak English when in crowds made up of people with various language background and mix among nationalities. Most classes establish a Facebook group for exchanging information, asking questions and sharing events.
  • Use and check your KUmail. If you do not use the KUmail as your main address be sure that everything you receive is forwarded, including your Absalon e-mails. As Head of Studies I only communicate with you on your KUmail. The same goes for the administration. See how to handle your KUmail.
  • Read your study messages and study events not to miss important deadlines, messages and events.
  • Use your opportunity to influence courses and teaching for the benefit of the next class. Evaluate all courses you follow. Teachers, course responsible and the departments, take evaluations seriously and you could experience that teachers mention what last year's class suggested and how that has changed this year.

New encounters? Oral exams and plagiarism

  • Oral exams are frequent in MSc courses at University of Copenhagen. If you are not familiar with oral exams from your previous studies, you may be uncertain of how to approach it. In many courses, the oral exam is based on some written assignment (project report, written assignments, lab reports). Ask the course responsible to use some time in the beginning and by the end of the course to describe the oral exam: What are the assessment criteria? What will be the different parts of the exam? How should you prepare? What could you bring to the exam (often anything you want)? What does the teacher suggests you bring? Remember that teachers want you to demonstrate that you have learned something in his or her course. How to best prepare is a personal thing. Ask your fellow students who have experience with oral exams what works for them and get inspired. Read about how to get ready for your exams and improve your exam techniques, and read up on the rules about oral exam.
  • Be aware of unintentional plagiarism when you do written projects and assignments. Lack of knowledge of the exact rules will not save you from serious sanctions such as exclusion or expulsion from the program. If you are not fully familiar with the basic rules of plagiarism from your BSc degree, read about good scientific practice and take a quiz.
