How do I apply for a dispensation or submit a notification of illness?

Once you know what you need to apply for, you must

  1. write an application with an explanation
  2. get documentation confirming your illness
  3. prepare a study plan
  4. complete the form: Long-term illness.


Written application – what to include


Your application must include

  1. an explanation of what you are applying for an dispensation to do (e.g. how much extra time etc.)
  2. a description of the reasons why your illness is preventing you from living up to the rule(s) which you are applying for a dispensation from.


In your description, explain – preferably giving specific examples

  • how you are being affected by the illness
  • how your condition is preventing you from studying and/or sitting exams
  • if there are any factors in addition to your current illness that are affecting your studies and which have caused a delay in your studies.


Documentation – how to document your illness

Enclose a medical certificate, a statement from your psychologist, hospital admission papers or other relevant documentation supporting your application for a dispensation.
If your documentation is not ready when you need to submit your application, you can send it later. In the comments field on the dispensation form, explain that your documentation is on the way.
Once you have your documentation, send it via the form Get Study Administrative Help.



The documentation must state

  • that you have been ill
  • the date on which you became ill and unable to study
  • how long your doctor (or other therapist) expects you to be absent due to illness
  • how your illness has affected your ability to take courses, projects or exams.
See a guide to what your documentation should include


Study plan – what your plan needs to show

Your study plan needs to show what your course of study will look like when taking account of your illness.

First and foremost, your study plan needs to be

  • realistic given your situation – for example, it should not include period(s) of full-time studying if it is not realistic for you to study on a full-time basis.
  • based on what you know and can document at the moment.

Read how to prepare a study plan according to your needs under study at reduced hours.
Find a digital study plan template in which the recommended structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure and your year of admission.


Fill in the form

What to do:

  1. Fill in the form: "Apply for dispensation: Long-term illness".
  2. Tick the various things you want to apply for.
  3. Use the 'Other' field if you want to apply for something (due to the same illness) that is not listed on the form.


Use the same form when you apply for

  • Permission to withdraw from exams and/or courses after the deadline
  • Permission to submit a notification of illness for an exam
  • Permission to postpone the deadline for submitting your Master's thesis
  • A dispensation from the first-year exam
  • A dispensation from the maximum duration of study

Reply to your application

As a general rule, you will receive an answer within 6 weeks from when the Study Board has received all relevant information about your application. If you need to send supplementary material, you will be contacted via your KUmail.
