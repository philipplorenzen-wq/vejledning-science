The possible outcomes are:

  1. Offer of new assessment with new examiners (not possible for oral exams)
  2. Offer of a re-exam
  3. A combination of 1,2,and 4, if the exam or test includes a written assignment with an oral defense, or
  4. That the complaint is not upheld.

If you say yes to a re-exam or a re-assessment

If you accept one of the above, the university appoints new examiners.

The new grade applies - even if it is lower

The new grade from a re-exam or re-assessment applies, and you cannot complain about your grade.

There is no guarantee that you will improve your grade through a re-exam or a re-assessment. You can therefore risk getting a lower grade for a re-exam or re-assessment.
