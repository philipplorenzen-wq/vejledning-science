Gælder for målgrupperne: Landscape Architecture - Kandidat

Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/Your-way-through-the-programme-SCI-LA.aspx

The MSc Landscape Architecture programme has two specialisations, Landscape Design and Urban Design, covering a wide range of subjects, encompassing multiple scales and involving different phases of the planning and design process. Both specialisations are focused on planning and design methods, and students from different specialisations should be able to work together.

From the academic year 2019/2020, the compulsory courses amount to 15 ECTS and the restricted elective courses are divided into two groups. You must have 45 ECTS from the first group and up to 15 ECTS from the second group. Finally, you can take 15 ECTS elective courses if you do a 30 ECTS thesis.

See your curriculum and options in your programme.

You can specialize in a specific area or create a broader compound curriculum. You can also create a curriculum based on personal interests. In any case, you will acquire planning and design knowledge, skills and competences in multiple scales and subject areas of Landscape Architecture – based on the content of the compulsory and restricted elective courses.It is important that you gain design experience and acquire personal competencies. 
You should also consider that new technologies and changing societal problems require the ability for well-founded academic reflection and well-founded design skills in order to be successful in a dynamic job market in the long term. Even if future jobs focus on new specialist areas such as biodiversity, solution-oriented planning and design methods with a well-founded scientific approach remain the basis of professional practice.
