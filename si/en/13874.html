The inclusion survey’s main report about students reveals that while the majority of students feel supported and welcome at the university, one in four reports experiences of exclusion. 43 percent of students identify as minorities and report facing greater barriers to belonging, accessibility, and academic opportunities: Students with neurodivergence, mental or physical functional variations, ethnic and religious minorities, as well as students with international backgrounds report the most pronounced challenges. 
Key issues include social exclusion, language barriers, inaccessible facilities, and lack of representation. Implicit cultural norms and alcohol-centred social events further complicate integration. Many students report feeling pressure to mask aspects of their identity to fit in. 
Students express broad support for inclusion efforts at the university and a clear desire for change. The findings thereby offer UCPH a valuable opportunity to strengthen its culture of belonging through targeted initiatives such as inclusive teaching practices and study formats as well as improved support systems and communication.
