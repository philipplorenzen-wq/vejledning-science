We have a revised curriculum, where the MSc programme is modernized so it corresponds to what a modern geologist must be able to know and work with; Increased digitalization, sustainability, green transformation, public and society relevant work, teaching and communication. There are field trips and field courses in Denmark and abroad. The curriculum consist of a general specialization, with a great opportunity to tone your studies based on a connection to the four research groups.

These are my important notes in the MSc programme:

It consists of a compulsory course (15 ECTS), 30 ECTS restricted elective credits, 15 ECTS elective credits and a 60 ECTS Master's thesis. You also have the option of doing a Project in Practice and go on exchange.

The compulsory course gives you a good insight to geology as an academic area. The four research groups all contribute to the course, where some of the classes take place as field trips. The course also gives you a good opportunity to meet and get to know all your peers, Danish and international alike.

The restricted elective credits allow you to choose between 17 courses, all related to the four research groups. You're free to choose the courses you like from the list. We have also made suggestions of courses that could be of interest to you if you're interested in Geophysics, Sedimentary Systems, Water Resources or Geochemistry and Mineral Resources.

The elective credits are free to use as you like. If you would like to take more courses in the geological area, you can use your elective credits on restricted elective courses.
