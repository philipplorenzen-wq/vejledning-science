Please note: This chapter is for students following the specialization "Chemistry, Toxicology and Health", and "Soil and Water" only.  

In addition to the Master's thesis, you also have the opportunity of doing a Project in Practice or a Project outside the course scope. Note that the project work might count as restrictive elective or elective, it depends on the specific specialisation. There are therefore plenty of opportunities for you to specialise in projects that you find particularly interesting.

Getting in touch with supervisors

The most important thing concerning all types of projects is to create connections to potential supervisors well in advance of the project start. In November at year one, we therefore have a meeting where you can meet representatives from the different scientific fields. They can help give you advice on project ideas and create contacts between you and other potential supervisors in their network: both within their scientific network, but also to people in industry or other organisations in Denmark or in other countries.

You can see some examples of master's theses for students of the programme above. New ideas for projects are posted in different places.

  • Find project ideas in KU Projects & Jobs.

  • For project ideas at home-departments and other departments, I strongly recommend you contact the professors with a research profile of your interest and ask them personally.  

If you have ideas of your own, do not hesitate to present this to a relevant supervisor. If in doubt, you are always welcome to ask me for advice on whom to contact. The university homepage, however, often gives good and updated research profiles!

Projects in collaboration with industry or organisations

If you are interested in doing an MSc project with public institutions or the industry, I can recommend you to become a member of the "Environmental Chemistry and Health Alumni" at LinkedIn. This group contain most of the old students of Environmental Science-Chemistry, Toxicology and Health, of which many are employed in private companies. These "old" fellow students know the program and your competences, and can create valuable links to their present workplace.
