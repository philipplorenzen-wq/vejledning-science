Sometimes other external or internal events or circumstances can engulf your life and overshadow your motivation for your studies. In this case, trying to work on your motivation in isolation will not help.

You should therefore try to find out whether it is primarily some other external or internal factor that is getting in the way right now.
Examples of internal and external factors that can affect your motivation: 

  • Falling in love
  • Struggling with loneliness
  • Illness (your own or in your immediate family)
  • A really exciting student job that consumes all your energy
  • You lack structure and overview of your life
  • A hobby that takes up a lot of your time

2 ways you can take action 

Depending on what the issues are, you may need to take a closer look at: 

  • how to plan your study day.
    Read more about how to plan your study time
  • whether you actually need to make a choice in the specific situation.
    Read more about making choices during your studies

You are always welcome to discuss your situation with your student and career guidance counsellors – even if you are unsure about exactly what the issues are.
