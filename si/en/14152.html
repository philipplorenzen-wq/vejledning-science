The General Data Protection Regulation grants some rights to the persons who provide data to you. The data subjects have the right to:

  • see what data you have registered about them 
  • receive a copy of the data
  • have the data rectified if they contain inaccurate information.

You should also be aware that they have the right to withdraw their consent. This means that you are not allowed to continue processing the data. If they also demand erasure of data, you are obliged to erase the data.

You can read more in the guidelines on data subjects rights (only available in Danish).

Need for the data subject's written consent

If you collect data about other persons yourself, you must ensure that the data subjects in question consent to your use and disclosure of their data in connection with your education. You get a consent in writing by asking the participants to sign a consent form.

You must not collect and process personal data before the form has been signed.

With a signed consent form you are sure that the persons in question agree to:

  • the use of their data as part of your education
  • the disclosure of their data to University of Copenhagen and that the data may be published if you publish your assignment, your project or your master's thesis, for example via at the Royal Library.

You must submit a copy of the form to the person who has signed it.
