Can I withdraw from submitting my Master’s thesis?

No. As a rule, you cannot withdraw from submitting your thesis once the contract has been approved and you have passed the start date stated in the contract.

  • If your contract has the status 'Approved' you can withdraw from submitting your thesis no later than the day before the date stated under 'Dissertation start' in the contract.
  • If your contract has the status 'Ready for approval' you can still withdraw from submitting your thesis.

If you are unable to start your thesis at the agreed time, you can withdraw from submitting your thesis by completing the form ‘Get study administration help’. 

On and after the start date in your approved contract, you can no longer withdraw from the thesis. In that case, you will need to apply for additional time (postponement) or for dispensation to withdraw due to highly extraordinary circumstances.

Highly extraordinary circumstances that may prevent the completion of a Master's thesis process:

  • The research group you are working with is discontinued, which makes it impossible to proceed with your Master's thesis.
  • Your supervisor is no longer able to supervise you, and you can't find another supervisor for this Master's thesis.
  • The company you are cooperating with has backed out, and it's impossible for you to complete your Master's thesis without it.

Your must apply for a dispensation in order to withdraw due to highly extraordinary circumstances

Talk with your supervisor about your situation and your options or the lack of options, before you apply. Preferably, get a statement from him or her to use when you apply for a dispensation. Talk with your head of studies if your supervisor is not an option.

5 steps when you apply for a dispensation:
  1. Get a statement from your supervisor that confirms how and why it is impossible to complete the Master's thesis.
  2. Get documentation of your situation, e.g. a medical certificate from your doctor or an opinion from a psychologist.
  3. Write a statement which describes the highly extraordinary circumstances and how they make it impossible for you to complete the Master's thesis.
  4. Create a study plan for your entire course of study (not necessary, if you only have your Master's thesis left in your programme) which shows how you intend to complete your study programme. Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Planning your studies.
  5. Fill out an application by using 'Apply for a dispensation' and enclose your documentation, supervisor's statement, your statement, and study plan.

Reply to your application

As a general rule, you will receive a reply within 6 weeks of the Study Board having received all relevant information about your application. If you need to send supplementary material, you will be contacted via your KUmail.
