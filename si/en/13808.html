Why can it be difficult to study?

  • Slower reading speed than your fellow students
  • Problems learning new course content through reading
  • Problems in keeping sentences in your head while writing them down
  • Problems in formulating difficult content in writing
  • Major differences in written and oral formulation
  • Problems in reading and writing in a foreign language
  • Uncertainty of spelling

What help can I get?

  • Dyslexia test
  • IT pack with dyslexia programs for your own computer
  • Instructions on how to use the IT pack
  • Dictaphone to record sound
  • Handheld scanner + C pen
  • Digital teaching materials
  • Support hours with dyslexia consultant
  • Program that can read texts aloud or make PDFs easier to read

You can also apply for special conditions at exams. For example, extra time for the exam itself and permission to use study aids, etc.

And there are additional and specific aids that you are allowed to bring to a written on-site exam (ITX exam).

Read more about study aids for written on-site exams (ITX exams).

I think I'm dyslexic. How do I find out if that's the case?

  • If you are unsure of whether you are dyslexic, you can apply for a dyslexia test 
  • The SPS counsellors can apply for the test for you. 
  • Send an email to sps@adm.ku.dk

I know I'm dyslexic. What should I do?

  • Fill in the information form so we know which aids and support you need.
  • If you have not previously received SPS in, for example, upper secondary school, you must also send your dyslexic test to sps@adm.ku.dk.
