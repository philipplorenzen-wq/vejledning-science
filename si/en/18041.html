Can I make changes to my course of study and my specialisation?

You can make adjustments to the recommended course of study and make your own personal study plan. However, due to the academic progression of your course of study, not all programme elements can be changed around.

Within certain limits, you can

  • change the order of your courses
  • replace compulsory or restricted elective courses on your study programme.

Find out what you need to be aware of when making your own personal study plan here on this page under “How do I make a personal study plan?"

If you want to replace compulsory courses

You must have an approval from your Study Board before you can take other courses instead of those that are restricted elective courses or compulsory courses on your study programme.

The courses you choose must live up to the following requirements: 

  • Courses replacing compulsory courses must have almost complete overlaps in the academic content with the compulsory course it replaces.
You apply for permission to replace compulsory courses or restricted elective courses with other courses at SCIENCE by filling in a form.

If the new course is offered outside SCIENCE, you must follow a different procedure

Apply to replace a compulsory course


As a general rule, you will receive a reply within 6 weeks of the Study Board having received all relevant information about your application. If you need to send supplementary material, you will be contacted via your UCPHmail.
How your application is processed

Change of specialisation

You may change your specialisation, if the following elements can be included in the specialisation you wish to change to:

  • Passed courses and projects
  • Approved credit transfers you have applied for
  • Binding registrations
  • Binding pre-approvals

Read about how credit transfers from previous studies can be reassessed for your new specialisation under Credit from previous studies.

Read about binding registration for courses under Course withdrawal.
