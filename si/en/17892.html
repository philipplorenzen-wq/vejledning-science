Studying abroad can add an international academic dimension to your study programme. You can experience new ways of studying, increase your cultural insights, and broaden your understanding of the field, improve your language skills, make new friends and built an international network. 

The UCPH has many exchange agreements (bilateral agreements outside EU and Erasmus agreements inside EU) and besides these agreements, each individual student can find other places of interest. Many teachers do have international contacts from research and other international activities. Please ask around if you are interested in knowing more about one of the 110 landscape architectural programs in Europe. In North America the teachers’ association CELA provide a good overview.In Denmark we have a special agreement with the Royal Danish Academy, Architecture, Design and Conservation for a six month exchange.As a rule, the necessary pre-approval of courses as “restricted elective" courses is relatively easy, and with some closer investigation it is also possible to replace compulsory courses, however there are mobility periods on all specialisations.
