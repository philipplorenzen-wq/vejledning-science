If you want to study abroad, the ENRE program is structured in a way that enables this in blocks 1 and 2 in the second year, if you are admitted in September.

It is very important that you start planning this as early as possible, at least half a year in advance.

One reason to start planning this early is that you need pre-approval for the courses you take during your study abroad. Especially for the restricted electives, you need to find courses that match the criteria described above. The elective subjects could be any course, but I recommend that you choose courses that are relevant for your profile as environmental and natural resource economist. Hence, it is strongly recommended to visit a university that has plenty of relevant courses on offer.

To learn more about your possibilities for studying abroad, contact UCPH Study Guidance. I would also recommend that you get in contact with older students who have been abroad earlier, to learn from their experiences.
