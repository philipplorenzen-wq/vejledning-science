How do I follow up on an unsolicited application?

If you do not hear from the company, it is essential that you follow up on your enquiry with, for example, a call 1-2 weeks after your application. In this way, you take ownership and show them that you are interested, enterprising and serious.
Remember that a lack of response to your application does not necessarily mean a lack of interest. Perhaps they are just very busy.
Be well prepared when you follow up
Before you call, it is a really good idea to prepare yourself for
  • how you want to present yourself: Some information about who you are and why you are calling  
  • what you would like to ask about the company so that you learn more about the workplace, tasks and what they are looking for.
In this way, you can reassure yourself and ensure that you obtain the knowledge from the conversation that you need.
See example of example follow up call


Examples of questions you may ask when calling:

  • Have you tried collaborating with students before? What was the process like?
  • Have you employed student assistants? How do you recruit them and what do they do?
Assess during the conversation whether it is meaningful to suggest a follow-up interview or a meeting where you can discuss a potential future collaboration.
