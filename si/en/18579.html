As a student, you are responsible for storing your data in a responsible manner to prevent loss of and unauthorised access to data. Information on UCPH's Policy for Research Data Management will be available later.

If you want to know more about how to handle confidential data in the best way possible, you can take an online GDPR course for students.

Where can I store files that contain confidential data?

If you need secure storage for files containing personal or confidential data, you must follow the instructions provided by your lecturer. There may be specific storage and management requirements for what systems to use for personal and confidential data, for example TASJA or REDCap. In such cases, you must follow the relevant instructions.

If there are no requirements from your lecturer or faculty to store files in a specific system, you can save your files on your personal network drive: the t-drive.

Read how to gain access to your personal network drive: the t-drive.

Where can I store files that don't contain personal or confidential data?

As a student at UCPH, you can save files that don't contain personal or confidential data on OneDrive. OneDrive also offers the opportunity to share and work together on files.

Information about t-drive

  • Your t-drive has a storage capacity of 10 GB.
  • You have access to the t-drive while studying at UCPH and six months after graduation.
  • It is not possible to share files on the t-drive. The t-drive is your personal drive, and only you can access it.

If you want to share files

If your lecturer/supervisor also needs access to the data, they should create a folder on the S drive and give you and any fellow students you are working with access to the folder. If you have any question, please ask your lecturer.

Deletion

You must always erase personal data when you no longer need them for your assignment/project/master's thesis. This means that you must erase data when your assignment/project/master's thesis has been assessed, and the deadline for complaints has expired.

Obligation to describe processing activities in a record

As data controller, you are obliged to describe the data that you collect and the purpose for which you collect the data. This must be described in a document known as a record of processing activities.
