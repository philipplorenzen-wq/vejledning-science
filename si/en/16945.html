If you need to stop a course before it is finished, you must first withdraw from the exam to avoid using an exam attempt.

If the deadline for withdrawing has not been exceeded, you can withdraw from the exam via Self Service.

Go to Self Service

If the withdrawal period has ended, you can apply for a dispensation to be withdrawn after the deadline due to illness or death in the family.

How to apply for a dispensation to be withdrawn from an exam after the deadline


Need to be withdrawn from a course and ways of doing so

Once the course is in progress, your only way of withdrawing from it is by applying for and being granted a dispensation due to your illness.


Withdrawing can be a good idea if you

  • are unsure whether you want to take the course again later. Once you have used an exam attempt for a course, it is very difficult to withdraw later.
  • want to be guaranteed a place on the course again. You are already guaranteed a place if you cannot take the exam without attending the course again.
    See whether this applies to your course in the course description 

Read more about how to apply to be withdrawn from a course after the deadline


Register again when you are ready

If you are withdrawn, you must remember to register for courses (and thereby also exams) or just the exams again later.
Register for courses during the ordinary registration period if you are guaranteed a place and want to use it.
