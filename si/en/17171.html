It is important that you take safety precautions when you are going on a stay abroad. You should therefore consider a number of matters that will improve your travel safety. You can then concentrate on enjoying your stay abroad. 
Checklist prior to your stay abroad:
  • Register on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Denmark's list of Danes abroad (the Ministry of Foreign Affairs will then be able to contact you if serious situations arise in the country in which you are staying).
  • Contact your insurance company to find out whether you have the right insurance policies before your departure.
  • Remember to order a student visa in good time and check that your passport does not expire while you are away.
  • Make sure to print at least one copy of your passport, your visa, insurance information and vaccination card in case you lose them.
  • Check what the culture is like in your destination country and city. Check whether there are, for example, special cultural customs and norms about how to dress that you should be aware of. Find out whether there are places you should not go to and how you can most safely travel around the country and the city.
  • Write down all important contact details for, for example, insurance companies and possibly your doctor in Denmark

Checklist during your stay
  • Find out how to contact a doctor, hospital, fire department, the police and possibly campus security during your stay and save the telephone numbers on your mobile.
  • Write home on a regular basis, so that your family and friends know that you are well.
  • Notify your friends and family if you go on trips away from the place where you normally live and stay.
  • Always have your phone on you – especially if you are on trips.
  • Take good care of yourself – and make sure you know where you are.
  • Look after your things – but remember that your safety is more important.

Culture shock is common
Remember that it is common to experience a culture shock when moving to another country. This also applies even though you may not be far from Denmark.
If you are overwhelmed, it is a good idea to structure your everyday life with, for example, running, study time and social activities. Preferably with other foreign students. You are very likely not the only one to feel that way. Check out the possibilities for support and guidance offered at your host university. Contact the UCPH Study Guidance.
