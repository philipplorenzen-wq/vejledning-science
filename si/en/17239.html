The academic council is the faculty's top collegiate body. The council has 10 members:

  • the dean
  • 6 academic staff
  • 3 student representatives.

3 observers from the administration also attend all academic council meetings.

Members of the academic council advise the dean on strategy, financial matters and strategic recruitment issues.


Examples of duties of the academic council:

  • Prepare job ads for academic posts
  • Appoint assessment committees for academic positions and doctoral theses.
  • Contribute to awarding academic degrees such as PhDs and doctoral degrees.

You can see the council's rules of procedure and meeting dates, read minutes or see the current members of the council on the faculty page on KUnet.

You are always welcome to contact the student members of the academic council, as they are your representatives.
