The feeling of having found the right field for us and of mastering something is a strong driving force. Conversely, it can really undermine your motivation if you have doubts about your ability to master your studies. 

Mastering your studies involves: 

  • feeling that you are able to do what you need to – e.g. a specific assignment or course or studying in general
  • sensing that people around you believe you can handle your studies, e.g. your fellow students, teachers and supervisor.
  • being able to recognise yourself, your driving force and your interests among fellow students or teachers. 


The feeling of mastering your studies is thus the essential feeling of being able to handle the challenges you encounter along the way. It does not necessarily have anything to do with your grades or the grades achieved by others.

3 tips to enhance the feeling of mastering your studies 

  1. Write down your successes in student life. For example, that you:
    - got through a difficult course
    - gave a presentation to your fellow students that you had been nervous about
    - put your academic skills to practical use in a student job or a project.
  2. Remind yourself of the things you have overcome so far, even though it was difficult for you.
  3. Ask your fellow students, if they also find certain things difficult on your study programme. Most students struggle academically at times, without it affecting their ability to master their studies in general.
