If it is assessed that you have cheated at an exam, the typical consequences for a first-time occurrence are as follows:


•    You are expelled from the exam in question.
•    You are expelled from the University for typically one semester.


and


•    You are given a written warning.


Each case is assessed and decided individually on its own merits.
If you have violated the regulations several times, it will be seen as an aggravating circumstance, and this will be reflected in the sanction. This may involve a longer or permanent expulsion from the University.

Expulsion from the exam

If you are expelled from an exam, your assignment will not be assessed, and you will be automatically assigned the lowest grade possible and it will count as an exam attempt.

Expulsion from the University

If you are expelled from the University, you will be excluded from all courses and exams as well as all social and academic activities at the University.


You will be expelled for a limited period of time or permanently.


You will not receive SU while you are expelled. If you have any questions about SU, please contact the SU-office
