You can withdraw from the project if you do so before the deadline for the block in which you were due to start the project. This is the same deadline as for submitting the project contract.
After that deadline, you will need a dispensation to withdraw and can only withdraw from the exam. You can apply for a dispensation due to unusual circumstance, like illness or death in the family (see the bottom of this text).
You cannot postpone the submission of your report – you can only apply to withdraw from the exam. If you do not withdraw from the exam and you don’t submit your report before the submission date, you will use an exam attempt.

Your options for proceeding with the project in practice 

You have 2 options when you are to proceed with your project – regardless of whether you have withdrawn from the exam or you have used an exam attempt.

1) Continue with the same project by:
  • agreeing with your supervisor on when you will restart the project
  • registering for the same project
  • submitting your report – possibly in a revised version that stays within your problem formulation
  • attending the oral exam.
2) Start a new project by following the same procedure as last time:
  • Find a new topic and possibly a company.
  • Find a new supervisor, if necessary.
  • Complete and submit a new project contract.

How to apply for a dispensation to be withdrawn from an exam after expiry of the deadline 

After expiry of the deadline for withdrawal from the exam, you may apply for a dispensation for withdrawal if you can document extraordinary circumstances. 

Examples of extraordinary circumstances may be:

  • your own serious illness
  • serious illness or death in your immediate family
  • problems in the company or research group with which you are cooperating
  • problems with your supervisor (illness, serious cooperation difficulties or the like). 
How to apply for a dispensation: 
  1. Obtain documentation for your situation, such as a medical certificate, a note from your psychologist, or a statement from your supervisor, head of studies, contact in your company, etc. 
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Write a statement describing the extraordinary circumstances that prevent you from submitting the report/taking the exam. 
  3. Prepare a study plan for your entire course of study showing how you intend to complete your degree programme. Use the study planning tool under Programme structure. 
  4. Complete an application via ‘Apply for a dispensation’ and attach your documentation, reasons and study plan.
