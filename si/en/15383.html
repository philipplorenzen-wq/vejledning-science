Do I need to meet the SU activity requirement if I am on parental leave?

Remember to apply for childbirth portions for the period of your maternity/paternity leave.

If you receive single or double childbirth portions during your maternity/paternity leave, you do not need to meet the SU study activity requirement during this period. In respect to the SU study activity inspection, the number of grant portions you have received will remain the same as before you started receiving childbirth portions.

However, if you receive mixed portions (for example a standard grant portion and a childbirth portion), your use of grant portions in relation to  the SU study activity control will increase by one grant portion for each month you receive mixed portions.

In order to receive childbirth portions, you must not have been declared inactive in the previous month to receiving childbirth portions. This means that if you are already declared inactive, you cannot receive childbirth portions until you are no longer delayed in your studies and are again declared study active.

Example:

Lisa is pregnant with the baby due in October. She takes maternity leave from her studies from September to August; two semesters.

Lisa is applying for single childbirth portions in the period from September to May. Hereafter she will resume her studies.

Lisa meets the requirement for study activity while she is on maternity leave, because she receives single childbirth portions during her leave, which eliminates the study activity requirement during this period.

Before Lisa went on maternity leave, she had received 24 standard SU grant portions, and when her maternity leave ends she will still be registered as having received 24 standard grant portions.
