In order to learn and absorb new and unfamiliar material, you have to be targeted and curious about everything you do not understand. 

The best way to test your understanding of the material is to put it into your own words. 

What you can do: 

  1. Try to explain what you have read to:
    - your study group
    - a friend
    - a plant
    - your reflection in the mirror. 
  2. Pay attention to where you have most difficulty explaining the material. This is usually where the gaps in your understanding of the material lie. 

Once you have found these gaps in your understanding, you know: 

  • what areas you might need to read a bit more about and perhaps discuss with your study group 
  • what you need to ask your lecturer, supervisor or teacher about.
