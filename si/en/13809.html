  1. Contact UCPH

    When you apply for SPS for dyslexia you have to:
    1. Fill in and send the information form
    2. check if you need to apply for special conditions

    You do not need to book an appointment for an interview if it is only about you wanting to apply for SPS.
    If you have a diagnosis in addition to your dyslexia, it is a good idea to book an interview with the UCPH Study Guidance. Then your SPS will be adapted to all your needs for support.

    See how to book an appointment with UCPH Study Guidance here.

  2. Apply for SPS support

    You should fill in and return the forms we send to you.

    UCPH applies for the support and/or aids that you have applied for.

  3. Message in your e-Boks after 3-6 weeks

    3-6 weeks later, you will be notified via e-Boks when your application has been processed.

    UCPH will send a message to KVUC if you are to be tested for dyslexia or interviewed about your needs for support. KVUC is responsible for dyslexia testing, support and assessment. 


    If you are not to be tested for dyslexia or interviewed about support needs, you can go to item 6. 

  4. KVUC contacts you

    KVUC will contact you via e-Boks within 10 days to arrange for an interview.

    If you are dyslexic:

    You will only be recommended for an assessment of support needs if it is unclear what kind of support you need. The assessment is made by KVUC to identify whether there are any additional aids that may be useful for your studying at the university.  

    If you suspect that you are dyslexic:

    KVUC offers to test you for dyslexia. If you are dyslexic, KVUC will make a report stating which support will be best for you.

  5. UCPH applies for the recommended support for you

    UCPH applies for the recommended support.

  8. Notification in your e-Boks

    You will be notified in your e-Boks when your application has been processed. It typically takes 3-6 weeks.  

    UCPH will order the support or aids that have been recommended for you. It is Nota, Atea and KVUC that provide the support or aids.

  9. Now your aids are ready

    You will receive an email from Atea with information about downloading IT programs. Remember to contact KVUC once you have received the programs so that you can get instructions.

    You must pick up any physical aids at Njalsgade 72, 7. sal (vest), 2300 København K.

    Please note that when using a dictaphone, you must obtain permission from the lecturer and your fellow students before you can use it in classes.

  10. IT instruction and/or study support hours at KVUC

    If you need instruction and/or study support hours, an IT instructor and/or dyslexia consultant from KVUC will contact you. Then you can start working together.
