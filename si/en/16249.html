The Master's programme in Geography and Geoinformatics is a two year programme consisting of 3 specialisations: Physical Geography, Geoinformatics and Human Geography. Each specialisation consists of two mandatory courses each counting 7.5 ECTS, one placed in block 1 and one in block 2.

See how the programme is structured

Between 30 to 45 ECTS credits (depending on the size of your Master's thesis) are to be used for restricted elective courses. Within each specialisation a minimum of 15 ECTS credits must be taken within a specific category of courses that are very closely linked to the specialization. In addition, you have 15 ECTS freely elective courses, which can be selected from the Geography and Geoinformatics programme or another Master’s programme.

Find restricted elective course listings under your programme structure.

Search for courses provided by University of Copenhagen in the course database.

If you are unsure which restricted elective and elective courses that fit your interest you can contact me, talk to fellow students or contact UCPH Study Guidance.

Courses in the programme are structured as class room teaching, teaching in auditoriums, and for some courses in laboratories and in the field. The classes are very much dependent on student interaction. Therefore you are expected to prepare well for the classes and take active part in theoretical, methodological and empirical discussions during class.
