Find the right topic for your thesis

The master’s thesis is your opportunity to work with a topic that interests you and which you want to work with for an extended period.Your topic needs to fall within the field of landscape architecture and live up to the description of the learning outcomes of the master’s thesis in the curriculum. Most of the students opt for a project-oriented master’s thesis with an experimental design part. It is also possible to work on a theoretical topic, but you must show how it is incorporated into landscape architecture practice.Before deciding on a topic, I recommend that you think about what you want to get out of your master’s thesis: Would you like to benefit from the experimental freedom that the university offers you, e.g., for a critical or utopian project or a research topic that you have defined yourself? Would you like to create “a business card” for your future career by demonstrating your skills through a project that can help you land your dream job? Or do you want to learn something new, e.g., certain design methods, a certain subject area that you want to delve into or a certain profile you want to strengthen? These considerations can help you focus and determine how you want to approach your thesis.Be curious and seek inspiration throughout your studies from day one. Talk to your fellow students, discuss with teachers and lecturers that you find inspiring. You are also welcome to arrange an informal discussion with me as the head of studies.Read more about choosing a topic.

Find your thesis supervisor

All senior staff employed at SCIENCE can act as a primary supervisor for your thesis. PhD students can provide practical and academic support as co-supervisors on specialised topics.If you want to work with a project-oriented master’s thesis, be sure to choose a supervisor with design competencies. It may be a good idea to find a primary supervisor among the staff you have met in your courses and with whom you have had good supervision experiences or who have inspired you. However, if you want to delve into a new subject or method, you might prefer to seek a supervisor or co-supervisor with expertise in that subject.One way to find a suitable supervisor is to discuss a rough project description with two or three potential supervisors and use that to come to an agreement with your preferred supervisor. Another option may be to approach someone you would like to work with and develop a project together that he/she is particularly interested in.

Get off to a good start with your thesis

From 2022 we offer a common thesis framework for master’s thesis students in landscape architecture. We begin with a start-up event in September with general information about the thesis and the possibility to meet supervisors and former students who share and discuss their experiences. In the week between block 1 and block 2, we offer a workshop to support the development of your thesis programme and project description. In block 3, we organise a midway seminar. Finally, we aim to arrange an exhibition and festive celebration of thesis projects that will also serve as a matchmaking event with professional networks and potential future employers in Denmark.
