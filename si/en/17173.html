Make sure to check that your passport is valid for the whole duration of your stay abroad well in advance of your stay.
As a minimum, your passport must be valid for the whole stay. Some countries require that your passport is valid for up to six months after you have returned home to Denmark.
Check whether you must submit your passport to the embassy for a shorter or longer period in connection with your visa application. Whether you need to submit your passport and for how long differs from country to country.  Therefore, you should plan your application for a student visa well in advance. Without a student visa, you cannot be sure that you will be allowed to enter the country.
If you are not a Danish citizen
If you have a temporary residence permit in Denmark and are NOT an EU/EEA citizen, you risk losing your residence permit in Denmark if you leave Denmark for an extended period.
If you lose your residence permit, this means that you no longer have the right to stay in Denmark. This means that you must apply for a new residence permit, which can be both cumbersome and time-consuming.
Read more about the rules for residence permits on the website, New to Denmark.
