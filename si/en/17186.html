How do I find and apply for a student job?

You must use the same methods regardless of whether you apply for a job or project collaboration as a student or for your first job as a graduate.
Read about finding and applying for jobs and projects
However, there are also some special considerations and some specific research that you can do in your hunt for a student job.

Consider: What do you want to get out of a student job?

  • Do you need labour market experience in general?
  • Would you like to acquire insight into a special industry or job type?
  • Would you like to acquire specific academic or personal competences?
  • How many hours and when would you like to work?
  • Are your wishes realistic in relation to possible student jobs and your current competences?

Find employers you can send unsolicited applications to

Unsolicited application is often a fruitful approach. Many student jobs are never posted because employers hire people who contact them themselves or who are recommended to them by, for example, current student assistants.
2 places you can look for potential employers 
  • Ask your fellow students about their student jobs – they may be of relevance and perhaps they can recommend you?
  • Ask your lecturers whether they know some good student workplaces – perhaps they need students in their research section or know someone who does?
