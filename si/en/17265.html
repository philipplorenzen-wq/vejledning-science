Although sometimes frustrating, unemployment is also a chance to

  • learn more about what is important to you in a job
  • get to know interesting new people to add to your network
  • improve your job searching skills.

And there are many meaningful things you can do with your time

3 tips to help you in your job searching period

  1. Meet new people and expand your network
    Reach out and expand your network e.g. by participating in trade union events. When you go out in the world unplanned opportunities sometimes occur. So try to meet new people and stay open and curious about the people and possibilities you meet.
    You can also connect with new people online via LinkedIn. 
    Read more about using LinkedIn for networking
  2. Use your support network
    It can be lonely to search for jobs. So it may be a good idea to start a job searching group to meet up with e.g. with people from your study programme.
    Also remember to reach out to friends, family or others who can support you and cheer you on.
  3. Do things that spark your energy
    Job search is hard work and therefore you must remember to take breaks and do the things that give you energy.
    In the end, this will help you to do a better job searching for jobs.
