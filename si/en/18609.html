What dyslexia programs are available on the computers on the UCPH exam house?

Computers in the UCPH exam house have:

  • CD-ORD
  • AppWriter Windows
  • IntoWords

Do you need help to apply for dispensation? 

Contact UCPH Study Guidance

Read more about special exam conditions

I use AppWriter Mac, and I am to sit an exam in the exam house. What should I do?

AppWriter Mac is not available on the computers in the exam house. Instead, you can use CD-ORD at the exam. 

You can also contact SPS and have your license switched to IntoWords. See how below.

Switching from AppWriter Mac to IntoWords

You can have your AppWriter MAC license permanently switched to IntoWords.

If you switch, you will get new support hours from KVUC on how to use IntoWords for your studies. 

Contact SPS if you want them to apply for the change of license.

Read more about IntoWords here (link in danish).

Please note that the processing time may be long.

Can I take the exam using my own computer?

You can use your own computer for your exam if you have been granted a dispensation from your study board. 

Do you need help to apply for dispensation? 

Contact UCPH Study Guidance on your programme.

What other aids can I bring to the exam as a dyslexic?

If you want to use your own headphones or hearing protectors at exams, you must apply for a dispensation from your study board.

If you want to borrow hearing protectors at the exam house, you must apply for a dispensation.

Borrowing standard headphones at the exam house does not require dispensation.

Borrowing earplugs at the exam house does not require dispensation.

Do you need help to apply for dispensation?

Contact UCPH Study Guidance

Read more about special exam conditions


What is a terminology List?

Terminology lists help you write difficult or uncommon words that may not automatically appear in the word suggestion lists of standard dictionaries. A terminology list is designed to make it easier for you with dyslexia to spell subject-specific and technical terms.
A terminology list is defined as:
A list of single words, compound words, and hyphenated words that are naturally related to a particular subject or field.
A terminology list does not contain definitions, explanations of concepts, sentences, or notes.
If you are unable to access your terminology list through your assistive technology software, please make sure that your document contains only subject-specific terms and not definitions, notes, or sentences. The terms must be arranged in alphabetical order.

Can I bring terminology lists to the exam?

You can bring your terminology lists to the exam.

At exams without aid, you will need a dispensation due to dyslexia.

If you have been granted a dispensation for extended time due to dyslexia, you are allowed to use terminology lists.


Access to terminology lists on the computers at the Exam House CD-ORD and IntoWords:
  • You must bring your terminology lists on a USB stick or upload them to digital notes in advance.
  • You can also use a list in .lst file format. Text documents such as .txt, .pdf and .docx will work fine for both programs.
  • The staff at the Exam House can help you with importing the terminology lists. Please, make sure to arrive in good time.

 AppWriter Windows:

Log in with your own license key and access your terminology lists on the computers in the Exam House .


Before exam

You must bring your SPS login, meaning your username and password which you have received from Atea. Bring this to the exam. If you use AppWriter Windows, you will need to bring your license key for the program.

You can find your username and password in the menu at sps flexvalg. (link in Danish).

If you need help, contact Atea's support by phone: +45 70 21 12 86 and select option 2.  


At the exam

Arrive in good time before the exam starts.

Open the program and log on. If you need help, contact Atea's support by phone : +45 70 21 12 86 and select option 2.
IntoWords: On the login page, choose MV-login. Tick the box Remember me (if available). Then you can work offline.

How do I make a terminology list?

You can contact KVUC if you need help making terminology lists.

Nota

Nota is a collection of digital materials for students with special needs. You can access it if you are registered as a user and have a username and password from your SPS grant.

It is now possible to access the portal nota.dk from the Exam House computers.

You can check if nota is accessible for your ITX exam by viewing the list of permitted resources in Digital Exam. If you're uncertain, please contact your exam administration to clarify if nota.dk will be available during your exam

Please note that access to Nota is granted via SPS. If you need support, please contact Nota.dk directly.

Contact Nota here (link in Danish).

NB! Some of the study materials in Nota can be locked, you nee to use the program Thorium Reader to open them by providing your Thorium login information (it can be a different login information than the one you use to log on to Nota). Thorium Reader is available at the Exams House.

You can only open a book from Nota on three devices. An exam computer is a device and it is possible, that access is closed during your exam. You need to be secure that you will be able to open your books during the exam. Contact Nota, if you are in doubt.

Thorium Reader has a function for making annotations. If you write annotations on your own computer, you will need to:

  1. Export them from Thorium Reader on your own computer
  2. Upload them as digital notes to your exam (or have them with you on a USB, if digital notes are not allowed during your exam). 
  3. You will then be able to import them to Thorium Reader on your exam computer. 

Check Nota's guide for export and import of annotations (in Danish) here.
