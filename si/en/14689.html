As a graduate student, you are expected to take responsibility for your own education. This means making optimal choices during the programme and composing a study plan that reflects your interests. There are many different options to consider, when making your study plan. You can read more about recommended study tracks for each specialisation below.

It is your own responsibility to be aware of deadlines for registering for courses and exams and where the lectures/exercises and exams take place. All important messages about your study programme and deadlines are announced as a study message, on the front page of KUnet. It is very important that you read these messages on a regular basis. 

Engage yourself, both academically and socially in the daily life as an MSc student. I hope you will find inspiration and enjoy your studies at Niels Bohr Institute!

Here is some good advice for your studies:

  • Participate in the Welcome Programme. See programme and details.
  • The MSc study is a full-time job. You should expect to spend 40-45 hours per week on your studies. It is important that you structure your everyday life to accommodate this.
  • You need to know programme structure and its rules. Read more about how to plan your studies here. 
  • The teachers and the teaching committee are dependent on feedback from students to improve the courses and curriculum. Please take some minutes to evaluate the courses after each block.
  • You must avoid plagiarism and cheating at exams. Violation can lead to exclusion from the University. The rules for what is considered plagiarism may be different from what you are used to, so read about cheating and plagiarism.
  • You can participate in academic events such as Study events , department seminar and conferences. All research groups at NBI welcome MSc students at their seminars. Check the research group pages at www.nbi.ku.dk.
  • Read your study messages and study events not to miss important messages and events.
  • You can involve yourself in student politics.
  • You should involve yourself in social activities: Christmas dinner, Galla party, FysikRevy™ (satire show by students), Legestuen (workshop playground for students), Student Cafés, etc. Keep updated through the Council of Physics students and their facebook site , or the general facebook group for Physics at UCPH
  • Try to avoid stress. You must figure out how to get relaxed and refilled with energy. Read more about stress.
  • You can sign up for sport activities at campus at student friendly prices.
  • Are you an international student? You may experience you first oral exams and may want to practice before the oral exam. Read about good exam technique, and participate in the oral exam workshop held at NBI in block 1. This workshop will be announced for all first year students of the MSc.
  • Around the time between blocks 2 and 3, there is a Master's Student Symposium where you can present your Master's project at an early stage. This meeting is great fun and can help you get feedback. As an example, follow the link to the 2026 event.
  • If you need guidance on your study plan, rules and regulations or on career, contact UCPH Study Guidance.
