If you believe your application for a credit transfer of courses from another Danish educational institution (including other faculties at UCPH) has been unfairly rejected, you can appeal the academic decision to a credit transfer board of appeals.


The decision may be based on the academic grounds

  • that the academic level of the desired course is not high enough
  • that the academic content does not fall within the scope of your study programme.


How to appeal the academic grounds for a decision

Send your appeal to studyguidance-science@adm.ku.dk no later than 2 weeks after you have been notified of the Study Board's decision. In exceptional circumstances, you may, in some cases, be allowed to submit your complaint after expiry of this deadline.
Your appeal must be made in writing and must contain

  1. a description of what you are complaining about
  2. an explanation of the academic grounds on which you find that the course for which you wish to get a credit transfer covers the academic core competences you want.


Processing of your appeal

Once your appeal has been received, the Dean will appoint a board of appeals consisting of

  • a representative of the academic staff associated with your study programme or a related study programme at the University
  • and a representative of the academic staff associated with your study programme or a related study programme at another university.


Possible outcomes of your case

The board of appeals may

  • grant a credit transfer for more programme elements than approved by the Study Board
  • reject your appeal
  • submit the appeal for renewed consideration by the Study Board (rarely happens).


Reply to your appeal

You will get a reply when the board of appeals has submitted its decision to the University. The Study Board must notify the University as soon as possible – and no more than 1 month after the Dean's appointment of the board of appeals (July does not count due to the summer holidays).
You will be notified if the deadline cannot be met for some reason. You will also be informed about the reasons for the delay and when the processing of your appeal is expected to be concluded.


Complaining about the processing of your appeal

You cannot complain about the academic assessment on which the board of appeal has based its decision, but you can complain to The Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science if you believe that your appeal has not been processed in accordance with the rules – i.e. about legal issues.

This may, for example, be the case if you think the board of appeals has

  • applied incorrect rules
  • has attached importance to factually incorrect information
  • has not considered important arguments in your application.

The Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science cannot take a position on the board of appeals' assessment of whether exceptional circumstances exist in your case. Nor does the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science normally assess whether the decision is generally reasonable or appropriate.


How to complain to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science

If you wish to complain about legal issues, you must adress it to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science. You send the complaint to UCPH Study Guidance at studyguidance-science@adm.ku.dk. UCPH Study Guidance will forward your complaint to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science with a statement.


The deadline for complaining about legal issues is 2 weeks from the day on which you were notified of the decision.

Processing of your appeal

You will receive an email in your KUmail when the Study Board has prepared a statement in the case, which will be enclosed with the complaint as it is sent to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science. You have at least 1 week to comment on the Study Board's statement. The Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science will decide the case on this basis.

Reply to your appeal

You cannot appeal against the decision made by the Danish Agency for Science and Higher Education in your case.
Please note, all complaint options and specific deadlines will be specified in the emails you receive in your KUmail in connection with your complaint.
