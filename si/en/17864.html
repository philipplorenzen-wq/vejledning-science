You can largely design your Master's thesis according to your academic interests, but within a fixed framework.

The thesis must include:

  1. Your independent study of a topic/problem/issue within your field of study – for example working with a research unit or a company
     
  2. A thesis report in which you formulate, analyse and process your material
     Read more about your thesis report
  3. An oral defence based on the thesis report which you have handed in.
     Read more about the oral defence


Master's thesis purpose and requirements

In your Master's thesis, you must demonstrate your ability to work with an academic problem which is central to your study.

Depending on the size of your thesis, there are different requirements for the content:

  • If you are writing a thesis of 30 ECTS points, it can be purely theoretical but may also be experimental.
  • If you are writing a thesis of 45 or 60 ECTS points, it must be experimental.

See which specific purposes and requirements your Master's thesis must meet in the programme-specific section of your curriculum.
