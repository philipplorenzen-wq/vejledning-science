The master programme is extremely short – only two years with the Master's thesis taking up 3 out of 4 blocks of the second year. I therefore recommend that you:

  • Consider the study programme as your work and allocate the necessary time (approx. 45 hours per week).
  • Familiarize yourself with the study programme and the rules you will have to follow. . This includes e.g. the deadlines for registration of courses and exams and very importantly, the rules to avoid plagiarism and cheating.
  • Many students find it helpful to form study groups. Get inspiration for your study group.
  • We are continuously adapting and improving the courses. You are therefore encouraged to give feedback to course responsible teachers, e.g. during course evaluations.
  • Use your influence to improve the study programme and the study environment e.g. through participation in the study board, the departmental education committee, or the student organisation called FEF.
  • Prepare for the time after your studies. 

In addition, I will recommend that you keep yourself informed about what's happening in the study programme throughout the year. In particular, I encourage you to:

  • check your KUmail regularly (preferably daily)
  • join the lunch meetings or other meetings that will be arranged, mostly in the first year of the study
  • read your study messages and study events
  • check out the SPOT ON events
