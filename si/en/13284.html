What does inclusion really mean at UCPH?

Inclusion is about the degree to which everyone feels a sense of community and belonging despite different starting points and circumstances, and that cultures, structures and norms are inclusive for everyone regardless of background, orientation and needs.
In other words: inclusion is about how you experience your ability to be yourself and whether you feel recognised and respected in your study programme. The inclusion survey asked about everyday situations, such as how you perceive your opportunities to voice your opinion, receive feedback, and generally collaborate with your fellow students. The survey also asked if you experience challenges with, for example, language or physical and digital accessibility in your study programme.
Diversity, equity and inclusion are a prerequisite for UCPH’s general purpose and activities and for the work with the University's strategy 2030, Creating benefit for more people.
