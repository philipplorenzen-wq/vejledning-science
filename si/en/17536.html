The following project types are examples of what is possible. You might have another idea. If so, submit it. Remember to work with your supervisor to develop and better define your project.


Study within your field

Objective: To explore an academic field in depth and gain specialised theoretical and/or methodological knowledge.

Content/ form: You initiate the project because your own subject area curiosity is what is pivotal to the project. You will be able to organise and orient the project towards a subject area of deep interest for you and operationalise theories and methods in relation to issues that are of concern to you.

The project may be entirely theoretical in nature or a combination of theory with more practical elements.


Do research assistance or something similar

Objective: Involvement with research related collaboration

Content/form: If you have the opportunity to participate in a research group at a relevant department or faculty, you may create a project that deals with one or even numerous research group projects. 

While completing independent tasks is important, you should also engage in dialogue and collaborate with the research group with regards to processes and results.

Depending on the project's scope and duration, evaluation may be in the form of a written article, public presentation or report.
