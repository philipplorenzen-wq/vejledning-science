You will receive a KUmail from International Education with information on how your grant will be disbursed when you have been given an exchange place through Erasmus+.
The Erasmus+ grant is calculated based on the period of time where you are physically present at the host university and have obligations at the host university (meaning: intro weeks, lecture period, exams).

 We grant you an Erasmus grant for the official semester (meaning: intro weeks, lecture period, exams) at your host university prior to your Erasmus exchange.

 We will re-calculate the Erasmus grant, when you have completed your exchange and we know how many days you were physically present and had obligations at the host university.

 This means that you can never be granted a larger Erasmus grant than the one calculated based on the official semester dates prior to your Erasmus exchange.

How you will receive your Erasmus+ grant
Your Erasmus+ grant is dibursed to your NemKonto account in two instalments: before you leave Denmark and when you return to Denmark after your exchange.


What to do before leaving
You must upload three documents to the application portal to receive payment of the first part of your Erasmus grant (70%). The three documents are:

  • Your pre-approval
    The pre-approval of your courses is your guarantee that your courses abroad can be credit transferred to your UCPH study programme when you return home. 
  • Your Learning Agreement
    Your Learning Agreement is a supplement to your pre-approval and is a kind of education agreement that you enter into with the host university. It must be signed by you, your Erasmus coordinator and your host university.
    You cannot complete the Learning Agreement until you have received your pre-approval from UCPH.
    You will find a guide how ro fill out your Learning Agreement under the sektion 'before mobility' in Mobility Online.
  • Your Erasmus+ Grant Agreement
    The Erasmus+ Grant Agreement is a contract that describes the terms under which you will receive your Erasmus+ grant. It should only to be signed by you. You will receive your Erasmus+ Grant Agreement in June, if you go on exchange in the autumn semester and in November, if you go on exchange in the spring semester.

Besides these documents, you must complete a placement test, to assess your language skills.

You receive information about all describe in this section in the semester, before you go on exchange.

What to remember after you have returned to Denmark
The second part of your Erasmus grant will be disbursed when UCPH has approved your evalution of the Erasmus+ grant after you have returned to Denmark. You will receive an email in your Kumail when  it is time to evaluate your stay.

Please note: UCPH is only allowed to give you an Erasmus grant for the period of time where you are physically present at the host university and have obligations at the host university (meaning: intro weeks, lecture period, exams). This means that you must expect that your Erasmus grants will be re-calculated when you evaluate your Erasmus grant by the end your Erasmus exchange. You will therefore know the final Erasmus scholarship when the evaluation of your Erasmus scholarship has been approved.

This means that you can never be granted a larger Erasmus grant than the one calculated based on the official semester dates prior to your Erasmus exchange.
