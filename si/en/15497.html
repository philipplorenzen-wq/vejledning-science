There are multiple ways to complete the Master’s programme in Molecular Biomedicine, and you therefore have to make a number of choices during the programme. My recommendation is that you choose based on interest. If you have questions about rules and regulations, your options for planning your studies and choices you have to make, contact UCPH Study Guidance. If you have questions about my academic recommendations on this page, feel free to contact me.

Advice on how to be a well-functioning student in the Master’s programme:

  • Your programme is your job! You are expected to work 35-45 hours a week on your schedules teaching, course preparation and Master’s thesis project.
  • Consider forming a study group with fellow students to discuss and solve course-related topics, i.e. assignments and challenging topics.
  • Avoid exam cheating. It can have serious consequences for your enrolment at the University of Copenhagen.
  • Remember to evaluate your courses at the end of every block. Your evaluation is the most important tool for the continuous improvement of a course.
  • You will have a lot to do during your Master’s studies. Make sure to dedicate time for relaxation and recharging to avoid stress. See more about student life at UCPH here.
  • Read your study messages and study events not to miss important messages and events.
