If you are feeling stressed, it may be a good idea to look into and challenge the expectations you are trying to meet – yours and those of others, whether articulated or not. 

Examples of expectations: 

  • having to get top marks in all exams 
  • having to read everything for every course 
  • wanting to actively participate in multiple associations and launch initiatives in your study environment. 

Where do your expectations come from? 

If you have difficulty understanding where the expectations you are struggling with come from – and whether they make sense in your situation – you can do this short exercise:

1) Answer the following questions and write down your answers:
  • Who says these things are important?
  • Why is it important for you to live up to these expectations? 
  • What will happen if you fail to meet the expectations? 
2) Then reflect on the following:
  • Is it realistic to meet them given the time and resources you have available right now? Consider all the things happening in your life – including outside your studies. 

  • What would you say to a friend who felt the same expectations – from themselves or others? 

  • What do you actually most need to prioritise right now in order to thrive in your student life?
