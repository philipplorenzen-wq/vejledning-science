Your academic internship must fulfill all criteria described below if you want to apply for an Erasmus Traineeship grant.

1. Definition of Erasmus Traineeship
In your academic internship, you must perform tasks and have a working week of approximately 37 hours to meet the EU's definition of a Traineeship (work placement) and thus be eligible for a grant.

This also applies if you are doing an academic internship at a university (for example as an research assistant).

Fieldwork is not considered as a “work placement", and you cannot receive an Erasmus Traineeship-grant for conducting fieldwork.

2. Enrollment at UCPH:
You must be enrolled at UCPH throughout your academic internship (from application period to evaluation period). You cannot apply for an Erasmus Traineeship as a recent graduate in the academic year 2022-2023.

3. Host country
The academic internship must take place in an EU Member country or a country participating in the Erasmus+ programme (see list of countries above). You cannot receive an Erasmus grant for Denmark or the country you live in during your studies at UCPH (example: if you live in Sweden, you cannot receive an Erasmus grant for an academic internship in Sweden or Denmark).

4. Host institution
The host institution cannot be an EU institution or an institution that administrates EU programme funds (e.g. the European Commission). However, you may work as an intern for a Danish MEP in the European Parliament.

5.Duration 
The academic internship must last between 2-12 months. You can participate in Erasmus exchange (studies + traineeship) for max 12 months per Bachelor- and Master.  For example: if you have been on Erasmus study exchange for 6 months during your Bachelor's Degree, you can participate in Erasmus Traineeship for max 6 months, if it takes place during your Bacelor's Degree

6.ECTS
The academic internship must be credit bearing. At University of Copenhagen we do not give Erasmus Traineeship-grants as a voluntary traineeship that does not award any credits (second option in the learning agreement in Table B). Therefore you must be able to obtain pre-approval or other approval of the equivalent of a full-time study programme (30 ECTS credits/semester ) for the academic internship.

If you fail to do so, your grant will be reduced – unless you can document that there is a valid reason why you have received pre-approval for less than 30 ECTS credits per semester. A valid reason may, for example, be if you have less than 30 ECTS credits per semester left for an academic internship, or that you have compulsory courses that you must take at UCPH.

7. Minimum requirements for ECTS
Your academic internship must be approved for at least 15 ECTS per semester, if you want to apply for the Erasmus grant. If you have less than 15 ECTS per semester you cannot apply for the Erasmus grant. If you have less than 15 ECTS per semester for an academic internship, the total duration of the academic internship must be accordingly shorter. For example: You have 5 ECTS for an academic internship, then the total duration of the academic internship must be 2 months. This rule applies, if your academic internship starts after 1st of September 2022.

8. Insurance 
You can only receive an Erasmus Traineeship grant if you have third-party liability and accident insurance. Ordinary travel insurance is not enough, as the insurance must cover you in a 'work-like relationship'/internship. If relevant, check whether you are covered by third-party liability and accident insurance at the place of your academic internship. You are not covered by the University of Copenhagen.

UCPH does not require documentation of your insurance if your traineeship begins in the autumn of 2025 or later.

9. Following courses at a higher education institution cannot be considered as a traineeship
If you apply for an Erasmus Traineeship grant at another European university you are not allowed to be enrolled in and participate in exams at the host university during your stay. In this case you must apply for an Erasmus exchange for studies.

Stay longer than one semester due to Denmark's EU Presidency:

If your academic internship is part of Denmark's EU Presidency in 2025 and therefore lasts longer than one semester, please note the following:

The University of Copenhagen has been in contact with the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science, who confirms that UCPH may grant a full scholarship for a stay longer than one semester (approximately 5-6 months), even if the stay only provides 30 ECTS.

However, the following requirements must be met:

  1. Duration: It is not possible to arrange a stay that only lasts 5-6 months. Therefore, we require documentation from the host institution to confirm this. For example, the Learning Agreement that you upload with your application for the Erasmus Traineeship scholarship should indicate that the stay must exceed 5-6 months.
  2. ECTS: It is not possible for you to obtain more than 30 ECTS for the stay, even if it lasts longer than 5-6 months. Therefore, we need documentation confirming this. For example, it can be an email from a study secretary/study advisor confirming that the stay can only be approved for 30 ECTS, even if the duration is more than one semester.
  3. Erasmus for max 12 months: Students may only participate in Erasmus stays for a maximum of 12 months per degree (BA or MA). For example, if you have previously been on an Erasmus study exchange for 5 months during your master's degree, you can only participate in an Erasmus Traineeship for 7 months during your master's degree. If your stay is longer than 7 months, you will not be able to apply for the Erasmus Traineeship scholarship, as the total number of months will exceed 12 months. This rule cannot be waived, for example, by granting a scholarship for fewer months than the actual stay, as it is the duration of the actual stay that counts.
