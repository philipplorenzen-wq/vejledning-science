The choice of company and topic are closely linked and have a significant impact on the nature of the project and what you gain from it.

3 considerations when choosing a topic/company

1) What do you want to achieve with your project?
  • Are you seeking specific academic outcomes? For example, particular methods you want to learn, a specific academic field you want to delve into, or a particular professional profile you want to strengthen.
  • Do you want to develop specific general skills? For example, your communication skills or your teamwork abilities in a group project.
  • Is there a specific type of workplace, job, or industry you want to learn more about or connect with?
2) Is the topic or the company/organization more important?
  • Do you have a concrete idea or a specific area of interest that you're passionate about working on?
  • Are you open to a topic formulated in collaboration with a specific company/organization or industry that you'd like to work with?
3) What type of workplace do you want to experience?
  • A private company, public institution, or nonprofit organisation?
  • A large workplace or a small one?
  • An established workplace or a small start-up?
  • Your own start-up, where you can test your business idea?
  • Are you interested in a particular type of corporate culture?
  • Should the workplace/employees closely align with your educational background, or do you want to try a place that's not directly related to your expertise?
