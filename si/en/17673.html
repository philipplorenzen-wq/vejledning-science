What happens if I complain about a fellow student?

The following happens when your complaint has been submitted:
1

The complaint will be sent to the student's head of department. 

2
The faculty processes the complaint in accordance with the UCPH rules of conduct for students.
3

The head of department may need to talk to you about what happened to be able to assess how the complaint should be handled.

You do not need to provide a detailed explanation. The important thing is that the head of department understands the gravity of the matter. We recommend that you bring a friend/family member/the student ambassador to the meeting.

4

After the head of department has received the complaint and talked to you (if relevant), the given student is notified about the content of the complaint and who lodged it.

The student is given an opportunity to comment – unless your complaint is deemed to be unfounded. You will not be able to see the statement from the student you have complained about.

5

When the student has commented on the complaint, the head of department will assess what the next step should be in consultation with the faculty.

6

Your complaint can result in three outcomes:

  • If management assesses that the incident is not serious enough to warrant disciplinary sanctions, the case will be closed. You will be informed by the head of department or the faculty that the case has been handled in accordance with the rules, but not what the result is. This is because management has a duty of confidentiality in disciplinary matters.
  • The student you have complained about may receive an oral or written warning or be expelled for a fixed period of time or permanently. Such sanctions are confidential, and the management or faculty may not inform you about this.  You will be informed that the case has been handled in accordance with the rules, but not what the result is.
  • The management may reject the complaint. In this case, you will be informed by the management or faculty.
