First and foremost, carefully consider what you are looking for in a supervisor. You will have to work together for a period of time.


Your primary supervisor should ideally have both

  • the academic qualifications needed to provide guidance on your topic (if you have any specific wishes regarding this)
  • the personal qualities which are important for your cooperation. 

You can't always have it all in one and the same person. Therefore, consider what kind of support you need most for this particular project.


How to find a supervisor

Four possible ways of choosing a supervisor: 

  • Take a look at your former lecturers and consider whether one of them could be a good match.
  • Check out the various research groups at SCIENCE, and see if anyone is working on something that overlaps with your academic interests.
    Find research groups at SCIENCE
  • Ask your head of studies who knows about the various academic competences on your study programme.
  • Ask your fellow students – for example ask someone who has come further in their studies than you have if they have any recommendations or ideas.


Company contact

In addition to your supervisor, you also need a contact at the company where you are doing your project. Your contact is responsible for your well-being at and your communication with the company.
