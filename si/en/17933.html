When you should start planning your stay will depend on the type of stay abroad you want to go on.


You must, for example, comply with UCPH's specific application deadlines if you want to go under an exchange agreement or to a summer school.

Correspondingly, there are important deadlines for self-arranged stays and academic internships.

Three phases in planning your stay

You must go through three phases in your planning:

Phase 1: Clarify your wishes, priorities and opportunities:     

  • Find out which countries, universities and study abroad types make the most sense under your curriculum.
  • Weigh with yourself your wishes and how best to achieve them.
  • Check agreements that you can apply for in Mobility Online

Phase 2: Apply for a stay at your host university

When you have found out how and where you want to go, it is crucial that you meet the deadlines and procedure applicable to the specific type of stay.

You must also pay attention to admission requirements, tuition fees, language requirements, etc.

Phase 3: Apply for admission and apply for pre-approval, grants and accommodation, etc.
Once you have been nominated for a place, the final part of your preparation begins.

Now you must:

  • apply for pre-approval for the courses/projects you want to take abroad
  • complete the Learning Agreement (Erasmus+ only)
  • find accommodation abroad and find out what to do with your home in Denmark
  • make sure you’re covered by your insurance(s) during your stay abroad
  • send your pre-approval to the State Education Grant (SU) Office (only if you are entitled to state education grant)
  • buy train, bus or plane tickets.

Depending on your destination, you may also need to:

  • apply for a visa or residence permit
  • be vaccinated
  • make language preparations
  • apply for grants.
