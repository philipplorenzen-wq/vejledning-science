What is the extent to which I can study at reduced hours for a special reason?

To find out the extent to which you may be able to study at reduced hours or take a break from your studies, you must first find out how much time off you need – and whether you will actually need to apply for a dispensation.


1) Prepare a study plan covering the whole of your study programme

Prepare a study plan which will allow you to study at a pace that you can manage in your present situation. If needed, you may keep a whole or several blocks free from scheduled teaching activities.
Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure. 


Important when preparing your study plan:

  • Be realistic about how much you can manage (consult e.g. your doctor, a psychologist, etc.).
  • Include all programme elements – including the ones you have passed.
  • Check that the various courses/exams are offered in the blocks in which you want to take them, and that you are not required to have taken other courses first (click on the course in the digital study plan and read the course description).

Make sure to add time in the plan for preparing for any re-exams.


2) Find out whether you meet the study activity requirements

Under each year of study in the digital study plan, it will be shown how many ECTS credits you have entered.
For each year of study, make sure you still meet

  • UCPH's study activity requirements, including:
    Maximum duration of study
  • SU study acticvity requirements

Now you know whether, and if so, how much you will be exceeding the study activity requirements and which UCPH requirements you need to apply for a dispensation from.


3) You may consult a study and career guidance counsellor about your study plan

If you need advice on your new study plan, you can contact a study and career guidance counsellor from UCPH Study Guidance.


4) Apply for the necessary dispensations

 Apply for the dispensations your new study plan requires if any.
You should initially only apply for what you need here and now and which you can document in your application in the form of a medical certificate or the like.
Read more about when and how to apply for a dispensation from the study activity requirements
 

If you prolong your completion time due to illness or the like, you may be able to apply for extra monthly SU grant portions.
Read more under SU and grants
