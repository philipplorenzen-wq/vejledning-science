As the only Danish university, the University of Copenhagen has a student ambassador. The student ambassador is a sort of ombudsman – an independent entity that works to secure your rights as a student. The student ambassador sees things from your perspective and has a duty of confidentiality.

You can contact the student ambassador to get impartial legal advice on matters related to your course of study. It may be that you need to clarify what your rights are in connection with a specific case with the University or what requirements you must meet.

For example, you may need guidance if you:

  • are considering applying for dispensation
  • are considering appealing against a decision or an exam
  • are suspected of exam cheating
  • encounter barriers related to functional impairment or serious illness
  • have experienced sexual harassment or other forms of offensive behaviour
  • feel that you have been treated unfairly
  • need mediation because a case is deadlocked
  • find yourself in a procedural grey area and don't know where to turn.
  • as a study-board member are in doubt about how a case should be dealt with

Read more at  www.studenterambassadoer.ku.dk
