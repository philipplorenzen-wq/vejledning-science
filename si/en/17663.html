Studies show that after 45 minutes of concentrated work, you need a break of about 15 minutes. To ensure that these breaks happen, it is a good idea to plan them before you start work. 

How to ensure you take good breaks:

  1. Write down a plan for your breaks. Plan small breaks of about 15 minutes each time you have worked for 30-45 minutes. And allow a longer break as well, e.g. for lunch.
  2. Set a timer on your phone to go off when you have a planned break.
  3. Work efficiently until the timer goes off and leave all interruptions aside until later. Try the exercise Put Interruptions Aside (on this page).

  4. Then take at least a 15-minute break.

  5. Repeat.

Use the break to recharge 

Make sure to spend your breaks doing something that gives you a real time-out. 

For example, you can create a nice break by: 

  • getting a cup of coffee 
  • going outside to get some fresh air 
  • speaking to a fellow student. 

It is a good idea to avoid using your phone or computer too much during your breaks. Otherwise your brain will not get the break it needs in order to focus and absorb a lot of knowledge.
