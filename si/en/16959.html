What courses can I replace with credit transfers?

It is usually most relevant to replace elective courses. In connection with your admission, an assessment was made of whether credits could be transferred from any previously uncompleted study programmes to replace either compulsory courses or restricted elective courses.

Credits cannot be transferred for courses to replace courses you have already passed or for which you are registered after the deadline for withdrawal. If you want credits transferred to replace a compulsory course based on an already completed course, you can apply for it, even if you are registered for the compulsory course and the deadline for withdrawal has passed.


Requirements for elective courses

If you are applying for credit transfers to replace elective courses on your new study programme, the courses must be at least at Master's level. However, you may do a certain number of courses at Bachelor's level.
See how many Bachelor's courses you may include in your course of study under Planning your studies


Requirements for restricted elective courses and compulsory courses

A number of specific requirements must be met by courses for which credit transfers are sought to replace restricted elective courses or compulsory courses. 

  • If a course is to replace a compulsory course: 
    There must be considerable overlaps between the academic content of the course and the content of the compulsory course that it is to replace. 
  • If a course is to count as a restricted elective course:
    The course must fall within the core academic content of your study programme.
