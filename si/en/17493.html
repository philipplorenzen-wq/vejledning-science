If you can imagine that your exam will go well, it's more likely to come true. That is why athletes, for instance, often visualise a good result before an important match or competition.


Visualising exams

Imagine the best possible exam – preferably in as much detail as possible, e.g.:

  • How you enter the room.
  • Where you sit.
  • How your body feels.
  • What the result will be.

Once you have created a mental picture of a really good exam, you should

  1. bring it to mind a few times a day while studying for the exam
  2. tell yourself that this is how your exam will be – this will improve the chance of you being right!


Demystify 'disaster'

If you spend a lot of time thinking about how disastrous your exam could be, you should allow yourself to imagine a bad exam. Once you know the worst-case scenario, it often seems less scary.


Pep talk on the day of the exam

Give yourself a pep talk just before the exam where you speak to yourself as you would speak to a friend who was about to take an exam.
Tell yourself that

  • it is going to go well
  • you can do it!
