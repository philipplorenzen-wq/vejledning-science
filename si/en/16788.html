Share your masters thesis with others

When you embark on the final and most important paper of your education, it may be a great help to read other theses to get a sense of level, topic and scope. However, it requires that people are willing to share their theses. 

What you will gain 

Your thesis will be part of the University's digital collection and others can search for it in the Royal library system (soeg.kb.dk).  You will be able to refer to your thesis in connection with job hunting or otherwise promote your work. Last but not least, you support an important agenda in society which is to make knowledge more open and accessible to everyone.

If your thesis contains sensitive personal data or is not open to the public, please do not upload it to the library systems.

Do not worry about uploading your thesis in Digital Exam. The system is able to handle confidentiality issues.

How to make your thesis public

When submitting your thesis in Digital Exam, you will be asked whether you want to make your thesis and appendixes open to public:

If you wish to do so, click on the button 'Go to project library'. Then, CURIS (Copenhagen University Research Information System) will open, and you will be asked to fill in a few key data, such as title and supervisor. When you have entered this information, you will receive a receipt by email, and that's it. 

The university library will automatically be notified when your thesis has passed, and your thesis will be made visible in the library system (soeg.kb.dk). The thesis will be available online, and everyone can access it without waiting time.

If you want to withdraw or have questions

If you want to withdraw your thesis from the library or you have questions, please contact 'Ask the Library', who will be able to help you.
