The following happens when your complaint has been submitted:

1
The complaint will be sent to the employee's head of department. 
2The head of department will look into the matter with HR, in accordance with UCPH's guidelines on the processing of written complaints about members of staff. 
3

The head of department may need to talk to you about what happened to be able to assess how the complaint should be handled.

You do not need to provide a detailed explanation. The important thing is that the head of department understands the gravity of the matter. We recommend that you bring a friend/family member/the student ambassador to the meeting.

4

After the head of department has received the complaint and talked to you (if relevant), the given employee is notified about the content of the complaint and who lodged it.

The employee is given an opportunity to comment – unless your complaint is deemed to be unfounded.

5
The head of department makes a decision in the case together with HR. If the nature of the incident is very serious, the employee you have made the complaint against may be warranted disciplinary sanctions, for example a warning or dismissal.  
6

When the case has been closed, the head of department will inform you that it has been dealt with. The information you receive about the case will vary depending on the specific case. 

Management handles cases in accordance with UCPH's guidelines for dealing with written complaints about employees and managers.
