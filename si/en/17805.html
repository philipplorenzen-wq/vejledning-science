There are plenty of career opportunities when graduating, and there is only little involuntary unemployment among our graduates. The network you develop, the courses and project topics you choose during your programme might affect your subsequent job opportunities. I recommend that you carefully consider your options and choices during your study as this may direct you towards an interesting job and career.

While studying, you might consider taking a student job, as this can also be a way to establish valuable network leading to a job once graduated. While I encourage you to take a student job, if you have the opportunity and you desire to do so, you should only take the job, if it is relevant for your studies, and if it does not prevent you from studying full time. A Project in Practice may serve some of the same functions without taking time from the studies. A thesis made in collaboration with an external partner/host (e.g. private company, NGO or public sector institution) may also lead to improved chances for a first job. Sometimes, a successful Project in Practice leads to a thesis project hosted by the same company/organization – and even a job in the end...

Always remember that some skills and competencies simply are in high demand on the job market – such as economics, digital skills and competencies in statistics and mathematical modelling – or simply mastering working with spreadsheets or GIS may turn out to be a good “selling point” while pursuing your dream job or most interesting career path… 

Go for it!
