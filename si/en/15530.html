Any delay caused by the fact that you postpone a course may have minor consequences. The more delayed you are in your studies, the greater the consequences may be.


Consequences of postponing courses

How postponing a course may affect your course of study:

  • You may have to wait a full year before you can take the course – depending on how often the course is offered.
  • Having postponed one course, you may find that you have to postpone other courses, as some courses need to be taken in a specific order. Ask the course coordinator or read about the formal requirements in your course description.
  • You will not be able to take the course with the class, or the year group, with which you started your study programme.


Long-term consequences of delays

Major delays may have serious consequences if

  • you will not be able to meet your study activity requirements before you risk being disenrolled from your study programme
    Read about your study activity requirements under Planning your studies.
  • you have used all your exam attempts for a course without passing it.
     Read about your three exam attempts


Exemptions possible

If you are in one of these situations due to exceptional circumstances in your life, you may apply for a dispensation.
See how and when to apply for a dispensation:

Delays may have consequences for your SU

If you do not follow the recommended course of study, you should check whether you meet the SU study activity requirements. Failing to do so may affect your right to receive SU.
