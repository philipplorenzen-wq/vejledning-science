To promote well-being among the general population, a group of researchers at UCPH have formulated an ABC for mental health.


3 areas you can prioritise in order to improve your mental health:

  • ACT: Be active 
    You can be physically, mentally, culturally or socially active. It could be anything from dancing or going for a walk in nature to reading a book or going fishing.
     
  • BELONG: Do something together
    You can develop a sense of community with others in many ways. Examples include joining a book club, going to a party, prioritising your study group or joining a football team.
  • COMMIT: Do something meaningful
    You can do something meaningful by involving yourself in your study environment, e.g. as a tutor, mentor/student teacher, a volunteer in the study café, or by being active in a political organisation. You can also find meaning by engaging academically on your courses etc.
Read more about the ABC for mental health

Applying the ‘ABC’ to your student life

How you can go about it in practice:
  1. Choose 3 specific things from the ABC that you will start to do (or continue to do) to improve your mental health. Choose the ways that make sense to you as a student. A single activity can cover multiple areas. For example, as a volunteer at a campus café, you get to spend time with others, while also doing something active and meaningful.
    Find inspiration under involvement and communities
  2. Include your 3 chosen fields of action in your weekly schedule when planning your study day. This is one way of ensuring you give priority to them.
