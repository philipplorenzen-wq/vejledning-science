When the places under UCPH's exchange agreements are allocated, importance is attached to the following:


Your compliance with the terms and conditions for the agreement:
You must meet the terms and conditions for the places for which you are applying.  These may be grade requirements, academic requirements, or requirements pertaining to your educational level. You can find the specific conditions in the information sheets available in the Mobility Online portal.

Full study load 
You must intend and be able to obtain a pre-approval for -and to complete - 30 ECTS per semester at the host university (see more under “How many ECTS do I need to take during my stay?").

Whether you have been on previous exchange stays
If you have already been on an exchange stay, you will be prioritised after the students who have not yet been on a stay abroad. However, this does not apply if you have been on a mandatory exchange stay.

Academic and disciplinary diversity in the agreements
To ensure that students across the university have access to the agreements they are qualified to apply for, all faculties will be considered when allocating spots under university-wide agreements.

Additionally, some agreements are established specifically to ensure mandatory exchange stays for certain programs. For these agreements, students with mandatory exchange requirements will be given priority. This will be stated in the  information sheet of the agreement.


Your grade-point average:
All places are allocated on the basis of the applicants' grade-point average. In cases where two applicants have the same GPA, the spot will be allocated based on ECTS seniority—i.e., the applicant with the highest number of passed ECTS credits in the program they are applying for will be prioritized. Bachelor's and Master's degrees are considered as one combined program.

If both GPA and ECTS seniority are equal, the spot will be allocated by lottery.
