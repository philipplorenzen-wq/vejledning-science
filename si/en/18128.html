As the organiser of a party for your fellow students at SCIENCE/UCPH, you can book rooms/areas at SCIENCE. You must always comply with the
Guidelines for parties in booked rooms at SCIENCE


There are lots of things to think about before, during and after the party. It is therefore a good idea to

  1. start planning early
  2. get a group together to share the planning and responsibilities

Before the party – what you need to do

Things you need to do when planning a party:

  • Book a venue at least 1½ months before the party For Christmas parties – at the earliest from 1 May of the same year. Book the room for the whole period in which you need to use it, incl. time for setting-up and tidying-up – at weekends max. until noon the next day. You will receive a reply within 7 days. The room is booked once you have filled in and received approval of the agreement form you receive from the Room Administration.

  • Find out how many you expect to attend the party and who If, in special cases, you want to invite guests who are neither students nor staff at UCPH, you must send a list of such external guests to the Room Administration no later than one week before the party. So do not start marketing the party broadly through, for example, public posts on Facebook, Instagram or the like. 
    Use this scheme to list invited guests who are not from SCIENCE
  • Designate party organisers who must be present during the party. If more than 50 people are expected to attend, you must designate at least 2 party organisers – and it is a good idea to have more persons share the responsibility at large parties. At least one of the party organisers/contacts must be at least 21 years old if you will be serving alcohol at the party.
  • Organise the required number of student guards as well as professional security guards in case of more than 300 participants. See how many guards are required for your party and how to book professional security guards inGuidelines for parties in booked rooms at SCIENCE
  • Apply for an occasional licence to serve alcohol/notify the police. Under the current rules (January 2022), you must apply for an occasional licence for parties with more than 150 participants. For less than 150 participants, you only need to notify the police. Check up on the rules and apply for an occasional licence or give a notification on the police's website
  • Prepare a space allocation plan (fire safety). For parties with more than 149 participants, and if you want to rearrange the furniture in the room, you need to prepare an alternative space allocation plan. The Room Administration will help you with this.
  • Book professional cleaning for after the party. For parties with more than 50 participants, you must book and pay for professional cleaning by the faculty's cleaning team. For parties with less than 50 participants, it is up to you whether you want to do the cleaning or pay someone else to do it.

Just before and during the party – what you need to do

Just before and on the day of the party, you must:

  • Ensure that all student guards are familiar with their task – Adapt and print out this checklist
  • Display your approved space allocation plan on top of the existing space allocation plans if you have changed the furniture arrangements and make sure the log is available (you will receive it from the Room Administration).
  • Display alarm instructions throughout the room so that the party participants can easily find them. You will receive the alarm instructions from the Room Administration (together with the space allocation plan).
  • Make sure the party does not spill out from the booked area to retain an overview and facilitate cleaning.
  • Keep noise levels down out of consideration for the neighbours – Both when guests arrive and leave, and during the party. Music may only be played indoors and with closed windows and doors.
  • Look after your guests and handle any emergencies or difficult situations during the party as described in Guidelines for parties in booked rooms at SCIENCE

After the party – what you need to do

After the party, you must:

  • Tidy up thoroughly – both indoors and outdoors. For example, remove all bottles, cups, forgotten items, cigarette butts, etc. Collect and place all waste in the right waste containers. The Room Administration will send you a map showing waste containers in the area. 
  • Rearrange tables and chairs in accordance with the original space allocation plan for the room. Remove your alternative space allocation plan (if you have changed the furniture arrangements).
  • Clean the premises (for parties with less than 50 participants where you elect to do the cleaning yourselves), so that you leave the room and the toilet facilities in the same condition as when you arrived.
  • Send a completed log (which you received when you booked the room) to the Room Administration no later than 5 weekdays after the party.

Contact the Room Administration if you have any questions

If in doubt about anything, you are welcome to contact the Room Administration at SCIENCE.
