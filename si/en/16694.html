Gælder for målgrupperne: Quantum Information Science - Kandidat

Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/Your-way-through-the-programme-SCI-QIS.aspx

The Master’s program in Quantum Information Science is very interdisciplinary covering subjects with focus in Computer Science, Mathematics, or Physics. Within these areas, you can choose to specialize in more theoretical or applied subjects. Since there are many options, we do not have specific study tracks.

Moreover, the program is taught at two universities: the University of Copenhagen and The Technical University of Denmark.
The first teaching Block at the University of Copenhagen is entirely mandatory and is a general introduction to the many aspects of the field. It consists of the two courses Introduction to Quantum Computing (IQC) and Introduction to Quantum Information Science (IQIS).
Already in the second teaching block, i.e., the second half of the first semester at the University of Copenhagen you can start making choices relevant for the direction you want to take. You must choose one of the two courses.• Quantum Information Theory (QIT)• Physical Implementation of Quantum Information Processing (PIQIP)
If you want to go in a more mathematical or computer science direction, i.e., what we could call the direction of quantum software, you should choose QIT. If you want a more physics direction or more on quantum hardware, you should choose PIQIP. It is completely fine and a very good choice to take both QIT and PIQIP. Alternatively, and if you have the right physics background, it would be natural to combine PIQIP with the restricted elective courses in physics, i.e., those with a course number starting with NFY. Likewise, if you have the right mathematics or computer science background, it would be natural to combine QIT with the restricted elective courses in Mathematics or Computer Science, i.e., those starting with NDA or NMA.
After the first semester at the University of Copenhagen, you will be taking courses at the Technical University of Denmark in the second semester. Courses here run for a whole semester, but there are also some summer courses. There is a mandatory course Quantum Information Technology that all students must take. At the same time, you must choose two of the three courses.• Experimental Techniques in Quantum Technology• Quantum Compilers• Quantum Algorithms and Machine Learning
If you want to go in an experimental direction, you should of course choose the first and one of the other two. If you want to focus on quantum software, you should choose the last two. Your remaining choices of restricted elective courses should naturally reflect the direction in which you want to go. If in doubt, you should seek advice from your mentor.
When it comes to choosing purely elective courses it is important to think about the master thesis project you want to work on whether it will be experimental or not.
