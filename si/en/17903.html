Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/Your-options-after-graduating-SCI-IFS.aspx

Graduates from the Integrated Food Studies seek employment throughout the food system. Some work in public administrations; some in businesses; some in NGOs; some in research and some start their own business.

Across all the opportunities, our evaluations show that the key qualifications they brought from their education were:

  • Interdisciplinary experience, the ability to put your knowledge into a wider (development) context.
  • Intercultural collaboration, because most companies need people who can perform in many cultural settings.
  • Project management and problem solving skills, through working in the field, both in groups and on your thesis.
  • Academic excellence, but getting good at whichever topic you choose – or becoming an expert generalist.

Remember that many get their first job in companies or institutions where they have had student jobs or have had collaborations during the Master's thesis or in a Project in Practice collaboration. It may therefore be a good idea to make use of all opportunities to engage with companies or institutions in the food sector during your studies.
