When you know at what times of day you are best able to concentrate, plan your various study activities and tasks in line with this. 


Consider, for example: 

  • when can you best concentrate? And when do you find it most difficult to concentrate? 
  • what types of tasks require a strong focus? And which ones can you handle without being fully focused? 

If you are unsure when you work best, you can monitor this over a few days. 

How to find your prime time: 

  1. In the table below, mark the times of day when you feel most concentrated over a period of five days.
  2. Identify what the trend is during the period. Is there a pattern?
  3. Consider whether it would be good to change some of your habits: Perhaps you end up working too late in the evening, which impacts your sleep?

  4. Start planning based on your pattern and change any bad habits.
