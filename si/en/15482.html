There are several ways to successfully become an ENRE graduate. First and foremost, it is important that you take responsibility for your own education. You will have to make lots of choices during the program, and you are the only one who knows what is right for you. If you are in doubt, you are always welcome to contact me or UCPH Study Guidance for advice and feedback.

Making it successful through the ENRE program requires independence, self-discipline, study skills and the ability to cooperate - and on top of that you need to study hard. In order to progress through the program without unnecessary stress or delays, my advice to you is that you:

  • Consider studying as work and spend the necessary amount of time on it. Studying full time means that you on average are expected to spend at least 45 hours per week during study active weeks.
  • Attend the lectures and the exercises provided in the courses you take and be active. This will improve the study and teaching environment and be good for your own learning.
  • Structure your time, both weekdays and weekends.
  • Attend the available SPOT ON events that can help you manage your time better and learn more efficient study techniques.
  • Engage in social activities, both with your fellow ENRE students as well as others.
  • Read your study messages and study events not to miss important messages and events.  
  • Engage in scientific activities, e.g. attend department seminars and similar scientific events.
  • Do not cheat in exams or plagiarize (knowing or unknowingly) and check how you can avoid doing so.
  • Use your influence to improve the program and your study environment, i.e. participate actively in course evaluations and annual study environment surveys.
  • Use your KUmail (notice, this is actually a requirement!).
