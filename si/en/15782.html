You already have a network. So start by creating an overview of the persons you already know. You can then work on expanding your network.

Create an overview of your existing network

Make a list of those you already know who can be included in your professional network, e.g.

  • fellow students of the same and previous year groups
  • lecturers and supervisors
  • colleagues from your student job, volunteer work, student associations etc.

They may also be persons with whom you do not share an area of academic expertise, but who work or study in a place you find interesting or who work on exciting assignments.   

How to expand your network

Throughout your studies and working life, you will naturally get to know more people and expand your network.
By taking an active approach you can also work strategically to expand your network with relevant people.

4 tips on how to expand your network

  • Participate in career fairs and events where you can meet different companies. Here you have a chance to ask about e.g. the possibilities for project collaboration in the company, types of jobs/ student jobs and career paths in the company. 
    Tips on how to find and participate in career fairs
  • Attend academic network groups and student associations at SCIENCE.
    Find student communities
  • Go to after-hours meetings or other professional networks in your trade union or unemployment fund, which typically have formal or informal networks on work-related themes, industries or the like. 
    Read about trade unions and unemployment funds.
  • Use LinkedIn to expand your network. Read more under 'How do I network via LinkedIn?'.
