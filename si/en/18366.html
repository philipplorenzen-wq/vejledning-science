What if I disagree with the rejection of my application for pre-approval?

If your application for pre-approval of a course is rejected, you may appeal the academic grounds for the decision to the credit appeals board.

The decision may be based on the academic grounds

  • that the academic level of the desired course is not high enough
  • that the course is not considered as academically relevant to the specific part of your study programme in which the course will be incorporated.


How to appeal the academic grounds for a decision

Send your appeal to klager-science@adm.ku.dk no later than 2 weeks after you have been notified of the Study Board's decision. UCPH can give special permission to process delayed appeals due to exceptional circumstances.

Your appeal must be made in writing and must contain a

  1. description of what you are complaining about
  2. reason why you think the academic grounds for the decision are wrong.


Processing of your appeal

Once your appeal has been received, the dean will appoint a board of appeals consisting of

  • a representative of the academic staff associated with your study programme or a related study programme at the university
  • and a representative of the academic staff associated with your study programme or a related study programme at another university.

Possible outcomes of your case

The board of appeals may

  • grant a pre-approval of more programme elements than were approved by the Study Board 
  • reject your appeal
  • in exceptional cases submit the appeal for renewed consideration by the Study Board.

Reply to your appeal

You will get a reply when the appeals board has submitted its decision to the university. The board must notify the university as soon as possible – and no more than 1 month after the dean's appointment of the board of appeals (July does not count due to the holidays). You will be notified of any delays.


Appeal against the decision of the credit appeals board

You cannot appeal against the academic grounds for the board's decision, but you can complain to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science if you don't think that your case has been processed according to the rules – i.e. complain about legal issues.

This may, for example, be the case if you think the board of appeals has

  • applied incorrect rules
  • has attached importance to factually incorrect information
  • has not considered important arguments in your application.

The Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science cannot take a position on the board of appeals' assessment of whether exceptional circumstances exist in your case. Nor does the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science normally assess whether the decision is generally reasonable or appropriate.


How to complain to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science

You complain by sending a written complaint addressed to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science no later than 2 weeks from the day you were notified of the decision of the appeals board.

Send your complaint to klager-science@adm.ku.dk, which will forward your complaint to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science with a statement.

You will be given the opportunity to comment on the statement of UCPH Study Guidance before it is forwarded to the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science.

Reply from the Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science and the next steps

You will receive a reply directly from Danish Agency for Higher Education and Science when your appeal has been processed.

You cannot appeal against the decision of the Danish Agency for Science and Higher Education.

Please note, all complaints options and specific deadlines will be specified in the emails you receive in your KUmail in connection with your case.
