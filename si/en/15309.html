How is ‘minority’ defined in the survey?

Placering: Your student life › Well-being in your study life › Inclusion survey

Sprog: English · Elementtype: Accordion (narrow format)

Gælder for målgrupperne: Københavns Universitet - Alle uddannelsesniveauer

Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/How-is-minority-defined-in-the-survey-KU.aspx

In the survey, it was possible to indicate whether you experience being part of one or more minority groups at UCPH – in other words, if you find that you are the only one or one among just a few with e.g. your nationality, ethnicity, age or family situation at your place of study or work at UCPH. Next, it was possible to indicate which minority group(s) on a list of 20 predefined groups you experience belonging to. The groups covered a broad range of possibilities, including being a minority based on one's ethnicity or sexuality, but also being a minority based on having younger children or one's language skills.
The broad approach was used to avoid limiting and assuming which minority groups exist at UCPH in this very first inclusion survey at the university. In a free-text field, respondents could add any minority group affiliations not listed, and several made use of this option. The responses to the inclusion survey show that there can be many different reasons why someone may experience being part of a minority at UCPH.
The two largest minority groups in the survey are individuals with a different nationality than the majority and individuals who are the first generation in their family to attend university. The two smallest minority groups are individuals with care-requiring relatives and individuals with a different political belief than the majority.
