There are many ways to study. The most important thing is that you take responsibility for your own effort and the choices you will take during your Master's programme. You should select the courses you find most interesting, but if you are in doubt, you can contact me or UCPH Study Guidance or talk to fellow students.

Here follows some information and advice that can come in handy:

  • A Master's programme in Geography and Geoinformatics is equivalent to a full-time job. Expect to use 35-40 hours each week on classes, preparation/homework, and group work.
  • Use your fellow students for discussing the subjects you have chosen and to prepare for classes. It may be a good idea to set up study groups for the various courses you are following.
  • Try to structure your time. Study when you study and be off when you are off. Some students benefit a lot from making detailed plans for their time for the entire block. Read about study techniques. 
  • Make yourself acquainted with the programme structure and the rules and exemptions that apply to the Master's programme.
  • Keep yourself updated on information related to the study programme by checking study messages and your online year group room in Absalon . The students manage an electronic board on Facebook – ‘Geotavlen’ – which is also good to check.
  • Faculty of Science organises a number of Study events every block. They focus, among other things, on study techniques, exchange stays at universities elsewhere, and career opportunities.
  • Read your study messages and study events not to miss important messages and events.  
  • Avoid plagiarism and cheating. It is illegal to perform plagiarism from others or your own work. As a consequence, you can get expelled from the university. Also, familiarize yourself with the rules regarding use of generative artificial intelligence.
  • Remember to evaluate the courses you are following. This is a good opportunity to get influence on how individual courses are structured. Course evaluations are actively used by the teachers, who are requested to respond to all evaluations when designing next year’s course.
  • Take part in social activities at the department. At Geography and Geoinformatics we have a student association who arrange professional and social activities and can be linked to through social media (mainly Instagram and Facebook). You can also attend students' Friday bar and parties, and get engaged in our choir or football team.
  • I also recommend you to be engaged in Geofagrådet, which is a formal student association that engages in student politics. The association also arranges other events, e.g., meetings with representatives of firms who have geographers employed, talks about research projects by staff at the department.
