Graduates from the Global Environment and Development program will seek employment in a wide range of businesses. An increasing number of private companies employ graduates with sourcing, certification, or product development expertise. Traditionally, many are employed in environment, nature, and agricultural management, either in public offices, ministries, or private consultancy companies. Many continue with research in a PhD and eventually end up in a research organisation, university, or the applied end of implementing research via extension and consultancies. Others go to development organisations and NGOs as technical experts or managers.

Across all the opportunities, the key qualifications you will bring from this education are:

  • Interdisciplinary experience: the ability to put your knowledge into a wider (development) context.
  • Intercultural collaboration: most companies need people who can perform in many cultural settings.
  • The international fieldwork experience you just cannot get sitting in an auditorium.
  • Project management and problem-solving skills, through working in the field, both in groups and on your thesis.
  • Academic excellence, by getting good at whichever topic you choose – or becoming an expert generalist.
