It is important that you take out insurance covering your whole stay abroad – regardless of whether you will be studying or doing an academic internship.
In most cases, an ordinary travel insurance policy does not cover study stays abroad.

If you travel without insurance, you risk facing huge expenses in case of damage or injury.
The insurance should, as a minimum, cover the following:

  • illness and repatriation
  • travel
  • accidents
  • theft
  • lost or damaged luggage
  • liability if you accidentally cause injury to other persons or damage to their property
  • any supplementary insurance in connection with laboratory experiments, clinical training or the like.

Take out health insurance for a stay outside Europe
You should take out health insurance in Denmark for the vast majority of destinations outside Europe because you cannot be included in the host country's health insurance scheme.
Many universities in, for example, the United States require you to buy the university's health insurance. Check whether it also covers accidents and liability – it typically does not.
Remember your blue health insurance card for a stay in Europe
If you are going on a study stay in Europe, it is important that you remember your blue health insurance card.
