Your project contract is usually enough, as it covers the formal framework for the project cooperation – also in relation to your host company. 

If the company asks for a confidentiality agreement

Be careful when signing a confidentiality agreement from the company. It may curb your right to write and talk about your findings at the exam and afterwards.


Four good tips about confidentiality agreements:

  1. Always discuss the agreement with your supervisor before signing anything. 
  2. Perhaps suggest to the company that instead you draw up an agreement together.
    Use the UCPH template for agreements for external cooperation.
  3. Only sign a confidentiality agreement from the company if it is absolutely necessary.
  4. If the company insists that you sign a cooperation agreement, try to keep the period in which you are required to maintain confidentiality to a minimum.


If you want to protect your rights

Use the UCPH template for external cooperation if you have a special need to protect your rights in relation to, for example

  1. ideas
  2. articles
  3. specific products.
