During your Master's programme in Geography and Geoinformatics, there are two types of projects you can choose, which represent good opportunities for you to focus on specific themes of interest before starting your thesis:

  • Project in Practice 
  • Project outside the course scope 

The programme finishes with a Master's thesis of 45 or 60 ECTS credits. 

Thesis planning and contact with supervisor

To start the process of getting a thesis planned, I suggest you approach a potential supervisor as soon as possible to discuss your thesis idea. If you can’t find a supervisor, contact me, and I will help you to get in contact with a relevant supervisor. 
I suggest that you write half a page of structured thoughts summarizing the direction of what you would like to work on in your thesis. The project must have a distinct geography and/or geoinformatics focus.
If you plan a project that involves travel, sampling, analyses etc., you should count those costs into your project.
Listen to the advice that your supervisor gives – they have valuable experience in supervising theses. 

Find a topic 

If you do not have an idea for a thesis project, consider what has triggered your interest in classes during the last year. Use this as a starting point and think of an interesting case to look into based on this. Often your supervisor can help to elaborate an idea. 

Collect data and write your thesis 

Once you have made the thesis contract and started the thesis process, it is important that you make a detailed time plan for the thesis work with your supervisor. 
Note that a 45 ECTS thesis is scheduled for 6.5 months and 60 ECTS thesis for 9 months. Make sure that the plan is realistic and that you scale your ambitions to the time available. Your thesis will have a submission deadline and an examination date, and it is important that you submit on time – just like when you complete a course with an exam. 
If you get sick or there are other circumstances of force majeure that delay your thesis work, then discuss with your supervisor and/or me about applying for extension of your thesis time. You can get guidance on the application at UCPH Study Guidance.
