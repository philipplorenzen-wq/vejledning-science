Your MSc-programme in Biotechnology is composed of compulsory, elective and restricted elective courses as well as your Master's thesis project. Consider your study as full-time work and use the time it requires.

The programme starts in block 1 with a 7.5 ECTS compulsory course Big Data in Biotechnology. This course will give insight in handling and analysing large data sets, which is important in biotechnology.

In addition to this, you have to choose 37.5 ECTS courses from a list of restricted elective courses. These are courses, that I generally recommend, and these courses have been pre-approved for you to take. 

When you read about the courses at the course description page, it is important to take notice of the Recommended Academic Qualifications, so you don't choose a course that is too difficult to follow and pass.

In general, I advise you to choose courses that fit your interests and are relevant for complementing your skills. You may choose to specialize in a certain area such as “microbial biotechnology", “plant biotechnology" or “animal biotechnology". Remember to check that the courses you choose are not placed in the same schedule group.

In block 4, I can recommend you take the 15 ECTS restrictive elective course Advanced Biotechnology and Intellectual Property Rights. This course will give insight in innovation, entrepreneurship, and patenting.

For students admitted in 2021 or earlier

The first year is completed with the 15 ECTS compulsory course Advanced Biotechnology and Intellectual Property Rights. This course will give insight in innovation, entrepreneurship and patenting.

MSc thesis project

During the second year, you do a 60 ECTS experimental Master's thesis project.
