What is the function of a CV?

The CVs are typically the first thing a hiring committee/employer skims through when deciding which candidates, they want to read applications from and possibly invite for an interview.

Some companies only request a CV – with just a few lines added about why you are applying for the job.


A quick overview of your relevant experience

The CV should provide a quick overview of

  • your experience in terms of previous jobs or education
  • what you can do that is relevant to the job you are seeking.

Your task is to make the CV point forward towards the role and tasks you can fulfil for the employer, so it becomes more than just a list of past experiences.


You must tailor the CV to the specific position

Always ensure to tailor your CV so the employer can see how your profile matches the specific position.

This applies to

  • which tasks or courses you highlight – or downplay
  • which skills you emphasize under different jobs or education/courses
  • how you formulate, for example, the profile text and heading.
