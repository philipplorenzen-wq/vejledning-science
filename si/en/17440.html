I warmly recommend you to do a study abroad, and this is possible in blocks 1 and 2 in the second year. It is important to start planning this as early as possible, at least half a year in advance, and even earlier if you want to apply for specific exchange programmes.

Another reason to start planning this early is also that you need pre-approval for the courses you take during your study abroad. Especially for the restricted electives, you need to find courses that can be approved as restricted electives. The elective subjects could be any course, but I recommend that you choose courses that are relevant for your profile as a manager of forest and nature. Hence, it is strongly recommended to visit a foreign university that has plenty of relevant courses on offer.

To learn more about your possibilities for studying abroad, contact UCPH Study Guidance. I would also recommend that you get in contact with older students who have been abroad earlier, to learn from their experiences.
