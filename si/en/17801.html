Your future career starts when you start the Biochemistry programme. I highly recommend that you visit the Study Start module "How to explore your career possibilities" in Absalon, which can help you think about your future career.

Completing the research-based thesis project often helps you to decide the direction of your future career. Many candidates want to continue doing basic research and look for PhD programmes while others look for other kinds of jobs. The number of PhD grants is limited and generally requires high grades, a successful thesis project and that you finish your MSc without delay. You also have the possibility to follow a PhD programme abroad.

Approximately 50 % of candidates continue in a PhD programme. Many get jobs in R&D or production in the biotech/biomedical industry while some become product specialists in lab equipment. Some even start their own biotech company.

Even though you may think that your thesis has a very narrow focus, you obtain many competences like identifying, analyzing and solving problems which are relevant for a large number of different jobs you probably never thought could be relevant for you. So, don't hesitate to apply for jobs that are not in the field of your thesis.

To create a professional network, I recommend that you join: 

  •  UCPH Alumni, 
  • BIO Alumni
  • Biochemistry LinkedIn Group 

Expand and use your network e.g. Synapse-Life Science Connect which was established by former Biochemistry and Molecular Biomedicine students and arranges different networking events.

You can participate in SPOT events on career.

If you are an international student, you have of course all the same options. And remember that English is the preferred language in biochemistry no matter where you work, and job opportunities are good in Denmark
