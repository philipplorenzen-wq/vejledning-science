If you receive special educational support (SPS), you can use your support options and aids at your host university. The rules and regulations for using aids in classes and for exams may differ from university to university. This means that you must contact your host university to ask about the relevant rules and regulations and what dispensations you should apply for. 

Here are a few examples of support and aids that other students have been allowed to use during their stay abroad:

  • Students with dyslexia have used their digital aids (program package, Nota) and received online support hours with KVUC.
  • Students suffering from mental or neurological conditions have had online meetings with their study guidance counsellor from the UCPH Study Guidance, student mentor or academic support teacher.
