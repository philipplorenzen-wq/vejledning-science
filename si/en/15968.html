Quite simply – and importantly – communities centred around your studies are good sources of: 

  • joy and energy in your daily routine 
  • support during difficult periods along the way. 


Research also indicates that it is crucial for your motivation and your ability to learn new things that you: 

  • find meaning in your student life 
  • feel that you make a difference and matter to your fellow students. 
  • feel that you belong to a social or academic community. 

These are all things you can find by seeking out communities and getting involved in your study environment. 

The types of communities you will have the time and inclination to participate in will vary from person to person. But no matter how you involve yourself, it will make a positive difference in your student life.
