If you are not sure whether you will be ready for your exam at the end of the course due to illness – without being formally prevented from taking the exam, you should first and foremost discuss your situation with your course coordinator.

Your course coordinator can help you assess whether there is anything you can do to make up for what you have missed on the course so that you can still manage to pass the course this time around.

Find the course coordinator's contact details and information about the exam in the course description

Questions that can help you assess your situation

  • What will it be like to take the exam?
  • Is there a risk that I will not pass because I am not feeling well enough or am not sufficiently well prepared due to my illness?
  • How will it affect my study plan if I do not take the exam now?
  • Can I still meet my study activity requirements?
    Read more about your study activity requirements under Planning your studies

Feel free to discuss your thoughts with a study and career guidance counsellor from UCPH Study Guidance


If you cannot take the exam

You must apply for a dispensation to withdraw from the exam due to your illness in the following two situations:

  • The course is compulsory so that you must pass it during your study programme.
  • The course is an elective or a restricted elective course, and you want to take it again later on.

When applying for a dispensation, you must be able to document with a medical certificate how your illness during the course prevents you from taking the exam.
Read more on this page under 'What are the requirements for documentation from my doctor?'

How to apply for a dispensation to withdraw from an exam


If you don't want to take an elective/restricted elective course again

If you do not want to take the course again and it is either an elective or a restricted elective course, you do not have to apply for withdrawal from the exam. Instead, you must apply for a dispensation to withdraw from the course after the deadline.

If you use or have used an exam attempt for the course, it is very difficult to withdraw.
