How do I document illness or death in the family?

Exactly which documentation you need to enclose when applying for an dispensation always depends on your specific situation. There is therefore no universal answer to this question.
In general

  • you must be able to document that 'exceptional circumstances' are affecting your studies
  • what is considered 'exceptional circumstances' depends on whether it is affecting your immediate family or not.


Your immediate family (in the legal sense) is your:

  • father and mother
  • siblings
  • spouse/partner 
  • children


Documenting illness or death in 'the immediate family'

Generally speaking, a serious illness or death in the immediate family is considered an exceptional circumstance in itself. Therefore, it is often enough to document the serious illness or death in your family.
Documents which can serve as documentation:

  • Hospital admission papers
  • Medical certificate (for your relative)
  • Death certificate

Documentation from your own doctor or psychologist may be relevant if you end up being absent for a longer period of time. The Study Board will contact you if it needs any further documentation to process your application.


Dispensations – special circumstances and relationships

There may be dispensations where a death or serious illness outside the immediate family will still be regarded as an exceptional circumstance. If a grandparent dies in dramatic circumstances, or if you have lived with your grandparents as a child, the death of a grandparent may well be considered an 'exceptional circumstance'.


Documentation of illness or death outside 'the immediate family'

Serious illness or death outside your immediate family is (legally) not an exceptional circumstance in itself.
If you are so badly affected that you need an dispensation or to take leave of absence, you need to document how the situation is affecting you and your studies instead.
Documents which can serve as documentation:

  • medical certificate from your own doctor
  • a statement from your psychologist or other authorised therapist

If the Study Board needs further documentation to be able to process your application, you will be contacted via your KUmail so that you can send it.
