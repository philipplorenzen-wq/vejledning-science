How do I decide on a topic?

You must first and foremost choose a topic that interests you and which you will want to work with for an extended period of time. However, the project must satisfy certain requirements.

Your topic must

  • fall within your field of study so that your head of studies can approve it as part of your study programme
  • live up to the description of the learning outcome for Master's theses on your study programme.
    Consult the programme-specific section of your curriculum

Two important questions to consider when choosing your topic

1) What do you want out of your Master's thesis?
  • Are you looking for a specific academic outcome? For example, certain methods that you want to learn, a particular subject area you want to immerse yourself in, or a particular academic profile you want to strengthen.
  • Are you keen to develop certain general competencies? For example, your communication skills, your ability to work with other people in a group project.
    Read about the possibility of doing a group project 


2) Do you have a specific idea, or are you open to input?

  • Is there a specific idea, or a specific field of interest that you are passionate about?
  • Are you open to letting your topic be shaped by the supervisor, research unit or company you would like to work with?
