If you experience mild signs of stress up to an exam or during a busy period, you can use some simple techniques to calm yourself and reduce your stress symptoms.

Try one of the 4 exercises here:

#1 Combat racing thoughts by focusing on something else

Is your head full of thoughts about everything you have to do, or all the things you are not managing to do? This distraction exercise can help you relax. 

What to do: 

  1. Look around you. 
  2. Scan your surroundings looking for round shapes (for example). 
  3. Let your eyes rest on each round shape. 
  4. Name the shape in your head. 

Try other shapes, colours, sounds etc.

#2 Breathe to calm your body and mind

Your breathing is central to your well-being. By practising to breathe calmly, you can feel more comfortable and prevent and reduce stress. 

What to do: 

  1. Breathe in right down to your stomach. Place your hand on your stomach to feel your breathing. 
  2. Inhale and hold your breath as you count to 10. 
  3. When you reach 10, breathe out and say the word ‘calm’ in your head. 
  4. Do this for a few minutes.

#3 Move around to regain your focus

When you have been sitting still focusing on your computer or book for a long time, you will reach a point where you cannot concentrate any longer. 

If you are feeling slightly under pressure, this is typically where you push yourself until you eventually cannot think clearly and start to feel anxious. Here negative thoughts may start to take over. 

This is where you need to practise taking a break to find new energy and regain your focus. The best way to do this is to close your book or computer and move around a bit. 

For example: 

  1. go for a walk around the block – or find a green area on campus. 
  2. move your legs and arms – to get your blood circulation going.

#4 Take good breaks

Taking good breaks is also a simple way to get some relief – where you focus on calming down, for example using your breathing.
