Your Master’s thesis must be anchored at SCIENCE or GLOBE Institute. This means that you must   
  • have a supervisor at SCIENCE or GLOBE Institute
  • hand in the project and have it assessed at SCIENCE or GLOBE Institute.

You are welcome to collect empirical data outside SCIENCE and GLOBE Institute, for example at a laboratory at another educational institution or at a company. Discuss with your supervisor whether it can be used for your project.

You need a pre-approval to do a Master’s thesis outside SCIENCE and GLOBE Institute 

It requires a pre-approval, if you want to do your Master’s thesis at another faculty or university – in Denmark or abroad. To get a pre-approval you must document that your thesis complies with certain requirements.


Requirements for your thesis at another faculty or university:

Your thesis must 

  • be done at a faculty/university that offers a programme that corresponds to your programme at SCIENCE
  • fully meet the specific objectives for Master's thesis stated in the programme-specific section of your curriculum
    Find the programme specific section of your curriculum
  • be of the same size/ECTS credits as the thesis at your programme
  • be defended orally
  • assessed with a grade (if you are doing a thesis abroad, your grade will be transferred as pass/fail when you apply for credit transfer)
  • assessed individually – also if you do your thesis in a group.

Talk to your Head of Studies early – and before applying for pre-approval
Discuss your thesis idea with your Head of Studies. Your Head of Studies can help you assess whether your project meets the required academic standards and aligns with the specific objectives of your programme.
