Your study programme has been designed to ensure the greatest possible academic progress, but it is possible to change the order of some courses.

You may change the order of elective and restricted elective courses as you like.

You can take compulsory courses in a different order than the recommended course of study – if the courses allow it.


What to check before you change the order of courses

4 things you check when you consider changing the order of your courses:

  1. Must the compulsory courses be taken in a certain order? Check any 'formal requirements' in the course descriptions for your courses.
  2. Will you need to read up on something to keep up? For example, if you are missing a basic course in your study programme, the course coordinator may expect you to have background knowledge from that course even though this is not stated in the course description.
  3. When will the courses you want to take be offered? Most courses are only offered once a year; some are offered only every other year.
  4. Can you be sure that the schedule groups for your courses still don't clash in the various blocks?

Find all relevant information in the course description 

You can find all the necessary information either directly in the course description or by consulting the course coordinator – whose contact details are also stated in the course description.
