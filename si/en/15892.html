How do I get ready to answer a job or project advertisement?

There are 3 steps in the research that you can, to your advantage, do before you start writing your application.
1) Read the job advertisement carefullyStart by reading the job advertisement carefully and see whether you can find answers to the following:
  • Which tasks are you required to perform?
  • Which competences are the employer looking for?
  • How are the tasks to be performed – alone or in a team? 
  • Does the employer specify any requirements for your education, competences, practical skills, previous experience, IT, language proficiency or other?

If there are questions that you think it is difficult to find answers to, you may call the contact person stated in the job advertisement.
2) Find out more details about the workplace

Writing a targeted application requires knowledge. Therefore, research the company a bit so that you know, for example:

  • its overall values and goals
  • if there are current challenges or specific conditions you should be aware of
  • what kind of position and workplace you are applying to.


Here you can find information:

  • On the company's website
  • Through a Google search
  • On the company's LinkedIn profile or other social media
  • From employees – for example, people with a background like yours, a type of position you are interested in, or someone in your network who happens to work there
  • From the contact person in the job posting, who can provide more specific information about the job and the workplace.

Questions you can ask the contact person when you call about a job

  • Of all the specified tasks, which will I be spending the most time on? 
  • What are the greatest challenges which your new employee will participate in meeting?  (remember to do your preliminary research, so that are prepared for the answer)
  • Who would I be working with and how? 
  • Other specific questions, that the job ad did not answer.

Calling about a job is a completely normal practice which demonstrates both that you are interested in the company and gives you valuable knowledge for your application.

3) Consider your new knowledge about the job in relation to your wishes and competencesBased on your research, you can now consider how the job fits your experience, competences and wishes.
You can do so by answering the following:
  • What are your professional interests and what motivates you in relation to the workplace and the job in question?
  • Which competences do you have that the employer is looking for?
  • What experiences do you have that the employer can benefit from?
  • How will you perform the tasks?
  • Do you have any competences or experiences that you think you can use in the job, but that are not mentioned in the job advertisement?

You don't have to meet all the employer's requirements. A job advertisement is typically an expression of the company's wishes for the ideal candidate. However, that does not mean that such a candidate exists – or that such a candidate will apply for the position.
