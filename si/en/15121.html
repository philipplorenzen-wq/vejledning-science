Under certain conditions, you may work with data which you receive from a researcher for use for your master's thesis or other assignments. However, it is only lawful to grant you access to data from a research project if:

  • the data in question are completely anonymous. This means that the researcher cannot recreate the correlation between data and data subjects because the researcher has deleted the so-called key linking the data subject to the data. This will often only take place five years after the research project has been completed,
  • the researcher has obtained consent from the project participants to use the data for teaching purposes. You must enter into an agreement on disclosure of personal data with the project coordinator, see below.
  • if your supervisor confirms that you handle personal data for scientific research purposes. You shall ask your supervisor to sign the form confirming that your assignment, project or master's thesis is scientific research.

When your supervisor has confirmed that you undertake scientific work and you get access to research data, you must confirm that you comply with the conditions for receiving research data for your own research activity. Please find the link to the form below.

You keep the form along with your other project documents and you provide a copy of the form to the researcher who has disclosed the research data to you.
