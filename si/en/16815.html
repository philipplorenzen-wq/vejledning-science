The programme offers several different kinds of informal study tracks. 

MATH offers several courses in finance and operations research. Many additional courses are also offered at the department of economics at KU, where you can register for courses through the Self service. 

You may also apply to follow courses at CBS as a credit student.

Find more information on the courses offered at CBS.

Read more about taking courses outside SCIENCE.

The following are merely suggestions. The placement, schedule (skemagruppe) and set of electives are only fixed for the study year 2025-26, so these study tracks are only meant to inspire one year ahead. 

Informal Study Track in Mathematical Finance

If you are interested in finance you should not miss or postpone the course Mathematical Finance that runs in block 1 and 2. This gives the general theory you need to follow, Finkont2 and Term Structure Models, but also many of the courses at the economics department. While the first year is strongly recommended the second year offers many different paths according to your interest, and the track below is merely a suggestion for an interesting path. See the general restricted elective course list for other options.

Informal Study Track in Econometrics/Statistics/Machine Learning

If this your interest you might have already taken Machine Learning A in your bachelor and this leaves open many different paths in your MSc. This means you can take the full package of mandatory statistics courses, Regression, Statistics A and Statistics B and Project in Statistics. Note Machine Learning A and FinMetrics are  both in schedule group (skemagruppe) B so they cannot be taken concurrently.
