You may register for an exam if

  • there are no particular examination prerequisites for your course
  • you have previously fulfilled the examination prerequisites for the course, which are still valid
  • the course does not have ongoing tests.


If your course has ongoing tests

If you need to pass a course with ongoing tests, you can only register for the exam if re-exam is offered in a different exam form.

Check in your course description if another exam form exists as re-exam

There are 2 ways to pass a course with ongoing tests, if no alternative form of re-exam is offered:

  • Ask the course coordinator whether it's possible for you to take an ordinary exam in a course with ongoing tests, without being registered for the actual course or the re-exam.
  • Register for and follow the course again.

 
In case of examination prerequisites


If you meet the examination prerequisites, you can register for the exam during the ordinary exam period or during the re-exam period.


If you don't meet the examination prerequisites, check the course description to see whether it's possible to meet the examination prerequisites without taking the course again. If possible, you should agree with the course coordinator how you can meet the prerequisites before the exam.

Read your course description

If it isn't possible for you to meet the prerequisites before the re-exam, you have to re-register for the course.
