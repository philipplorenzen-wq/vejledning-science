If your appeal is upheld, it can give you – and in some cases also other students concerned – the opportunity to:

  • retake the exam
  • have the exam re-assessed (only for written exams)

You must accept an offer of a retake or re-assessment within two weeks of being notified of the decision.


If you accept the offer of a retake or re-assessment

If you accept the offer of an exam retake or re-assessment, the University will assign new examiners.


The new grade is final – even if it is lower

It is always the new grade from a retake or re-assessment that will be shown on your diploma.
There is no guarantee that you will improve your grade through a retake or re-assessment. You may receive a lower grade.


Extraordinary exam retakes – for all students concerned

In very rare cases, an exam appeal can lead to the offer of an extraordinary exam retake for all the students concerned. The University will only offer this option if significant errors and omissions are discovered in a completed exam. 


You decide which grade applies

If you participate in an extraordinary retake of an exam, you can choose to keep your original grade for the first exam.
