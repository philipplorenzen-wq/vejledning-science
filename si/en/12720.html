Can I apply for dispensation from the SU rules?

Original side på KUnet: https://kunet.ku.dk/study/study_content/Pages/Can-I-apply-for-dispensation-from-the-SU-rules-SU-KU.aspx

It is not possible to apply for dispensation from the SU rules, but you can apply for what is known as additional grant portions (tillæg af klip). You can apply for additional grant portions as soon as the semester in which you have been delayed is completed.
It is important that you apply for additional grant portions for two reasons:

  1. It can help you to have sufficient grant portions for your education.
  2. It can help you to ensure that you meet the SU study activity requirement.

When deciding whether you are active in relation to  the SU rules, the following will be taken into consideration

  • For how many months you have received SU (you use one portion per month) and
  • How many ECTS credits you have passed 

Please note: You must not be more than 6 months (corresponding to 30 ECTS) delayed. For example, if you have received SU for 24 months (2 years), you must have passed a minimum of 90 ECTS (corresponding to 1.5 years of study).
When calculating how many grant portions you have used, the additional grant portions you have received are deducted.
Example: You have received 24 grant portions for your programme. Therefore, you must have completed 90 ECTS in order to live up to the SU activity requirement. However, you were ill for one semester (6 months) and did not pass any ECTS in that period. Therefore, you are applying for 6 additional grant portions to replace the SU from the semester where you were ill.
Your application for 6 additional grant portions will be approved, as it is considered to fall within the SU rules. When SU at the UCPH calculates your use of SU, it is 24 minus 6 = 18. The study activity requirement for 18 grant portions (1.5 years) is 60 ECTS (one year).
With additional grant portions you are able to reduce your total use of SU so that you do not have to meet the same requirements for the number of passed ECTS. In this way, there is a balance between your use of SU grant portions and the number of passed ECTS points.
