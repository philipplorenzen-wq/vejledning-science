How do I contact a potential supervisor?

You are always very welcome to contact a potential supervisor to find out whether he or she

  • is right for you
  • has the right academic competences to supervise you
  • has time to act as your supervisor.

It is a good idea to contact potential supervisors 3-4 months before the start of the Master's thesis so that the person in question is able to include your thesis in his/her planning.


When contacting a potential supervisor

Before contacting a potential supervisor, it is a good idea to consider the following:

  • When would you like to write your Master's thesis?
  • What do you want to achieve with your thesis? (Any specific ideas on the drawing board.)
  • Why could this particular person be the right supervisor for your thesis?
  • What particularly do you want out of the supervision?
  • Is there anything you want to know about the person in question?
    For example, has the person in question supervised this type of thesis before, how busy is he or she in the period, etc.?

If the person in question is unable to supervise you, you can ask if they can recommend an alternative supervisor.
