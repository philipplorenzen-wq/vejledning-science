The only thing you – formally – have to do to resume your studies is to register for the courses and exams that you want to take and to do so in the registration periods.

3 tips for getting back on track after a period of illness

  1. Find out how much you can study. It is important that you resume your studies at a pace that suits you. You may like to consult your doctor, psychologist, etc., about how active you can be on your study programme. If you feel that you need to take things a bit more slowly, you can apply for a dispensation.
    How to prepare a study plan tailored to your situation
  2. Update your study plan. This can help you get an overview of any courses you still need to take, when to take them, and any changes to your study programme.
    You can check your study programme under Planning your studies and your year of admission.
  3. Make appointments for the first days you are back. It may be good to have a lifeline or two when resuming your studies. Lifelines may be fellow students, a study and career guidance counsellor or perhaps a lecturer.
