Regardless of your destination, UCPH recommends that you protect your information by securing your computer and other electronic devices against hacking and eavesdropping.

Electronic equipment includes, for example, USB drives, hard drives, tablets, smartphones, cameras, audio recorders, and computers.

Checklist:

  • Keep an eye on your belongings.
  • Make sure to secure all your digital devices, e.g. enable two-factor authentication.
  • Always back up your data before departure.
  • Travel only with the data you truly need.
  • Update programs and apps regularly.
  • Be cautious when using public or shared computers, USB drives, and chargers.
  • Use a virtual private network (VPN) whenever possible.
  • Set up your phone so you can track it if it is stolen.

Strong passwords

Use long, unique passwords for all your accounts. Read UCPH's guide to secure passwords. 

Protect your digital devices

  • Use strong passwords.
  • Update your smartphone, tablet, and computer with the latest software and app updates.
  • Install antivirus software on all devices.
  • Never open attachments from unknown senders.
  • Do not download pirated software.
  • Be aware of fake apps – ask locals for safe alternatives.
  • Disable automatic Wi-Fi connections in your device settings.
  • Turn off internet sharing and be cautious with Bluetooth connections.
  • Avoid unsecured Wi-Fi networks – only use networks that require a password.
  • Only access online banking or sensitive information through a trusted network.
  • Plan regular backups of your files.
  • Keep an eye on your equipment and turn it off when not in use.

Use of VPN

UCPH recommends that you use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) to ensure encrypted and secure communication. UPCH does not provide a free VPN, so you must find your own solution.

Secure email

Use KUmail for communication with UCPH and fellow students, as emails between KUmail addresses are secure.

Webmail via webfile.ku.dk is not a secure connection for external emails.

If you need to send files to external recipients, encryption solutions such as Microsoft 365 Message Encryption or Filesender are recommended.

Encryption of sensitive data

If you work with sensitive or confidential data, you should encrypt your files so they remain protected even if your device is compromised.

Travel to high-risk areas

When traveling to countries with high IT security risks (e.g. Russia, DPRK, Iran, and China), you should take extra precautions:

  • Prepare a digital security plan.
  • Consider a separate IT travel kit.
  • Enable full disk encryption on your computer and phone.
