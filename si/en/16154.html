If you still have time before the planned start date

As long as you submit your contract before your planned start date, you will still be registered for the Master's thesis. However, you risk not having an approved contract ready for your start date. The closer you get to the start date of your Master's thesis, the larger the risk of your contract not being approved in time.

If your contract is not approved by the start date you will still formally have begun your Master's thesis period, but you will not have an approved thesis subject to work with.

If you have also passed the start date 

If you have exceeded the start date of the Master's thesis, you cannot be registered for the Master's thesis before the coming block unless you apply for and get a dispensation.

You can apply for a dispensation in exceptional circumstances

You can apply for a dispensation if exceptional circumstances prevented you from registering for the Master's thesis within the deadline. If you apply for a dispensation, you must document the circumstances preventing you from registering.

You can apply for a dispensation on the grounds of e.g.

  • illness (you or your immediate family) 
  • a death in your immediate family
  • other exceptional circumstances beyond your control.

Circumstances to do with your student job, housing situation or finances are not regarded as exceptional circumstances, but may be worth also mentioning in your application.

3 steps when you apply for a dispensation:

  1. Get documentation of your situation, e.g. maternity records/birth certificate/adoption documents, a medical certificate from your doctor or an opinion from a psychologist. In some cases, documentation can also be statements from your lecturer, supervisor, head of studies, etc. 
  2. Write a statement, which contains a description of the exceptional circumstances due to which you need a dispensation.
  3. Send an email and attach documentation and your written statement to studyguidance-science@adm.ku.dk.
