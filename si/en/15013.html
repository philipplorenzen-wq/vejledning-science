Is my teacher allowed to record or stream online teaching?

Your teacher is allowed to record or stream their own teaching without consent, if it's only the teacher that appears in the recording/streaming.

Your teacher may also upload the recording/streaming to the course room in Absalon, where only the students and the teacher can access it. The material must not be made available to anyone other than the participants in the course room on Absalon.

Your teacher must always notify you if the teaching is being recorded/streamed. It must be stated in the Absalon course room, so that all students can see it. It must also be stated that the content remains in Absalon and is not shared with anyone other than the students enrolled in the course.

If your teacher records/ in-person teaching, students must be notified. Your teacher must inform you where you and your fellow students can sit to avoid being included in the recording.

Can the teacher record or stream class if active participation (e.g. presentations) is required?

If your class includes mandatory active participation, such as presentations, you are still required to give your presentation even if the instructor is recording or streaming the session.

What if the teacher wants to share recorded/streamed online teaching outside the Absalon course room?

If your instructor wants to share recorded or streamed teaching outside the Absalon course room, where students can be identified, this requires the students’ explicit consent. This is part of UCPH's privacy policy, which also applies in the context of teaching.
