What happens if I’m suspected of cheating?

If the examiner and co-examiner suspect cheating (e.g. on the basis of plagiarism control), they will submit a report to the head of studies for your programme.

When the head of studies has received the report on the suspected cheating, you will be invited to a meeting where you will go through the report, and you will have the opportunity to explain what you think has happened.

You can bring a friend, a fellow student or a family member to the meeting – what is known as an observer. The observer can help you remember what you want to say and can subsequently help you remember what was said during the meeting.

After the meeting, you will receive a written summary of the meeting on which you are given the opportunity to comment.

If the head of studies believes that you have cheated, they will send a report to the dean of your faculty. If the dean also assesses that you have cheated, the report will be forwarded to the rector with a recommendation from the dean.

UCPH Education will assess the case on behalf of the rector and send the dean’s recommendation to you in your KUmail, so you can make any comments that you may have (hearing of the parties concerned in accordance with the rules of the Danish Public Administration Act). You will be given at least one week to submit your comments, otherwise UCPH Education will decide the case without your comments.

UCPH Education will make a decision in your case on behalf of the rector.

You will receive the decision in your KUmail.
