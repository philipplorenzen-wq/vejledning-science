You can be withdrawn from an exam outside the withdrawal periods if you apply for and are granted a dispensation.

Make sure to apply for a dispensation before the date of the exam. If you apply after the exam, it's much more difficult to get a dispensation.

When applying for a dispensation, you must be able to document that you have not been able to withdraw your registration before the deadline due to exceptional circumstances.


You can apply for a dispensation on the grounds of

  • illness (you or your immediate family)
  • a death in your immediate family
  • other exceptional circumstances largely beyond your control.

Circumstances to do with your student job, housing situation or finances are not regarded as exceptional circumstances.

It's better to apply for late withdrawal

In general, it’s better to apply to withdraw from the exam after the deadline due to exceptional circumstances than having to apply for a dispensation for e.g., an extra exam attempt or to withdraw from a course after the deadline with a used exam attempt at a later time.


How to apply for a dispensation

4 steps when you apply for a dispensation:

  1. Get documentation of your situation, e.g. a medical certificate from your doctor or an opinion from a psychologist.
    See a guide to what your documentation should include
  2. Write a statement, which contains a description of the exceptional circumstances due to which you need a dispensation .
  3. Createa study plan for your entire course of study, which shows how you intend to complete your study programme. Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure.       
  4. Fill out an application by using 'Apply for a dispensation' and enclose your documentation, written statement, and study plan.
