In the ENRE program you have to write at least two project reports; one in the form of a compulsory course, and one for your thesis.

The purpose of the compulsory block 4 course Applied Environmental and Natural Resource Economics. is essentially to give you experience with writing a scientific project, independently or in a group, so that you feel ready to start writing your Master's thesis half a year later. Additionally, this project might be a chance to start considering a topic and developing ideas for your thesis.

The Master's thesis is the final part of the program, where you show that you have become an environmental and natural resource economist. It is also the last chance to specialize further in the direction you want. You must choose your own topic for the thesis and find a suitable supervisor. I recommend that you develop your own idea for a thesis topic, but if you don't know what you want to write about or who you would like to supervise you, you can find inspiration at KU Project and Jobbank . 

Remember that it is your own responsibility to find a thesis topic and supervisor. It might take some time and require some effort, so it is a good idea to start doing this at least a few months before you submit your thesis contract.

Other project options

You also have other options for working with projects. One option is to do a Project In Practice (PIP) with a company/organization on a full-time or part-time basis over one or more blocks. This is essentially an opportunity for you to do an internship – i.e. get some hands-on experience with real-life work as an environmental and natural resource economist – while at the same time obtaining ECTS credits and thus remaining study active. I'll be happy to provide feedback on any internship considerations and questions you might have in this regard.

Another option is to do a Project Outside the Course Scope. This is an option e.g. if you want to work with some specific topic/theme/method that you cannot find in any of the available courses, or e.g. if for some reason you are missing some points and need to catch up by other means than simply following courses.

Many of the restricted elective or elective courses also imply project work.
