A study group is primarily a work group in which you get the opportunity to discuss your academic thoughts and uncertainties with others, and thereby become better at studying. 


Working together with others in a study group leads to: 

  • greater academic learning outcome, because you test your knowledge and receive feedback from others 
  • a more structured daily routine as you commit to your meetings, which also means that you have deadlines for tasks and submissions 
  • key experience and skills in working with others – something you will definitely need in your future workplace 
  • a feeling of belonging on your degree programme, and the chance to get to know some of your fellow students.
