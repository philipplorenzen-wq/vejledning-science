How do I make a personal study plan?

Make a new study plan if you for some reason or other diverge from the recommended course of study or make changes you your existing plan. This could e.g. be because you
  • have courses from a previous block that you still need to pass
  • need to study at a reduced pace e.g. due to illness or mental or physical functional impairment.


9  tips to a good study plan

  1. Use the study planning tool on this page. You open it by clicking the red button under the recommended course of study.
  2. Use the recommended course of study as your starting point. It's already entered in the digital planning tool (see tip number 1). Here you can see the courses you need to take and the recommended order of the different elements. Adjust the plan to your situation. You are allowed to change the order you take things in. Just remember you cannot place your Master's thesis before second year. 
  3. Enter all courses you've taken in your plan – including the ones you did not pass in the first round. 
  4. Check that you don't have courses in the same schedule groups. Courses in the same schedule group will often have teaching and possibly also exams at the same time.

    Find the schedule group of each course in the course description in the course catalogue.

  5. Find out if there are recommended academic qualifications by reading the course descriptions. They will give you a hint as to whether or not you have what it takes to follow the course. It can e.g. be recommended that you have taken another course first. Some courses have formal requirements. These you must live up to in order to take the course. Contact the course coordinator if you have questions about this.
    Find course descriptions and contact info for the course coordinator in the course descriptions.

  6. Consider if your plan is realistic for you with its total amount of teaching and preparation/study time. 

  7. Find out if your plan complies with the study activity requirements, or if it requires that you apply and get an exemption. 
    Read more about the study activity requirements

  8. Familiarise yourself with SU's study activity requirements that are in some ways more strict than the requirements from the university.
    Read more about SU's study activity requirements

  9. Get pointers on how to choose your courses from
    - your study- and career guidance counsellors under Plan your block
    - from the recommendations from your Head of Studies

You are always welcome to get guidance on your study plan from one of your study- and career guidance counsellors in UCPH Study Guidance.
