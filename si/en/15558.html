It is up to your study board to decide whether you can be granted an extra exam attempt. The board's assessment is always based on the extraordinary circumstances cited in your application.


Examples of extraordinary circumstances:

  • Death or illness in your immediate family around the time of the exam
  • Your own illness (which for one reason or another has prevented you from withdrawing from the exam) around the time of the exam
  • A need for special conditions or exam aids that has been realised too late
  • Other special circumstances surrounding your exam


Reasons that do not in themselves entitle you to dispensation but which can be mentioned in your application:

  • The course is especially difficult for you
  • Your student job has taken up too much time
  • Your housing situation or financial circumstances
