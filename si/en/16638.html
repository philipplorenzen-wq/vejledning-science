Who may act as my supervisor?

Your primary supervisor must be employed at either SCIENCE or the Faculty of Health and Medical Sciences (SUND).

Your supervisor must be employed in one of the following job categories:

  • professor
  • associate professor/external lecturer
  • teaching associate professor
  • teaching lecturer 
  • post doc
  • assistant professor
  • senior researcher
  • senior consultant
  • senior adviser 
  • affiliate associate professor
  • affiliate professor


Possibility for co-supervisor with a different background

If you want a co-supervisor for your project, employees with other titles (e.g. PhD students) can usually provide practical and academic support.


Consider choosing the same supervisor as you want for your thesis

It is not a requirement that you choose the same supervisor as for the thesis, but it is important that you consider the possibility of a longer collaboration before choosing a supervisor.

Advantages of choosing the same supervisor you want on your thesis:

  • You can more easily ensure academic continuity between your thesis preparation project and your thesis.
  • Your supervisor will be more on board with your project right from the beginning of your thesis.
  • You will have the opportunity to get to know each other and test out your cooperation before entering into a thesis contract covering more extensive cooperation. 

If you agree with your project supervisor that he will continue as supervisor for your subsequent thesis, your supervisor cannot refuse to be your thesis supervisor, unless this is approved by the head of department due to extraordinary circumstances.

You are under no obligation to write your thesis with your supervisor from the thesis preparation project.
