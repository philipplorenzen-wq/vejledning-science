Can I change classes?

If you have a functional impairment or a long-ter medical condition that prevents you from continuing in your current class, you may apply for an exemption to change classes.

You can only change classes if there are available places in the class you wish to transfer to.

1. How do I apply for a class change?

You must apply for a dispensation to change classes. In your application, you should:

  • Clearly describe the reason(s) why you need the dispensation. Your functional impairment or long-term medical condition must be the reason why you need to change classes.
  • Include all circumstances that are relevant to your application.
  • Specify the class you wish to transfer to, if you have a particular class in mind.

2. Documentation

Remember to attach relevant documentation of your functional impairment or medical condition.

You should provide the best possible documentation to support the reason for your application.

Read more about the documentation requirements under "What documentation should I include with my application?".

Number all attachments and submit them as a single combined document with your application.

3. When should I apply?

You should apply as early as possible. Processing time is up to six weeks, and once teaching has begun, academic or administrative considerations may mean that it is no longer possible to change classes.
