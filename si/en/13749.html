In the event of fire, fire alarm or other incidents that require buildings to be evacuated.

If you are in a teaching session, your lecturer is responsible for evacuating the class and taking you to the nearest assembly point.


If you are on campus, but not in a teaching session, follow the instructions from people in yellow or orange vests.

  • Avoid panic – leave the building calmly.
  • Use the nearest exit and never the use the lifts.

No member of staff present?

If evacuation is necessary in connection with Friday bars or parties where there are no staff members present, it is you or other students that have to ensure that everyone leaves the building.

Evacuation stations have been set up where Friday bars are held. It is a box on the wall like the one you can see to the left. Inside the box are instructions for the evacuation leader and assembly point leader.

The first to reach the evacuation station has the role of evacuation leader. Put on the yellow vest and read the yellow instructions.


The evacuation leader appoints an assembly point leader, who puts on the orange vest. Read the orange instructions.


The assembly point leader goes to the assembly to receive the evacuated.


The evacuation leader ensures that:

  • everybody in the evacuation zone is warned and informs neighbouring zones.
  • toilets, etc. are checked.
  • doors and windows are closed, if possible.
  • avoid fire or inhalation of smoke – do not take any chances


The evacuation leader goes to the assembly point and informs the assembly point leader that everyone is out.


The evacuation and assembly point leaders are responsible for contact with the fire brigade and the police.
