How do I prepare well for the career fair?

When you know what you want to gain from attending the fair, it is much easier to plan your day.

Consider why you are attending the fair

  • Are you looking for something very specific, e.g. a project partner or a job?
  • Do you simply want to browse a bit and check out different workplaces to learn more about various sectors?
  • Do you want to practise how to present yourself and your study programme?
  • Would you like to know more about the competences which employers are looking for?
  • Do you have a project idea which you would like to present to relevant employers?
  • Are there any specific employers you want to talk to, or do you intend to drop by most of the stands?


Set yourself some goals for the day

It may be useful to set yourself one or more goals for the day.
Your goals may, for example, be to

  • present your idea to minimum 3 employers
  • hand out 5 CVs
  • inquire about a student job with a minimum of 5 employers.


Think about what to bring with you

It is a good idea to bring some specific material with you to the career fair which you can distribute to potential employers.
This way you  

  • appear more serious and prepared
  • make it easier for the employer to remember precisely you after a day of talking to many different people.

This you can bring to a career fair

  • Your CV
  • A brief project description if you are looking for a workplace for project collaboration
  • Your updated LinkedIn profile and the app ready to connect to people you meet on the spot.
