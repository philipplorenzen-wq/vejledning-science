I’m Carsten Smith-Hall. As your Head of Studies, I know the academic contents of your programme in detail and have contributed to developing and deciding your curriculum.

My research area is environment-people relationships in general, with a particular focus on the role of environmental products in preventing and reducing poverty in low- and middle-income countries. Typically, I teach courses such as Global Forests and People, and Preparing Global Forestry Fieldwork.

As a student, you have many options and can decide on many courses. The choices within the curriculum are yours to make.

Here are my advice and recommendations for completing the first year of your master’s programme in Global Environment and Development successfully. Read my advice as inspiration for your choices - not as rules to follow.

To provide you with individual advice, I will invite you to a one-to-one meeting with me at the start of your studies.
