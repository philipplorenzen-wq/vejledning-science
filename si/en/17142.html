To develop your career management skills and strengthen your employability throughout your studies, your study and career guidance counsellors urge you to 

  • focus on building a professional network in Denmark
  • participate in activities that provide you with practical experience with Danish work life.

4 things that can improve your employability:

  1. Get a student job while you study. Any job will give you an idea of how work life is in Denmark, and it will also build your network. Read more about student jobs.
  2. Do a project in collaboration with a company. In this way, you apply your academic skills to a work setting and build your Danish network.
    Read more about thesis and project collaboration.
  3. Learn how to grow your network. As an international student it's particularly relevant to connect with people that can help you on your way and that you can learn from. 
    On top of that Danish employers use their network a lot in their recruitment.
    Read more about how to create a network.
  4. Learn how you search and apply for jobs or project collaborations in Denmark. How to search and apply for jobs is different from one country to another.
    Read more about how to apply for jobs and projects in a Danish context.
