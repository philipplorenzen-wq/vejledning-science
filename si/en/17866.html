Can I do a Master’s thesis in collaboration with a company?

You can do a Master's thesis that involves a company to a greater or lesser extent. 

Two different types of collaboration with companies:

  • You work closely with a company. You develop the topic for your Master's thesis together with the company (and your supervisor), and you work on your Master's thesis in the company.
  • You collect empirical data from a company which you use for your own Master's thesis, and work with it at the University.

Find a company

Be ready to use at least four months on finding a company. You will often have to ask several companies before one of them agrees to work with you.

How to find inspiration to find the right company:

  • Participate in career events, where you can meet private/public companies and organizations that are looking for collaborations with students. 

  • Consider companies that you have heard and read about, and which have
    aroused your interest.
  • Read abstracts from projects in practice at SCIENCE. 
    Find abstracts
  • Search in UCPH Projects & Jobs Here, you can find relevant project posts – or job ads you can apply for or use for inspiration.
    Visit UCPH Projects & Jobs
  • Ask your supervisor or a lecturer whether he or she knows about or has contacts with any interesting companies.

You are allowed to receive reimbursement from the company

You are not allowed to receive a salary from the company for the work you do as part of your project.

You are allowed to receive partial or full reimbursement for expenses you have during your project. For example, this could be travel expenses or rent if you have to work far from your home.

You must be able to document that the expenses are connected directly to your stay in the company.
