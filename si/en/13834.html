The inclusion survey's main report about students and employees reveals that while most respondents feel welcome and supported at UCPH, significant inclusion challenges also exist. 38 percent of all respondents identify as being a part of one or more minorities and one in three of these respondents also reports feeling excluded.
Groups facing the most pronounced barriers include individuals with neurodivergence, mental or physical functional variations, ethnic and religious minorities, international employees and students, and those with a social or political background different from the majority's. Key issues include exclusionary social behaviour, lack of belonging, unequal access to career and study opportunities, and physical and linguistic accessibility challenges. Many respondents report feeling pressure to mask aspects of their identity to fit in, and some consider leaving UCPH due to these experiences.


There is broad support for inclusion efforts and a clear desire for change across both employee and student groups. The report's findings offer UCPH a valuable opportunity to strengthen its culture of belonging through targeted initiatives such as inclusive teaching and leadership practices, improved support systems, and more accessible communication.
