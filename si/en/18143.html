Many students experience feeling lonely for short or long periods while at university. What brings on a feeling of loneliness, how it manifests itself, and how long it lasts can vary greatly from person to person.

You may feel, for example, that you:

  • do not fit in and feel different from your fellow students
  • have difficulty taking part in social events
  • do not have family or friends to draw on because you are new to Copenhagen or to UCPH
  • are having a particularly difficult time of it due to your specific life circumstances: Your background, mental or physical illness or disability, difficult family relationships or grief, loss, etc.

Because the reasons for feeling lonely can vary so much, how to combat loneliness is also very individual.

4 pieces of advice 

#1: Remember that it is a common feeling – and that you can do something about it
For many people, loneliness is something of a taboo subject that brings on feelings of shame – even though it is very common and quite natural. Feelings also tend to grow when we try to suppress them. So having found your way to this site, you are already taking an important first step: acknowledging and facing your loneliness and thereby making it possible to actually do something about it.
#2: Seek out a community with a clearly defined activity and a shared purpose
The feeling of loneliness often makes it especially difficult to seek out new communities and maintain existing ones. Make it a little easier for yourself by seeking out communities organised around, for example, a common interest, hobby or cause. Similarly, it can help if it is a clearly defined activity, so you can just show up and not have to take the initiative every time.
Read about different types of communities in student life
#3: Give yourself space to practice and allow yourself to make mistakes
You can easily feel awkward and wrong if you are a little out of practice talking to others and meeting new people. So remind yourself that you are just practising, and that it's okay to make mistakes – we all do that from time to time.

Learn more about how we are pressured by our ideas of what everyone else does
#4: Talk to someone about it – most people recognize how you feel
If you have people, for example, in your study group, at work, or in your family that you can talk to, it can be helpful and a relief to share your feeling of loneliness with others.
You can also start by talking to, for example, a study and career guidance counsellor. You will probably find that most people will recognize your feelings and what you’re going through – no matter how large a network they have.

These 4 tips are inspired by advice that Psykiatrifonden (the Psychiatry Foundation) gives on loneliness.

Seek support and guidance

You can always contact your study and career guidance counsellor for guidance on dealing with loneliness in your everyday life as a student. There are also others to turn to at and outside UCPH
