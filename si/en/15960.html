Stress is a biological reaction – an acute response that is activated when we need to perform better and faster. You need this, for example, when you are: 

  • sitting an exam 
  • giving an important presentation 
  • working hard putting the final touches on an assignment. 

In such temporary situations, stress symptoms such as anxiety, sweating and shaking are natural signs that your body and brain are ready to perform at their best, and that your system is working as it should. 

Serious and unhealthy stress can arise if your system is strained too much or for too long – without having a chance for relaxation and restitution in between. People are not meant to be constantly ‘on full alert’.
