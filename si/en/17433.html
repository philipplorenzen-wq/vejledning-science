Graduates from this program are employed in a wide range of jobs and many of you will work in contexts you did not foresee. Your combination of natural science competencies, analytical and professional skills and your experience with practical problems make you attractive in many situations.

Some parts of the job market in Denmark, e.g. the extension service and public institutions, are relevant mainly for Danish speakers and writers. However, many companies have English as their work language and there will be good chances of employment for non-Danish speakers.

Are you going for a PhD you must realize that it is a very competitive arena. Follow the UCPH Projects & Jobs website and job portals for other universities in Denmark and the rest of Europe, and make sure your teachers and your thesis supervisor know you are looking for an opportunity.

If you cannot see a crystal clear career path ahead do not let this stress you. It simply means that you are open to various opportunities, which may show up. Keep doors open and do not let practical limitations like leaving Copenhagen decide.

Study relevant student jobs outside (e.g. ministries and organizations) and inside the university (e.g. lab assistant) can be found, but are more easy to find for Danish speakers. There is no central job portal in Denmark, so make sure your teachers and fellow students know you are looking for a job.
