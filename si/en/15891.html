How many and which ECTS credits can I use on my project?

You may do a project outside the course scope for a total of 15 ECTS credits during your course of study.
Your project outside the course scope can have 2 sizes:

  • 7.5 ECTS-credits (workload: 206 hours)
  • 15 ECTS-credits (workload: 412 hours).
Check if there is a limit on how many ECTS credits you can use in total for different projects

How your project can count as a part of your programme

During your study, a project outside the course scope can count as:

  • restricted elective ECTS-credits: 15
  • elective ECTS-credits: 15.

If you are planning a project of 15 ECTS-credits you can divide it between both types of electives, so that it may look like this:

  • 7.5 elective ECTS-credits
  • 7.5 restricted elective ECTS-credits.

If you have some ECTS credits left that do not fit a course or project

You may complete a project outside the course scope with an uneven number of ECTS credits when you are at the end of your programme and are missing less than 7.5 ECTS in order to finish it.
Before you fill in your contract, you must check that you:
  1. are missing an uneven number of ECTS credits – this is the case when the number of ECTS credits you have passed does not divide evenly by 7.5.
  2. are at the final stage of your programme – this is the case when you only need to complete:
  • compulsory courses or projects
  • elective or restricted elective elements that you are registered or bound to. Please note that a pre-approval for elements outside UCPH does not count as a registration.
In some cases, you may still complete a project outside the course scope of up to 12.5 ECTS if you use ECTS credits from your elective or restricted elective elements. See the table below to determine which size of a project outside the course scope you can complete.
