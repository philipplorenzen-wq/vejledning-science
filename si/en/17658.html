The experience of pressure is often fed by your ideas about what everyone else is doing and is capable of. It is natural for us to compare ourselves with others. 


The problem when you do this is: 

  • It is easy to generalise and develop a distorted view of what everyone else can do and is doing all the time – especially when you feel under pressure. 
  • Such comparisons rarely take account of individual conditions and circumstances. 

Share your perceptions with your fellow students

Share your thoughts with some of your fellow students – e.g. with a small group or one person that you feel you can trust. 

By being honest and trusting, you create a space where others dare to do the same. 

You can start the conversation by asking something like: 

  • “How do you find time for your studies, voluntary work and a student job?” 
  • “Did you also find that theory difficult to understand?” 
  • “Do you all do all the set reading for all classes?” 
  • “Didn’t you manage to do all the preparation for today either?”
