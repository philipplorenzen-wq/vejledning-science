Where to find help:

  • Consult your project supervisor about your challenges and your possibilities for continuing your group project.
  • Contact a study and career guidance counsellor in UCPH Study Guidance for advice on how to handle your disagreement in the group.

If you cannot resolve your challenges and continue your group project, your options will depend on how far you have come with the project. 

Cooperation difficulties – at an early stage in the project 

If your supervisor accepts to supervise you individually, you can split up the group and continue to work on different projects. You can divide up into smaller groups or continue the work on your own. 

This requires that you can create completely separate projects within the problem formulation of the contract. 

Notify your department that you are splitting up the group, so that you can submit different projects in Digital Exam. 

Cooperation difficulties – at a late stage in the project 

If you have written so much of the project together that you are unable to divide it up and create completely separate projects within the problem formulation of the contract, you can start from scratch on a new project. 

You will have used an exam attempt if you do not submit the group project at the deadline.

You can each start over by following the same procedure as for the first project:

  • Find a new subject and possibly a new company.
  • Find a new supervisor, if necessary.
  • Complete and submit a new project contract.
