Practising communication of what you have learned makes you

  • better at putting your knowledge into words
  • feel safer in the exam situation


Oral exams

It's a good idea to practise for an oral exam, because it often makes you feel more on top of the situation and less nervous during the exam.

3 tips before an oral exam:


1. Say out loud what you have read. Saying it out loud can help you formulate and understand the subject better.
2. Practise a presentation of the various topics in your syllabus and get feedback from others, e.g. your study group.
3. Prepare for being asked and answering questions. You can either write down questions for yourself – and answer them – or get others to ask them.


Written exams

For written exams you need to be knowledgeable about the subject, and have a good overview of what you need to do and how long you have to do it.


3 tips before a written exam:


1. Say out loud what you have read. Saying it out loud can help you formulate and understand the subject better.
2. Complete an exam assignment from a previous exam – read the whole set through. If you get stuck, go to the assignments which you can do.
3. Divide your notes into the different topics – if you are allowed to bring notes to the exam. Check the course description to see what exam aids are allowed
