Apply for dispensation for an extra exam attempt as soon as you find out you need it.


4 steps when you apply for a dispensation:

  1. Get documentation of your situation, e.g. maternity records/birth certificate/adoption documents, a medical certificate from your doctor or an opinion from a psychologist. In some cases, documentation can also be statements from your lecturer, supervisor, head of studies, etc. 
  2. Write a statement including
    • a description of the exceptional circumstances that have prevented you from passing the course
    • if relevant, a statement explaining how your situation has changed.
  3. Create a study plan for your entire course of study, which shows how you intend to complete your study programme. Find a study plan template in which the structure of your study programme has already been entered under Programme structure.
  4. Fill out an application by using 'Apply for a dispensation' and enclose documentation, written statement, and study plan.

Apply for a dispensation

You can forward your documentation later if it takes too long to get. However, the processing of your application may be delayed if you do not submit all the documentation together at the same time. Write in the comments field if the documentation will be submitted later.


Reply to your application

As a general rule, you will receive an answer within 6 weeks from when the Study Board has received all relevant information about your application. If you need to send supplementary material, you will be contacted via your KUmail.   

If you receive an email alerting you that you will be disenrolled from your study programme within 14 days while your application is being processed, you will not be disenrolled until you have received a reply to your application.
