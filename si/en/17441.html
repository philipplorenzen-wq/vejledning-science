Read more about studying abroad during your master programme in Chemistry here.

We recommend you study abroad in the first year of the programme, but you should be aware of the fact, that you have to find a course that can replace your compulsory course(s). You can hear more about your possibilities to study abroad at:

  • SPOT ON events
  • Study abroad events at the Department of Chemistry

My recommendations:

  • For all European exchange places for the Master Programme in Chemistry, you will find the courses that can replace your compulsory course(s) in block 1+2 or 3+4. If you apply to ETH, you also have the option to apply for a grant from Novo Nordisk International Talents Program (NNITP).
  • For the Nordic universities, you will find a master's degree programme in Chemistry at the following universities: University of Helsinki, Norwegian University of Science and Technology, University of Oslo, University of Stavanger, Linköping University, Linnaeus University, Lund University Stockholm University, Umeå University, University of Gothenburg, Uppsala University
  • For overseas universities, you need to check if you can take master's courses (graduate courses). You may want to have an alternative study plan for your study abroad.

Students on the Master Programme in Chemistry have stayed abroad at:

  • Eidgenossische Technische Hochschule Zürich (ETH Zürich), Schweiz
  • University of Amsterdam
  • University of Helsinki
  • Stockholm University
  • National University of Singapore, Singapore
  • University of Western Ontario, Canada
  • University of British Columbia Vancouver, Canada
  • Boston University, USA
  • University of California, Berkeley, USA
