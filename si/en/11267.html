My name is Nikoline Juul Nielsen and I am your Head of Studies. I was trained as an environmental chemist with a focus on analytical chemistry during my university studies and have a PhD in natural products research with a focus on analytical and data processing aspects. Daily, I am an associate professor in the Analytical Chemistry Research Group , embedded in the Section of Environmental Chemistry and –Physics at the Department of Plant and Environmental Sciences. You will meet me at the introduction days in August, and maybe as a lecturer if you follow the courses Advanced Analytical Chemistry– Chromatography and Mass Spectrometry or Advanced Analytical Chemistry – Arctic Pollution Research, or as a supervisor on projects. Otherwise, you can find me in room T632, 6th floor, Thorvaldsensvej 40 at Frederiksberg Campus.

My research area is analytical chemistry applied to biological samples (environmental, but also agricultural produce, food, or human samples) with emphasis on liquid chromatography and mass spectrometry as measuring techniques. In the Analytical Chemistry Research Group, we strive to obtain the ultimate chemical fingerprint using many different and combined analytical techniques. We believe that the chemical fingerprinting approach will change the way we do environmental monitoring in the future: from being focused on a few priority pollutants which are identified and quantified (target analysis) to a more holistic approach where 'everything' in the sample is measured, and then afterwards we assess what compounds should be prioritized for identification, quantification, or monitoring (non-target analysis). I am also interested in advancing fundamental analytical chemistry, e.g. we work with multidimensional chromatography as a means of augmenting how many compounds we can measure at the same time or applied advanced signal processing for extracting as much information from complex analytical chemistry datasets as possible. 

Environmental science has experienced a fast development over the past 25 years. Today, the discipline manifests itself as an important contributor to the development of sustainable production and environmental solutions, and to build rigid monitoring and risk assessment platforms for the assessment of chemicals and other potential contaminants.
